TPM (Trajectory-Pair Mass-Transport) maintains a per-(episode-position t, action a) signed per-channel 1-D Wasserstein vector M[t,a,:] computed as median-differences between terminal-cumulant subpopulations: episodes that took action a at step t vs. those that did not (both of length > t). The improvement operator fires a logit nudge at each decision step by the Pareto-non-dominance margin — count of actions dominated by the current action minus count dominating it — in the coordinate-wise partial order on R^k. No scalar collapse, no critic, no observation hash, no hash-collision precondition; every episode of length >= t contributes to M[t,a,:].
