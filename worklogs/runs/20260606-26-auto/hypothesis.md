# 20260606-26-auto — ACCD (Action-Conditional Channel Dissociation)

## Research Gate

primitive: per-(observation, action, channel) signed marginal-probability shift `S_m[o_t, a] := P_a(v_m^{t+H}=1 | o_t) − P_full(v_m^{t+H}=1 | o_t)`, where `P_a` and `P_full` are two small offline-supervised classifiers predicting whether channel `m` will fire at any step within an `H`-step horizon, the former conditioned on the next action, the latter not.
improvement_operator: Pareto-non-dominance logit nudge over the k-dim row vector `S[o_t, a, :]` — coordinate-wise partial-order vote on per-channel signed shifts; no scalar collapse, no critic, no Bellman, no reward weight.
side_information: vector diagnostics (per-step channel-firing labels from `info["vector"]`), learned dynamics (the two predictive classifiers).
nearest_prior_or_disqualifier: cwai-channel-wise-action-influence (Jacobian-norm primitive on a forward model).
falsifier: if on RG (the highly stochastic vector env where CWAI collapsed) ACCD also scores below random, the "probability-shift survives stochasticity better than gradient-norm survives stochasticity" claim is wrong and the family is dead. Conversely, if on RG ACCD scores at-or-above strong (random ≈ 1.331), the claim is supported and the family graduates. DST is a sanity gate: must beat random.

## Mechanism

ACCD trains two small offline classifiers on the replay buffer: an action-marginal head `ψ_full(o_t) ∈ [0,1]^k` predicting `P(v_m^{t+1:t+H}=1)` for each channel `m`, and an action-conditional head `ψ_a(o_t)` per action (or a single shared head with a one-hot action input) predicting the same target conditioned on the immediate next action. Both heads are trained by binary cross-entropy on the actual (o_t, a_t, v_{t+1:t+H}) records in the buffer, with no reward weighting. At each decision step, the primitive is the matrix `S[o_t, a, m] = ψ_a(o_t)[m] − ψ_full(o_t)[m]` — a signed per-channel marginal probability shift expressing how much the choice of action `a` shifts the predicted firing probability of channel `m` over the next `H` steps. The improvement operator computes, for each action `a`, the Pareto-non-dominance count `n_a^{dom}` (number of other actions whose row-vector `S[o_t, a', :]` is coordinate-wise dominated by `S[o_t, a, :]` in the partial order on R^k) minus `n_a^{sub}` (number of actions that dominate `a`), and applies a logit nudge `Δlogit_a = α · (n_a^{dom} − n_a^{sub}) / |A|`. Channels whose `|S_m|` magnitude across all actions is below an adaptive noise-floor threshold are excluded from the Pareto comparison for that decision step (k_eff gating, prevents Pareto saturation when most channels are uninformative).

## Required candidate shape

1. **Experience object:** Standard transitions `(o_t, a_t, v_{t+1}, o_{t+1})` collected from on-policy rollouts, including the per-step vector signal `info["vector"] ∈ {0,1}^k` (or thresholded continuous signals); the buffer stores the full trajectory so the H-step horizon channel-firing label can be computed offline. No counterfactual rollouts, no simulator branches, no verifier calls.
2. **Core primitive:** The per-(observation, action) signed marginal-shift vector `S[o_t, a, :] ∈ R^k`, where `S_m = ψ_a(o_t)[m] − ψ_full(o_t)[m]` and the two ψ heads are supervised on observed `H`-step channel-firing indicators. This is a probabilistic primitive (not a gradient norm, not a return, not a value).
3. **Improvement operator:** Logit nudge `Δlogit_a = α·(n_a^{dom} − n_a^{sub})/|A|` where `n_a^{dom}, n_a^{sub}` are Pareto-non-dominance counts on the row vectors `S[o_t, ·, :]` restricted to channels whose `max_a S_m − min_a S_m` exceeds a noise floor `ε` for that observation. The base policy is updated by BC toward the shifted-logit target with a cross-entropy loss; ψ heads are updated by binary cross-entropy on the actual observed labels.
4. **Execution rule:** Sample actions from the base softmax policy `π_θ(a|o_t)` during data collection (no on-decision Pareto computation injected into rollouts — the nudge is applied only during the policy-improvement gradient step on buffer samples). At evaluation, sample greedily or by temperature.
5. **Vector feedback rule:** The k-vector of channel-firing labels enters via the BCE supervised signal of the two ψ classifiers and via the k-dimensional partial-order vote in the improvement operator. There is never a fixed or learned scalarization `wᵀv`. On envs with no vector feedback (DoorKey, KeyCorridor), the operator falls back to a single-channel binary "reward-fired-this-window" indicator and the Pareto comparison degenerates to a scalar comparison on that env (acknowledged scalarization-equivalence in the k=1 degenerate case; the family's anti-scalarization claim is on the multi-channel envs DST/RG/Craftax).
6. **Rollout-cost discipline:** One environment step per buffer transition. Two classifier forward+backward passes per buffer sample per update (ψ_full, ψ_a) — these are auxiliary training, not extra environment interactions. No counterfactual rollouts, no simulator branches, no verifier calls. At deployment time, two classifier forward passes per decision step (action-marginal once, action-conditional once over all `|A|` actions or via a single shared head with action input). Total: 1 env step + O(|A|·k) classifier evals per decision; environment-cost-equivalent to PPO/A2C with an auxiliary forward-model head.
7. **Nearest-neighbor novelty audit:** Closest prior is **cwai-channel-wise-action-influence** (alive-promising candidate). CWAI's primitive is `‖∂f_θ(o,a)[m] / ∂e(a)‖_2` — a gradient column-norm on a deterministic-mean forward model. Under stochastic transitions the forward model's predictive output saturates (Jacobian shrinks toward noise floor), which is exactly what killed CWAI on RG (0.011 vs random 1.331). ACCD's primitive is a **probability shift** between two predictive distributions `P_a − P_full`, not a gradient norm; under stochasticity this shift shrinks proportionally but its **sign per channel** survives (the Pareto vote is sign-based, not magnitude-based, so a smaller positive S_m still produces the same dominance vote as a large positive S_m).
8. **Predicted failure modes:**
   (a) On envs with **all-saturated channels** (e.g. step-penalty fires every step in DST/RG), `P_a ≈ P_full ≈ 1` so `S_step ≈ 0` and that channel contributes no signal — predicted effect: step-penalty is silent, not misleading; the Pareto vote falls back to other channels.
   (b) **Cold-start on terminal-only reward channels:** before any rewarded trajectory enters the buffer, `P_a(reward) ≈ P_full(reward) ≈ 0` so `S_reward ≈ 0` and reward channels do not contribute. This is the FED-family bootstrap wall **on the reward channel only**; on DoorKey/KeyCorridor, intermediate channel events (key pickup, door open) — if exposed via env instrumentation — would contribute earlier. On DST/RG with terminal-only reward channels, ACCD's signal during the bootstrap window depends entirely on the step-penalty channel and any non-terminal channels (e.g. RG resource pickups, if those are per-step) firing differently across actions. If both DST and RG have only step-penalty + terminal-only channels, ACCD inherits the FED bootstrap wall.
   (c) **Classifier underfit:** if ψ_a and ψ_full are too low-capacity or not trained enough, both predict near-uniform values and S ≈ 0 across the board; predicted effect is silent operator (random behavior), not wrong direction.
   (d) **Pareto saturation in high-k:** with many channels most actions become non-dominated; the k_eff gate (excluding channels with small spread) is the mitigation; if it fails to engage, vote diffuses to symmetric.
   (e) **Spurious early correlations:** in the first few episodes the classifier may pick up coincidental action-channel correlations that mislead the operator; predicted effect is initial wrong direction that washes out as more data arrives.
9. **Side-information channel:** {vector diagnostics, learned dynamics}. Vector diagnostics enter via per-step channel-firing labels from `info["vector"]`; learned dynamics enter via the two H-step predictive classifiers ψ_full and ψ_a. No environment instrumentation beyond the vector env's `info["vector"]` field; no language description; no demonstrations; no pretrained priors; no verifier; no reachability/reset structure.
10. **Monotonic improvement claim:** Under the assumption that `ψ_full` and `ψ_a` converge to the true conditional probabilities `P(v_m^{t+H}=1 | o_t)` and `P(v_m^{t+H}=1 | o_t, a)` respectively, the per-channel signed shift `S_m[o_t, a]` is exactly the marginal causal effect of action `a` on channel `m`'s H-step firing probability (under the on-policy distribution). The Pareto-non-dominance vote is monotone-improving in the following sense: if action `a` Pareto-dominates action `a'` on the channel-shift partial order at state `o_t`, then under the convex-cone preference `(a ≻ a' ⟺ S[o_t, a] − S[o_t, a'] ∈ R^k_{≥0} \ {0})` the policy update `π_{new}(a|o_t) > π_old(a|o_t), π_{new}(a'|o_t) < π_old(a'|o_t)` strictly improves the Pareto-frontier of expected per-channel H-step firing rates of the on-policy distribution. The claim is monotonic in the **vector-valued expected channel-firing-rate Pareto frontier**, not in any scalar return; it does not assume reward-bearing trajectories exist.

## Why it is not cwai-channel-wise-action-influence

CWAI's primitive is the gradient column-norm `G[a, m] = ‖∂f_θ(o, a)[m]/∂e(a)‖_2`, computed at decision time from a deterministic-mean forward model. Under stochastic transitions f_θ's predictive mean saturates and ‖·‖_2 collapses toward the noise floor (CWAI's RG result: 0.011 vs random 1.331). ACCD's primitive is the signed difference of two **probability outputs** from two distinct classifiers `S_m = ψ_a − ψ_full`, never a gradient norm; under stochasticity each probability becomes less extreme, but the **sign of their difference** (which is what the Pareto vote consumes) is invariant under proportional shrinkage. The structural distinction is: CWAI couples to the *local linearization* of a deterministic predictor, ACCD couples to the *categorical posterior shift* of two distinct predictors — categorically different objects under variable renaming.

## Why it scales beyond the substrate

In a 20k-action LLM-with-tools setting, the per-channel signal can be each of: validity (program parsed), correctness (verifier check), safety (no flagged content), latency (under threshold), cost (under budget), preference (user thumbs-up). A single binary classifier head per channel, conditioned on the candidate next-action embedding, predicts the H-step firing probability of that channel; the action-marginal head predicts the same without conditioning on action. ACCD's improvement operator selects among candidate actions ("generate this paragraph", "call this tool with these arguments") by Pareto-non-dominance over the k-dim probability-shift vector — the same operation as on DST, just with continuous action embeddings instead of discrete logits. The primitive does not require trajectory matching, observation-hash collision, prefix-trie indexing, or any per-(state, action) tabular cell — every replay sample contributes to the classifier training, so the bootstrap-wall and revisitation-bottleneck failure modes that killed FED/CEC/TPP/PCR/TRAC/CSD/ARP/PFA do not apply at scale. The operator is silent (not wrong) on channels that haven't yet fired in the buffer, gracefully degrading to base-policy exploration; once any channel fires the classifier picks it up. The vector-feedback rule is preserved at every scale: there is no fixed scalar weighting of (validity, correctness, safety, latency, cost, preference); the Pareto-non-dominance vote is independent of channel scaling and units, which matters when the channels have wildly different magnitudes (binary correctness vs. millisecond latency vs. dollar cost).
