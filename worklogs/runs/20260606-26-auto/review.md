## Reasoning

ACCD's central primitive — the signed per-channel marginal-probability shift `S_m[o_t, a] = ψ_a(o_t)[m] − ψ_full(o_t)[m]` between two separately supervised classifiers — is structurally distinct from every disqualifier family and every prior attempt in `prior_attempts.md`. It is not a gradient norm (CWAI), not a value or advantage, not a scalar-weighted log-prob update, not a return distribution, and not a cluster-indexed empirical Pareto front. The improvement operator (Pareto-non-dominance count over the k-dim shift row, converted to a logit nudge) consumes only the *sign* of each channel's shift, which the hypothesis argues survives proportional attenuation under stochastic transitions where CWAI's Jacobian norm collapses. This "posterior-shift vs. local-linearization" structural distinction from CWAI is articulated precisely and holds under variable renaming. All required candidate-shape slots are filled with substantive answers: the experience object, the primitive definition, the improvement operator (including k_eff gating for Pareto saturation), the execution rule, the vector-feedback rule (no fixed or learned scalarization), rollout-cost discipline, nearest-neighbor novelty audit, five predicted failure modes, the side-information channel declaration, and a monotonic improvement claim over the vector-valued Pareto frontier of expected per-channel firing rates.

The bootstrap-wall analysis in failure mode (b) is honest and important: on envs with only step-penalty (saturated, P ≈ 1 → S ≈ 0) and terminal-only reward channels (P ≈ 0 → S ≈ 0 before first reward), both channels contribute near-zero signal during the cold-start window, and the operator degrades to base-policy exploration rather than wrong-direction harm. The family is therefore not disqualified by the bootstrap wall — it inherits it partially but degrades gracefully rather than catastrophically, which is the structural property that differentiates it from the FED/CEC/ARP/PCR cluster. Whether that graceful degradation plus non-zero signal on RG resource pickups is sufficient to score above random within 120 s is the open empirical question the falsifier test is designed to answer.

## Risks the Engineer should be aware of

- On Deep Sea Treasure and Resource Gathering, if the step-penalty channel saturates (`P_a ≈ P_full ≈ 1` so `S_step ≈ 0`) and the treasure/gold channels have `P ≈ 0` before the first rewarded trajectory, the k_eff gate may exclude *all* channels for most of the run, leaving the policy in base-exploration mode. Check whether RG's resource-pickup sub-channels fire per-step (not terminal-only) — if they do, they provide non-trivial `S` signal before any terminal reward; if they don't, ACCD inherits the full FED bootstrap wall on that env.
- The two-classifier architecture (one action-marginal head, one action-conditional head per action or shared with action-one-hot) doubles auxiliary training cost relative to a single forward model. If the shared-trunk variant is used, verify that action conditioning is truly separable from the marginal trunk — a leaky shared trunk could make `ψ_a ≈ ψ_full` for all actions (analogous to the PCGA trunk-collapse failure in attempt #27), collapsing `S ≈ 0`.
- The monotonic improvement claim holds only under ψ convergence. In the early training regime where the classifiers are underfit (failure mode (c)), the operator is silent rather than monotone. The Engineer should instrument how often k_eff > 0 during training as a health diagnostic.
