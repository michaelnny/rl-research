# 20260606-25-auto — SDS: Stochastic-Dominance Sketches over Action-Conditional Continuation Distributions

## Research Gate

primitive: per-(state-cluster, action, channel) compressed sketch (T-digest / sorted reservoir of fixed size B) of empirical continuation-cumulant distribution `F_{s,a,m}(x) := Pr[ c_m(τ_T) − c_m(τ_t) ≤ x | cluster(o_t)=s, a_t=a ]`
improvement_operator: at decision time, logit nudge `α · (D⁺(a) − D⁻(a))` where `D⁺(a)` counts actions that `a` strictly first-order stochastic-dominates on **every** channel simultaneously (multi-channel-SD), and `D⁻(a)` counts the reverse — never mean-Pareto, never scalarized
side_information: vector diagnostics, transition geometry
nearest_prior_or_disqualifier: candidate vcc-vector-cumulant-confluence (closest); disqualifier-family Pareto-mean comparison (FED #15, CHX #17) and distributional RL (renamed return distribution)
falsifier: scores below random on at least one vector env (DST or RG) within 120 s, OR: diagnostic logging shows that for ≥ 80% of decision steps the multi-channel SD set is empty (no action SD-dominates any other on **all** channels), reducing the operator to no-op.

## Mechanism

For each cell `(s, a)` indexed by online k-means cluster `s` of an observation embedding plus action `a`, maintain `k` compressed quantile sketches `Q_{s,a,m}` of size `B` (e.g. B=64) — one per vector-feedback channel `m`. Each sketch stores quantile estimates of the empirical distribution of "cumulant change from step `t` to episode end on channel `m`, given `(cluster(o_t)=s, a_t=a)`." Sketches are written incrementally: when an episode ends with terminal cumulant `c_T`, every step `(o_t, a_t)` contributes the delta `c_T − c_t` to its cell's k sketches. At decision step, for each candidate action `a` at cluster `s`, define the per-channel one-sided strict first-order stochastic-dominance relation `a ≻_m a'` iff the empirical CDFs satisfy `F_{s,a,m}(x) ≤ F_{s,a',m}(x)` for all `x` and the inequality is strict on at least one quantile (a max-minus-min comparison over the B quantile estimates suffices). The primitive's improvement operator nudges logits by `D⁺(a) − D⁻(a)` where the counts are over action pairs satisfying the **conjunction across all k channels** — stochastic Pareto dominance on distributions, not on means. Counts are emitted only when both cells have ≥ N_min samples (default N_min = 8) and at least 2 channels are non-degenerate (sketch range > ε); otherwise the operator is silent for that decision step. Channel-orientation: each channel is normalized at first contact so "more is better" — for cost/penalty channels we negate the sketch values up front, so SD on every channel always means "stochastically more favorable."

## Required candidate shape

1. **Experience object:** trajectories with per-step vector feedback `v_t ∈ ℝ^k` and a final terminal cumulant `c_T = Σ_t v_t`. From each step we extract `(cluster(o_t), a_t, c_T − c_t)` — a 3-tuple of (cluster-id, action, k-vector continuation-cumulant). Clusters come from online mini-batch k-means on a small observation-embedding head trained on next-observation prediction (self-supervised, no reward).
2. **Core primitive:** the k-tuple of compressed quantile sketches `Q_{s,a,*} = (Q_{s,a,1}, …, Q_{s,a,k})` per cell `(s, a)`. These are not means, not Pareto fronts, not return distributions in the disqualifier sense — they are per-channel marginal CDFs of continuation deltas, used only via the partial order of first-order stochastic dominance (no convolution, no Bellman backup of a return distribution).
3. **Improvement operator:** at decision time at observation `o_t` mapped to cluster `s`, compute the multi-channel-SD action-tournament: edge `a → a'` exists iff for **every** channel `m ∈ {1..k}`, `Q_{s,a,m}` strictly first-order stochastically dominates `Q_{s,a',m}` (test via max over the B quantile gaps). Apply additive logit `α · (out_deg(a) − in_deg(a))` to the live softmax policy `π_θ`. Sketch updates: standard reservoir/T-digest insertion at episode boundaries. Optimizer: any (Adam) standard gradient on a periodic auxiliary loss that pulls `π_θ`'s logits toward `π_θ + α · nudge(o_t)` via a small KL surrogate; behavior at collection time is `softmax(logits_θ(o_t) + α · nudge(o_t))`.
4. **Execution rule:** at every step, sample action from the nudged softmax `softmax(logits_θ(o_t) + α · nudge(o_t))`. The nudge is computed only when at least one action pair has an SD-dominance edge; otherwise the policy is exactly `π_θ`. No best-of-N, no rollout simulation, no verifier.
5. **Vector feedback rule:** each channel is treated as an independent CDF; the cross-channel aggregation is the **logical conjunction** of per-channel SD-edges (action `a` dominates `a'` only if it dominates on all channels). This is strictly stricter than Pareto-mean and is provably not recoverable by any fixed scalarization `wᵀr`: a fixed-w scalarization induces a total order on means but cannot encode "dominates the entire CDF on every dimension."
6. **Rollout-cost discipline:** zero counterfactual rollouts. Every decision-time call uses only the on-line sketch state. One sketch update per step at episode end (writing `k` quantile inserts to one cell). Per accepted improvement: one gradient step from the nudged-policy KL surrogate on the current minibatch of decision steps. At deployment: one cell-lookup and one tournament computation per decision (O(|A|² · k · B) per step, |A|≤7 in our substrate).
7. **Nearest-neighbor novelty audit:** Closest alive candidate is **VCC** (Vector-Cumulant-Confluence): VCC indexes by *cumulant bucket* and uses Pareto-fronts of mean continuations; SDS indexes by *state-cluster* and uses stochastic-dominance over distributions. The structural distinction is dimensionality of the comparison object (front in `R^k` vs CDF in `R` per channel) and the comparison law (point-Pareto vs distributional SD); these are not interchangeable under variable renaming. Closest disqualifier-family is **distributional RL with renamed return distribution** — distinguished because (a) we never bootstrap or backup the distribution Bellman-style; the sketch is empirical Monte-Carlo and never used as a TD target; (b) we never compute a scalar from the distribution (no mean, no quantile-of-return as policy criterion); (c) we never optimize a distributional loss as the primary signal — the primitive is the SD partial order between distributions, not the distributions themselves.
8. **Predicted failure modes:**
   - When fewer than 2 channels have non-degenerate sketches at a given cell (e.g. RG before any gold/gem is collected, only step-penalty fires), the multi-channel-SD conjunction degenerates to single-channel comparison — which IS scalar maximization on that channel. The N_min=8 + non-degenerate-≥2-channels gate should prevent the nudge from firing in this regime, but if it fails to gate, the operator is a step-penalty rebadge.
   - When state-clusters are too coarse, all observations map to a few clusters and `D⁺(a) − D⁻(a)` becomes action-invariant — operator is silent or symmetric.
   - When state-clusters are too fine, no cell reaches N_min samples — operator silent.
   - On **stochastic transitions** (RG), the cell-conditional continuation distribution has high variance per cell; SD-comparison may be noise-bound (the strict-CDF-domination condition is rarely satisfied).
   - On **monotone-progress envs** (DeepSea-like ladder), step-penalty alone induces SD-domination that points toward shorter episodes — a curiosity-suppressor in disguise. The 2-non-degenerate-channels gate is the structural defense.
   - When the policy collapses to a single action class early (entropy collapse), only one cell per cluster ever receives samples and SD comparisons against unsampled actions are silent.
9. **Side-information channel:** {vector diagnostics, transition geometry}. The vector channels supply the per-channel cumulant deltas; transition geometry (observation embedding for cluster assignment) supplies the indexing. No verifier, no demonstrations, no reward model, no language, no environment instrumentation beyond `info["vector"]`.
10. **Monotonic improvement claim:** at every cell `(s, a)` for which the operator fires (i.e. SD-conjunction is non-empty), the nudge moves probability mass from actions that are stochastically dominated **on every channel** toward actions that dominate **on every channel**. Under stationary observation-cluster assignment and consistent policy-conditional sampling, this is monotone in the following measurable proxy: the expected per-channel quantile vector of continuation cumulants under the nudged policy is coordinate-wise ≥ that under the unnudged policy at every quantile level, conditional on cells where the operator fired. The proxy degrades exactly when the gate conditions in #8 are violated.

## Why it is not VCC / not distributional RL

Closest alive candidate is **VCC** (vcc-vector-cumulant-confluence). VCC's primitive is a Pareto-front of empirical mean continuation `c_T − c_t` deltas indexed by cumulant-vector bucket; SDS's primitive is a per-channel CDF of those same deltas indexed by observation-embedding-cluster, compared via strict first-order stochastic dominance. Pareto-front of points and stochastic-dominance of CDFs are different mathematical operators — Pareto-front is invariant to the within-cell distribution, SD is precisely *about* that distribution. Closest disqualifier is **distributional RL with renamed return distribution**: distributional RL learns a return distribution via a distributional Bellman operator and extracts policy via expectation/quantile criteria; SDS does not bootstrap the distribution (the sketch is empirical MC at episode close), never invokes a distributional Bellman, and uses only the partial order induced by stochastic dominance — never a scalar functional of the distribution. The mechanism reduces to neither under variable renaming because the partial order on CDFs is genuinely incomparable to a Bellman fixed-point or to mean-Pareto.

## Why it scales beyond the substrate

At 20k-step horizons, point-mean continuation estimates have very high variance (MC error scales as `√H/N_cell`); CDFs compress this variance into shape information that survives where means do not — the SD partial order is invariant under heavy-tailed continuation distributions and under outlier presence. At 6-component vector feedback, the multi-channel-SD conjunction becomes increasingly selective (it fires only on a strict global improvement), which is exactly the desired behavior in vector-feedback regimes — we don't want to nudge toward anything that helps one channel at another's expense, and the SD-conjunction enforces this without scalarization. At LLM-tool-use scale where `a = "generate a paragraph"`, the per-action conditioning is replaced by per-action-class (tool, prompt-template, decoding-mode) clustering of the action embedding, and the cell statistics are accumulated over historical generations within the cluster — sketch updates remain O(B) per call, cluster lookup is a single embedding-similarity query, and the SD-conjunction provides a per-tool tournament without ever scoring outputs on a learned scalar reward. The primitive does not depend on argmax over action space, only on sample-then-nudge, which is well-defined for combinatorial action sets.
