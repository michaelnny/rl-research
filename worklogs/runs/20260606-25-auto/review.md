## Reasoning

SDS's central primitive is a per-(state-cluster, action, channel) compressed empirical CDF of continuation-cumulant deltas, compared via strict first-order stochastic dominance (SD) on every channel simultaneously. This is structurally distinct from all disqualifier families and all prior attempts. It does not reduce to distributional RL: classical distributional RL (QR-DQN, C51, IQN) propagates a return distribution via a distributional Bellman operator and extracts policy via a scalar functional (mean or quantile) of that distribution; SDS writes Monte-Carlo episode-end deltas into empirical quantile sketches with no bootstrap, no Bellman, and no scalar extraction — only the partial order induced by first-order SD between per-channel CDFs is used to nudge logits. It does not reduce to Pareto-mean (VCC's primitive): VCC compares point-valued mean continuation vectors on a Pareto front, while SDS compares entire marginal CDFs under a distributional partial order — the within-cell distribution is precisely the object that carries signal, and it is invisible to Pareto-mean. The conjunction across all k channels is not scalarization: it enforces stochastic improvement on every channel simultaneously, which provably cannot be recovered by any fixed or learned weight vector wᵀr since a total order on means cannot encode CDF-dominance-at-every-quantile-level. All required-candidate-shape slots are filled with substantive answers: the experience object, primitive, improvement operator, execution rule, vector-feedback rule, rollout-cost discipline, nearest-neighbor novelty audit, predicted failure modes, side-information channel declaration, and monotonic improvement claim are each concretely specified.

The bootstrap-wall concern that killed FED, CEC, TPP, ACFC, and ARP is partially mitigated here: sketch cells are written at episode close with the full continuation delta c_T − c_t, so the universal step-penalty channel (strictly negative every step) populates sketches from episode 1, seeding non-degenerate CDFs for that channel immediately. The critical structural guard is the "≥ 2 non-degenerate channels" gate that prevents the operator from firing until a second channel (treasure, gold, gem) also has non-degenerate sketches. Whether this gate correctly fires or silently bypasses the bootstrap window on DST/RG is the primary implementation risk the Engineer must verify, but this is an empirical execution question, not a structural-identity failure.

## Risks the Engineer should be aware of

- The SD-conjunction (dominance required on ALL channels simultaneously) combined with the "≥ 2 non-degenerate" gate creates a narrow operating window: the operator is silent until both the step-penalty channel and at least one reward channel have ≥ N_min=8 samples per cell. On Deep Sea Treasure and Resource Gathering, reward channels are terminal-only — the operator may not fire until enough reward-bearing trajectories accumulate per cluster. Monitor `D+(a) - D-(a)` being non-zero for at least some decision steps by episode 50; if it remains zero throughout the 120 s budget the gate is effectively identical to FED's bootstrap wall.
- When state-clusters are too coarse (common early in training with few k-means updates), most observations land in 1–2 clusters, making `D+(a) − D-(a)` nearly action-invariant (all actions see the same sketches). The Engineer should log per-cluster cell-count distributions and confirm that at least 3–4 distinct clusters are populated before the operator begins nudging.
- The auxiliary KL-surrogate gradient step that pulls `π_θ` toward the nudged policy accumulates improvements only at decision steps where the nudge was non-zero. If the operator is silent for most of training the surrogate loss contributes nothing and behavior collapses to whatever `π_θ` learns from base policy entropy. Ensure the base policy has a sufficient entropy regularizer or the system degenerates to a near-deterministic policy before sketch coverage is sufficient.
