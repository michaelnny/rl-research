## Reasoning

PHI's central primitive — the order-2 Lévy-area (antisymmetric path-signature) in observation-embedding space, projected channel-wise through k independent linear regressors trained against terminal vector cumulants — is structurally distinct from every disqualifier family and every prior attempt. It is not Q/V/advantage (no Bellman, no return-to-go, no TD error), not scalar-weighted log-prob (no PPO/REINFORCE/GRPO), not elite trajectory cloning, not count-based exploration, not a planner/verifier stack, and not scalarization: the k regressors are fit and applied independently, and the improvement operator is a coordinate-wise Pareto-non-dominance count over a k-vector — no `wᵀr` is ever formed. The nearest prior is PFA (#29), which used signed cross-product in firing-probability space (collapsing to zero on always-on and terminal-only channels). PHI computes the same antisymmetric quantity in observation-embedding space, so it is non-trivially non-zero from the first step of the first episode — a structurally different bootstrap story than the FED/CEC/hash-collision family that killed sprints 4 and onwards. The distinction from CWAI (Jacobian column norms of a learned forward model, which shrinks under transition stochasticity) is also substantive: PHI uses a path-integral over realized observations, which averages stochastic noise as the buffer grows rather than amplifying it.

All required candidate-shape slots are filled with substantive content: the primitive is fully specified mathematically, the improvement operator has a precise update rule (`α(n_a^dom − n_a^sub)` over a coordinate-wise partial order), vector feedback is kept structurally separate end-to-end (k independent `P_m`, k-vector Pareto vote, no channel aggregation), rollout cost is accounted for with zero counterfactual rollouts, the side-information channels are explicitly named (transition geometry + vector diagnostics), predicted failure modes are stated with logged falsifiers (per-channel R², Pareto-vote firing rate), and a monotonic improvement claim is made (Pareto-improvement on the projected-area vector under the buffer distribution, contingent on R² > 0).

## Risks the Engineer should be aware of

- The claim that the Lévy-area matrix is "non-zero from step 1 regardless of reward" is correct geometrically, but the regression `P_m` fit against terminal cumulants will have near-zero R² and near-zero coefficients before any rewarded trajectory is collected on sparse envs — the operator will fire but produce near-zero logit nudges. Watch the diagnostic window R² logging carefully; the operator is effectively silent until R² lifts off zero, which may not happen within 120 s on DoorKey-8x8 or KeyCorridor.
- The "frozen random projection" embedding may fail to align observation axes with cumulant-channel-relevant directions (failure mode (a) in the hypothesis). The hypothesis's own falsifier is R² < 0.1, but consider whether a slow self-supervised embedder is needed from the start, or whether the random projection suffices if d is large enough.
- The per-(state-cluster, action) table for expected area-contributions inherits the same cell-revisitation bottleneck as TRAC/CSD/ACFC — if clusters are too fine-grained, cells are undersampled. The hypothesis does not specify cluster granularity or an adaptive coarsening fallback; the Engineer should choose cluster count conservatively (coarse) and log cluster-occupancy stats alongside R².
