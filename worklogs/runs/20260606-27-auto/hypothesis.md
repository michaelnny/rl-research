# 20260606-27-auto — PHI: Path-Homology Invariants

## Research Gate

primitive: per-(state-cluster, action) contribution to the trajectory-level Lévy-area (signed antisymmetric path-signature matrix) in observation-embedding space, projected channel-wise through k learned linear regressors trained against terminal vector cumulants
improvement_operator: Pareto-non-dominance vote on the k-dim vector of per-channel projected area-contributions per action; logit nudge `α(n_a^{dom} − n_a^{sub})` over coordinate-wise partial order
side_information: transition geometry, vector diagnostics
nearest_prior_or_disqualifier: PFA (#29 Per-Channel Phase-Flow Asymmetry) and CWAI (alive-promising, Jacobian column norms)
falsifier: per-channel projector R² stays below 0.1 on the buffer regression after the seeding window (the path signature carries no useful channel-correlated structure for the substrate's observation embeddings); or, the Pareto-non-dominance margin fires on < 5% of decision steps in the diagnostic window (operator silent)

## Mechanism

For each completed trajectory τ, embed every observation as a vector `e(o_t) ∈ R^d` (using a small frozen random projection of the observation, or a slow self-supervised embedder — implementation choice, not the primitive). Compute the trajectory's order-2 path signature, the antisymmetric Lévy-area matrix `A(τ) ∈ R^{d×d}` with `A_{ij}(τ) = (1/2) Σ_t (e_i(o_t)·e_j(o_{t+1}) − e_j(o_t)·e_i(o_{t+1}))`. This is a non-zero, action-discriminating object **on every trajectory regardless of reward**, because it integrates 2-D rotational structure of the path through embedding space — every step contributes. For each cumulant channel m ∈ {1,…,k}, fit a linear regressor `P_m : R^{d×d} → R` from `A(τ)` to the terminal cumulant `c_T(τ)[m]` (k independent regressions, never scalarized). Per-(state-cluster `s`, action `a`) maintain the empirical mean of the per-step area-contribution `Δa(t) ∈ R^{d×d}` with `Δa_{ij}(t) = (1/2)(e_i(o_t) e_j(o_{t+1}) − e_j(o_t) e_i(o_{t+1}))`, conditioned on `a_t = a` and `cluster(o_t) = s`. At decision time, project this expected per-action area-contribution through each `P_m` to obtain the k-vector `g[s, a, m] = P_m(E[Δa(t) | s, a])`. Apply the improvement operator: nudge logits at the current state by `α(n_a^{dom} − n_a^{sub})` where `n_a^{dom}` is the count of other actions whose g-row is coordinate-wise dominated by action a's row, and `n_a^{sub}` is the reverse count — pure Pareto-non-dominance partial order, no scalar collapse, no Bellman, no critic, no return-to-go.

## Required candidate shape

1. **Experience object:** Trajectories τ in a recent replay buffer, each carrying per-step observations `o_t`, executed actions `a_t`, per-step vector feedback `v_t ∈ R^k` from `info["vector"]`, and (after termination) the cumulant `c_T(τ) = Σ_t v_t ∈ R^k`. No goal labels, no event lens, no demonstrations, no verifier.

2. **Core primitive:** The per-(state-cluster, action) k-vector `g[s, a, :]` of channel-projected expected area-contributions: `g[s, a, m] = ⟨P_m, E[Δa(t) | cluster(o_t)=s, a_t=a]⟩` where `Δa_{ij}(t) = (1/2)(e_i(o_t) e_j(o_{t+1}) − e_j(o_t) e_i(o_{t+1}))` is the antisymmetric one-step Lévy-area increment in the d-dim observation embedding, and `P_m ∈ R^{d×d}` is a linear regressor fit by least squares on the buffer to predict `c_T(τ)[m]` from the trajectory-level area `A(τ) = Σ_t Δa(t)`. The k linear regressors are fit and applied independently — no Σ_m w_m P_m ever forms.

3. **Improvement operator:** Logit nudge `Δℓ(s, a) = α(n_a^{dom}(s) − n_a^{sub}(s))` where `n_a^{dom}(s) = |{a' ≠ a : g[s, a', :] ≼ g[s, a, :] ∧ g[s, a', :] ≠ g[s, a, :]}|` (count of actions strictly Pareto-dominated by a in the coordinate-wise partial order on R^k) and `n_a^{sub}(s)` is the reverse count. No scalar weight attached to any channel; the partial-order count is the entire signal.

4. **Execution rule:** Sample `a_t ~ softmax(ℓ_θ(o_t) + Δℓ(cluster(o_t), ·))` where `ℓ_θ` is a small trainable base policy (e.g. 2-layer MLP) initialized to uniform, and `Δℓ` is the operator-supplied bias. The base policy is trained by ordinary supervised distillation toward the operator-biased distribution (no scalar reward, no advantage, no PPO clip — just match the biased softmax).

5. **Vector feedback rule:** k channels are kept structurally separate end-to-end: k independent linear regressors `P_m`, each fit only against its own channel's terminal cumulant; the k channel-projections of the per-action area-contribution form a k-vector that enters the operator only through coordinate-wise Pareto-non-dominance counting. No scalarization `wᵀr` is constructed at any step. The Pareto vote is sign-and-order-sensitive but never magnitude-aggregating across channels.

6. **Rollout-cost discipline:** One environment step per agent step. No counterfactual rollouts, no simulator branches, no verifier calls. Per update: O(N · L · d²) for the trajectory-level area (N trajectories × L steps × d² area entries), O(k · d²) per regressor fit (k regressions on d² features). Per accepted improvement: zero extra rollouts. At deployment: one Pareto-vote operator pass per decision (O(|A|² · k) — count comparisons over k channels for |A| actions). Nothing branches.

7. **Nearest-neighbor novelty audit:** Closest priors are **PFA (#29 Per-Channel Phase-Flow Asymmetry)** and **CWAI (alive-promising, Channel-Wise Action Influence)**. PFA also used a signed cross-product / rotational primitive, but in **firing-probability space** (`p_m·q_m − p_m'·q_m'` for short/long-horizon channel firing heads) — which collapsed because terminal-only channels have `p_m ≈ q_m ≈ 0`. PHI computes the antisymmetric area in **observation-embedding space** (not firing-probability space), so the area is non-trivial whenever the path moves through embedding space — i.e. from step 1 of episode 1, regardless of reward. CWAI uses Jacobian column norms of a forward model (gradient magnitudes through a learned dynamics network) — these shrink under stochastic transitions, which is why CWAI failed RG. PHI uses an **averaged path-integral statistic** of realized observations (not a local linearization of a learned model), which averages out transition stochasticity rather than shrinking under it.

8. **Predicted failure modes:** (a) The frozen random projection `e(·)` may not align observation directions with cumulant-channel-relevant axes, leaving `P_m` regressors with R² ≈ 0 — operator silent, scores random. Falsifier: log per-channel R² in the diagnostic window. (b) On envs with very short paths (e.g. early DoorKey episodes lasting 5 steps), `A(τ)` is rank-deficient and `P_m` overfits — the operator over-nudges before stabilizing. Falsifier: log Pareto-vote firing rate; if > 80% of decision steps see a non-zero nudge from very few trajectories, regularization is needed. (c) On envs whose channel cumulants are essentially independent of any rotational structure (purely lexicographic / one-shot terminal events with no spatial rotation along the way), `P_m` will explain none of the channel variance and the primitive is silent. (d) Deep-Sea-Treasure has very low intrinsic dimensionality (essentially 2-D); for d much smaller than expected, the area matrix has very few independent entries and the regressor underfits. Mitigation: pick d ≥ 8.

9. **Side-information channel:** **Transition geometry** (the order-2 path signature `A(τ)` is a function purely of the realized observation transition geometry — it requires nothing beyond what the env returns at each step) and **vector diagnostics** (the k-channel `info["vector"]` is the regression target for the k independent `P_m`). No demonstrations, no pretrained priors, no verifier, no language, no instrumentation beyond what the substrate already provides via `info["vector"]`.

10. **Monotonic improvement claim:** Under the assumption that the linear regressors `P_m` recover non-zero correlations between trajectory-level Lévy-area and per-channel terminal cumulants on the buffer (a falsifiable assumption, logged via R²), the per-action vector `g[s, a, :]` is a consistent estimator (in the buffer-size limit) of the expected channel-wise Lévy-area-projected change-of-cumulant attributable to action a from cluster s. The Pareto-non-dominance count then monotonically pushes the policy toward actions whose expected channel-wise area-projection is non-dominated in the k-dimensional partial order on `R^k`. This is a Pareto-improvement claim on the **projected-area vector** under the buffer distribution, not a claim of monotonic scalar return improvement.

## Why it is not PFA (#29) or CWAI

PFA (#29) computed signed area between two **firing-probability heads** for the same channel (`p_m·q_m'` cross-product), which is identically zero on channels that are always-on (`p_m = q_m = 1`) and identically zero on terminal-only channels (`p_m = q_m = 0`); the rotational signal vanishes precisely on the substrate. PHI computes signed area in **observation-embedding space** between actually-realized observation pairs, then uses k independent linear projectors trained against per-channel cumulants to extract a per-channel reading. Under variable renaming PFA is `p_m·q_m' − p_m'·q_m` (probability-space, single-channel rotational), PHI is `e_i·e_j' − e_j·e_i'` followed by `P_m` projection (embedding-space rotational, channel-projected) — the substrate where one collapses (firing-probability space) and the other does not (observation-embedding space) is the structural distinction. Distinction from CWAI: Jacobian column norms are `‖∂f_θ(o,a)[m] / ∂e(a)‖_2` (a local linearization of the learned forward model), which shrinks under transition stochasticity; PHI's area is a path-integral over realized transitions, which averages stochastic noise as the buffer grows rather than shrinking it.

## Why it scales beyond the substrate

The order-2 path signature is a well-studied object from rough-path theory and has a hierarchy of higher-order signatures `A^{(n)} ∈ R^{d^n}` that capture progressively finer multi-scale temporal structure of trajectories. At horizon 20k actions, `A(τ)` does not blow up — it is defined as an integral and is computed in a single forward pass over the trajectory; the per-step contribution is `O(d²)`, and the total cost is linear in trajectory length. For an LLM-with-tools setting, "observation embedding" is naturally the LLM's hidden state at each tool-output ingestion step; the antisymmetric area `e_i(o_t)·e_j(o_{t+1}) − e_j(o_t)·e_i(o_{t+1})` then captures **which directions in latent space the agent's path winds through together** — an object that is well-defined for paragraph-level actions and 6-component vector feedback alike. The k independent regressors `P_m` and the Pareto-non-dominance vote scale identically: each channel is its own projector, the partial-order vote is `O(|A|² · k)` per decision regardless of how big the underlying trajectory is. The bootstrap story ("rotation in embedding space is non-zero from step 1") survives the horizon expansion: at 20k actions there are 20k more rotational increments contributing to `A(τ)`, not fewer.
