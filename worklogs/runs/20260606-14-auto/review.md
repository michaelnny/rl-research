## Reasoning

TPM's central primitive — a per-(episode-position `t`, action `a`) signed per-channel 1-D Wasserstein vector `M[t,a,:]` computed as median-differences between the terminal-cumulant subpopulations of episodes that took action `a` at step `t` vs. those that did not — is structurally distinct from every prior attempt and disqualifier family. The key structural break from the sprint-4 family (FED #15, CEC #18, CWTP #19, TPP #21, TRAC #26) is that the index is the agent's own clock `t`, not any observation-hash or state-cluster. Every episode of length `≥ t` contributes a sample to `M[t,·,:]` with no hash-collision requirement, directly dissolving the bootstrap wall that killed all hash-indexed primitives. The improvement operator is a count of coordinate-wise Pareto dominations `(n_a − m_a)`, which is invariant to per-channel monotone reparameterizations and cannot be expressed as `wᵀc_T` for any fixed or learned `w` — confirming it is not scalarized-vector-reward maximization in disguise. The distinction from REINFORCE/PPO/GRPO (scalar-weighted log-prob) holds: those multiply `∇log π(a|s)` by a scalar derived from return magnitude; TPM's coefficient is a domination count derived from the partial order on `R^k`, not a magnitude. The distinction from the alive candidate DPC is clean and clearly articulated (episode-position vs. first-divergence-state hash). All ten required-candidate-shape slots are filled with substantive answers including predicted failure modes, a monotonic improvement claim, an explicit side-information channel declaration, a falsifier, and a cold-start diagnostic requirement.

One structural subtlety is worth noting: on the vector panel envs, the step-penalty channel fires on every step, meaning `M[t,a,:]` has at least one non-trivial component from the first collected episodes and the Pareto nudge provides a real (if coarse) position-conditioned improvement signal before any terminal-reward channel fires. This is the concrete mechanism by which TPM avoids the "primitive needs reward correlation to bootstrap" cross-attempt failure mode — the step-penalty channel seeds `M` immediately, and the multi-channel Pareto gate does real work even when the terminal-reward channel is silent. This story is implicit in failure mode (e) but could be stated more prominently; if `k_eff = 1` throughout training (only step-penalty fires), the operator degenerates toward scalar median-shift, and the hypothesis's own flag applies.

## Risks the Engineer should be aware of

- The hypothesis explicitly acknowledges that on scalar-reward envs (k=1) the operator degenerates to a sign-weighted median-shift MC update. The Engineer should emit a diagnostic flag and not report scalar-env results as evidence of vector-method strength.
- The per-position storage budget grows linearly with horizon `T` and action space size `|A|`. On DoorKey/KeyCorridor with moderately long episodes this may be manageable, but the Engineer should cap the position grid (e.g. keep only the most recent `N` episodes per cell and use log-spaced position buckets) to avoid memory pressure exhausting the 120-s budget.
- The step "median-difference shortcut" for signed 1-D Wasserstein is only a valid surrogate when both subpopulations `B^+_{t,a}` and `B^-_{t,a}` have at least 2 samples; the Engineer should add a minimum-count guard (e.g. ≥ 3 per side) before emitting a nudge for a given `(t, a)` cell, or early updates will be dominated by single-sample noise.
