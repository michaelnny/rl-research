## Reasoning

PCGA's central primitive is the cosine similarity in **parameter space** between `∇_θ log π(a|s)` and `∇_θ ĉ_m(s)` for each action-channel pair — producing a `|A| × k` alignment matrix per state — with the improvement operator being a coordinate-wise Pareto-dominance vote over that matrix that nudges logits by `(n_a − m_a)`. This is structurally distinct from every disqualifier family: the actor-critic disqualifier requires that a critic's **output value** weight the policy gradient (scalar advantage × ∇ log π); here the head's output magnitude is never consumed by the policy update at all — only the direction of its parameter-space gradient enters, and the inter-action comparison is a partial order on `R^k`, not a scalar ranking. Under variable renaming the primitive does not reduce to Q, V, advantage, return-to-go, or any scalar weighting of log-prob. It is also structurally different from the alive CWAI candidate, whose primitive is the L2 norm of the forward-model output Jacobian in **output space** (sensitive to stochastic-transition noise); PCGA's cosine is between two parameter-space vectors and is unaffected by forward-model rollout variance. No prior attempt in `prior_attempts.md` #1–#26 uses parameter-space gradient alignment as the action-discrimination primitive. All required candidate-shape slots are filled with substantive answers: experience object, core primitive with exact formula, improvement operator with precise update rule, execution rule, vector feedback rule, rollout-cost discipline, nearest-neighbor novelty audit, predicted failure modes with concrete diagnostics, side-information channel (learned dynamics + vector diagnostics), and a geometrically grounded monotonic improvement claim (Pareto-meet of worst-channel cosine alignment of the trunk's parameter trajectory is non-decreasing when boosting Pareto-non-dominated actions).

The critical structural advance over the sprint-4 failures is the bootstrap argument: whereas FED, CEC, TPP, PCR, TRAC, and the rest required observation-hash bucket collisions or terminal-outcome gates that are silent before the first rewarded trajectory, PCGA's alignment tensor is computable on every SGD step from the first training update because the auxiliary head always has a loss (the step-penalty channel fires densely). The falsifier is concrete (per-step variance of `A` across actions < 0.1 sustained triggers the dead-family alarm), which satisfies the requirement that predicted failure modes be learnable from running the experiment.

## Risks the Engineer should be aware of

- The per-decision cost is `|A| × k` full backward passes through the trunk. For the panel envs (`|A| ≤ 7, k ≤ 2`) this is cheap, but the architecture must share a trunk between policy head and auxiliary head — if the Engineer separates the final layers, the cosine trivially decouples by action (failure mode b in the hypothesis). Verify trunk-sharing before running.
- Failure mode (c) — channel collinearity causing Pareto order to collapse to total order on a single channel — is a real risk on Deep Sea Treasure where step-penalty and treasure gradients may be nearly collinear during early training. The diagnostic (per-step rank of the row-stacked alignment matrix) should be logged from step 1 to catch a scalarization rebadge in the data.
- The monotonic improvement claim is in parameter space (trunk trajectory vs. cumulant-prediction manifold), not in expected reward space. This is honest and the hypothesis is explicit about it, but it means panel success is not guaranteed by the theory — the operator could improve cumulant-prediction alignment while the policy explores in directions irrelevant to task reward. The falsifier condition correctly targets this gap.
