# 20260606-15-auto — PCGA: Per-Channel Gradient Alignment

## Research Gate

primitive: per-(state, action, channel) cosine alignment in *parameter space* between the policy's log-probability gradient `∇_θ log π(a | s)` and an auxiliary supervised head's per-channel cumulant-prediction gradient `∇_θ ĉ_m(s)`.
improvement_operator: at each decision step, nudge action logits by `α · (n_a − m_a)` where `n_a` is the count of other actions whose alignment vector `A[a, :] ∈ R^k` Pareto-dominates `A[a', :]` coordinate-wise, and `m_a` is the count dominating it. No scalar collapse; the head's predicted *value* never enters the policy update.
side_information: learned dynamics (auxiliary cumulant-prediction head trained from observed suffix cumulants), vector diagnostics (per-channel signal from `info["vector"]`).
nearest_prior_or_disqualifier: actor-critic disqualifier family (a critic supplies scalar advantage to weight log-prob gradients) and CWAI (`worklogs/candidates/cwai-channel-wise-action-influence.md`, forward-model Jacobian in output space).
falsifier: panel run with diagnostic logging shows either (a) the alignment matrix `A[s, a, m]` is nearly uniform across actions for >95% of decision steps (Pareto vote degenerate), or (b) it produces no above-random score on any vector env after 120 s. Concretely: if mean per-step domination-count variance across actions is < 0.1 for the bulk of training, the parameter-space alignment carries no action-discriminating information and the family is dead.

## Mechanism

The policy network shares a trunk with a small auxiliary head `ĉ_θ : observation → R^k` that regresses, by simple MSE, on the observed cumulant suffix `c_{t+1:t+W} − c_t` (sum of vector feedback over a window of W steps after each step) collected in a replay buffer. The auxiliary head is trained on every step, including pre-success steps where the dense channels (e.g. step-penalty) carry signal even though the precious channels are zero. At each decision step in state `s`, for each candidate action `a` and each channel `m`, the primitive computes `A[s, a, m] = cos(∇_θ log π(a | s), ∇_θ ĉ_m(s))` — the cosine similarity in parameter space between the direction the policy moves when it commits to action `a` at `s` and the direction the head moves when it predicts cumulant on channel `m` at `s`. The vector `A[s, a, :] ∈ R^k` is then compared coordinate-wise across actions: action `a` Pareto-dominates `a'` iff `A[s, a, m] ≥ A[s, a', m]` for all `m` and strict on at least one. The logit nudge is `α · (n_a − m_a)` where `(n_a, m_a)` are the dominated/dominating counts of `a` over peers; the policy is sampled categorically as usual. There is no value, no advantage, no scalar return weighting, and no use of the head's output magnitude — only the *direction* of its parameter-space gradient enters.

## Required candidate shape

1. **Experience object:** Standard trajectory tuples `(o_t, a_t, r_t, info["vector"]_t, o_{t+1})` accumulated in a small replay buffer; per-step cumulant-suffix targets `s_t = Σ_{τ=1..W} v_{t+τ}` for the auxiliary head's regression labels.
2. **Core primitive:** The parameter-space gradient-alignment tensor `A[s, a, m] = ⟨∇_θ log π(a | s), ∇_θ ĉ_m(s)⟩ / (‖∇_θ log π(a | s)‖ · ‖∇_θ ĉ_m(s)‖)`. This is a `|A| × k` real matrix per state, computed on demand at decision time.
3. **Improvement operator:** Logit update `Δ logit(a | s) = α · (n_a − m_a)` where `n_a = #{a' ≠ a : A[s, a, :] ≻ A[s, a', :]}` and `m_a = #{a' ≠ a : A[s, a', :] ≻ A[s, a, :]}` under coordinate-wise partial order on `R^k`. Auxiliary head trained by ordinary supervised MSE regression in a separate optimizer step (its parameters move; its outputs are never used as weights on the policy update).
4. **Execution rule:** Categorical sample from the post-nudge softmax over actions. No special exploration schedule; the alignment-driven nudge itself produces directional bias from step 1 because gradients are non-degenerate even before any channel fires.
5. **Vector feedback rule:** Each channel `m ∈ {1..k}` gets its own auxiliary head output `ĉ_m`, its own gradient direction `∇_θ ĉ_m`, and its own column in `A`. Channels are never summed, weighted, or projected; the partial-order comparison is on the raw `R^k` vector.
6. **Rollout-cost discipline:** One env step per env step; no counterfactual rollouts, no simulator branches, no verifier calls. Per update: one auxiliary-head SGD step + one policy SGD step. Per decision: `|A| · k` backward passes through the trunk to compute alignment columns (parallelizable; on a 3090 Ti for `|A|≤7, k≤2` this is sub-millisecond per decision).
7. **Nearest-neighbor novelty audit:** The closest prior is the actor-critic disqualifier family — a critic provides per-state scalar weight on the policy gradient. PCGA's auxiliary head is *not* a critic: its scalar value is never multiplied into the policy update; only the *direction* of its parameter-space gradient enters, and the comparison across actions is a coordinate-wise partial order, not a scalar weight. The closest alive candidate is CWAI (Jacobian of forward-model output w.r.t. action embedding); PCGA differs structurally because Jacobians are in *output* space (sensitive to forward-model noise on stochastic envs, which killed CWAI on Resource Gathering), while parameter-space gradient alignment is an inner product of two parameter-space vectors and does not depend on forward-model rollout fidelity.
8. **Predicted failure modes:**
   - **(a) Gradient-alignment near-uniformity.** If the policy and head share most parameters and the trunk dominates both gradients, `A[s, a, m]` may be nearly action-invariant; Pareto vote saturates. Diagnostic: per-step variance of `A` across actions; falsifier fires if variance < 0.1 sustained.
   - **(b) Decoupled-head heads.** If policy outputs come from a separated final layer, `∇_θ log π(a)` is supported only on that layer's `a`-th row, while `∇_θ ĉ_m` is supported on a different layer; the cosine is then trivially zero or trivially-action-decoupled. Mitigation: alignment is computed on the *trunk parameters only*, and the architecture must share a trunk.
   - **(c) Channel collinearity in `R^k`.** If `k=2` and the two channels have nearly co-linear gradients (e.g. step-penalty correlates with treasure on terminal-only DST), the partial order degenerates to total order on one channel — scalarization rebadge. Diagnostic: per-step rank of the row-stacked alignment matrix; fall back to single-channel mode is the rebadge alarm.
   - **(d) Cold-start head underfit.** If the auxiliary head's gradient is essentially noise (untrained), the alignment is meaningless. Less severe than CWAI's analogous failure because the policy head also has noisy gradients early, so cosine *of two noises* is not directionally biased and the operator is symmetric (no harm); the operator engages as the head learns the dense step-penalty channel.
9. **Side-information channel:** Two channels: (i) **learned dynamics** — the auxiliary cumulant-prediction head is a partial dynamics model trained by self-supervision on observed `info["vector"]`; (ii) **vector diagnostics** — the per-channel decomposition is preserved through the partial-order comparison, never collapsed.
10. **Monotonic improvement claim:** Under the conditions that (i) the auxiliary head is consistent (its expected gradient direction `E[∇_θ ĉ_m(s)]` points along the manifold of trunk parameters that improve channel-`m` cumulant prediction at state `s`), and (ii) the policy gradient `∇_θ log π(a|s)` is action-discriminating on the trunk, then in expectation each Pareto-non-dominated action `a*` has a parameter-space update direction that is *cosine-positively* aligned with at least one channel's predictive-improvement direction and *not* cosine-negatively aligned on any. Boosting `π(a*|s)` therefore monotonically improves the *worst-channel cosine alignment* of the trunk's parameter trajectory with the cumulant-prediction manifold — a Pareto-meet quantity over channels. This is a parameter-space monotonicity claim, not a reward-space one, and it is what justifies the operator without reducing to value or advantage.

## Why it is not actor-critic / CWAI

Actor-critic disqualifier: a critic produces a scalar `V(s)` or `Q(s,a)` and the policy update is `∇ log π · weight(critic_output)`. In PCGA the head produces a *vector* `ĉ(s) ∈ R^k`, but the head's *output magnitude* is never used as a weight on the policy gradient. Only the cosine of two parameter-space gradient vectors enters, and the operator compares cosines across actions in a coordinate-wise partial order. The head is a parameter-space *direction generator*, not a scalar advantage estimator — under variable renaming it cannot be reduced to a critic because no scalar value of the head is consumed by the policy update. Distinction from CWAI (`worklogs/candidates/cwai-channel-wise-action-influence.md`): CWAI's primitive is `‖∂f_θ(o, a)[m] / ∂e(a)‖_2` — the L2 norm of the forward-model output Jacobian in *output* space (next-observation channel `m`) w.r.t. action embedding. PCGA's primitive is the *cosine* of two *parameter-space* gradients (log-policy gradient × cumulant-head gradient). These are different geometric objects in different spaces; CWAI's failure on Resource Gathering (stochastic transitions shrink the Jacobian toward noise floor) does not transfer because parameter-space gradients are not unrolled and not subject to forward-model variance.

## Why it scales beyond the substrate

The primitive is `O(|A| · k)` parameter-space backward passes per decision, with `|A|` candidate actions and `k` channels. For an LLM with `|A| = 50000` (vocabulary), this is intractable as written — but the primitive *naturally* admits the same approximation that classical policy-gradient methods use: sample a small candidate set `A_s ⊂ A` (top-`m` from the prior), compute alignment only on `A_s`, and apply the partial-order nudge within. For long horizons (20k actions), the auxiliary head's window-`W` cumulant target adapts smoothly: `W` is decoupled from horizon length, and the head can be trained on any-length suffixes via discounted-window or unbounded-window targets; the parameter-space alignment is not a function of horizon length per se. For vector feedback at scale (k=6: success/cost/safety/latency/validity/preference), the head simply has 6 output dimensions and the partial-order is on `R^6` — Pareto saturation in 6 dimensions is a real risk and is precisely the predicted failure mode (c), which the falsifier exposes. Crucially, the primitive does *not* require reward correlation to bootstrap: the head's gradient direction is non-degenerate from the first SGD step on any signal channel that fires non-trivially (step-penalty in our envs; tool-call count or token-count in LLM settings), and the cosine alignment fires from step 1. This addresses the dominant failure mode in `prior_attempts.md` — the bootstrap wall that killed FED, CEC, TPP, PCR, SIT, RSD, JFP, TPM.
