## Reasoning

CID's central primitive — the per-(observation, action, channel) one-step log-likelihood-ratio `LR_m[o, a] = log q_m(ô_{t+1}(o, a)) − log q_m(o_t)`, where `q_m` is a supervised binary firing-probability head — is structurally distinct from every disqualifier family and every prior attempt. The nearest disqualifiers are GVFs/successor features (which predict cumulative discounted cumulant magnitude and require downstream linear scalarization `wᵀψ`) and the CWAI/JFP gradient-norm family (which compute Jacobian column norms or Jacobian peak-times and shrink toward the noise floor under stochastic transitions). CID's primitive is a probability-space object, not a gradient-magnitude object and not a cumulative magnitude: the binary classifier `q_m` is trained on the same stochastic distribution it is later evaluated on, so added transition stochasticity re-weights the LR rather than shrinking it — this is the precisely articulated structural distinction from CWAI's confirmed RG collapse. The improvement operator (Pareto-non-dominance vote on the k-dimensional LR vector, logit nudge by signed domination count `n_a − m_a`) never collapses channels via a fixed or learned weight `w`, which separates it from scalarized multi-objective RL; and it does not use Bellman backup, policy-gradient reward weighting, or elite trajectory cloning. All required-candidate-shape slots are substantively filled: the primitive is precisely stated with an update equation, the improvement operator has a clear composition law (coordinate-wise Pareto on ℝ^k), the side-information channels are explicitly declared (vector diagnostics + learned dynamics), the monotonic improvement claim is given (Pareto-monotone increase in per-step channel-imminence vector under well-calibrated heads), and four concrete predicted failure modes are listed with falsification criteria.

The hypothesis also threads the sprint-4 bootstrap-wall problem: unlike FED/CEC/TPP/PCR, CID's `q_m` is trained on per-step channel-firing labels drawn from the step-penalty channel (which fires on every step in the panel envs), meaning the supervised heads receive dense signal from the very first trajectory rather than waiting for hash-bucket collisions or terminal-reward appearances. The channel-active gate (exclude heads with AUC < 0.55 from the Pareto vote) is a clean mechanism for handling terminal-only precious channels without letting them inject noise or collapse the operator. The bootstrap question — whether `q_m` for precious channels learns fast enough to contribute before the run ends — is acknowledged as the primary empirical risk and left to the substrate run to answer.

## Risks the Engineer should be aware of

- The improvement operator is well-defined only when at least one channel passes the AUC gate; if all channels are terminal-only and the step-penalty channel is also excluded (e.g., due to calibration lag at the very start of training), the Pareto nudge is zero everywhere and the policy effectively drifts randomly. Early training must be verified to have at least one active channel.
- The forward model `f_φ` trained by L2 on observation deltas can be accurate only in expectation; on stochastic environments it outputs a blurred average. The hypothesis correctly notes this causes action-invariant `LR` rows when `f_φ` cannot distinguish actions — but this degeneration is silent (nudge goes to zero rather than injecting bad signal), which is acceptable. The Engineer should log `f_φ` per-(obs, action) MSE variance and LR row-variance as diagnostics.
- The single-channel-collapse falsifier is explicit and well-scoped: if only the step-penalty channel passes the AUC gate on Resource Gathering after 90 s, the method has degenerated to RND-like exploration suppression. The Engineer must instrument this check and flag collapsed runs separately from successful ones to avoid masking the failure mode in aggregate scores.
