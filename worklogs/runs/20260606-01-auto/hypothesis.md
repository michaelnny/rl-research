# 20260606-01-auto — CEC: Continuation-Endpoint Concordance

## Research Gate

primitive: per-(state, action) empirical Pareto front of vector cumulants accrued from t to the next *naturally-defined exit observation*, indexed by exit-observation-hash bucket
improvement_operator: at each visited state, for each pair of actions (a, a') compare their per-bucket Pareto-dominance counts (number of exit-hash buckets where a's cumulant-to-exit Pareto-dominates a's, minus reverse) and nudge logits by the resulting signed concordance count
side_information: reachability/reset structure + vector diagnostics
nearest_prior_or_disqualifier: VCC (worklogs/candidates/vcc-vector-cumulant-confluence.md) and RSD (worklogs/candidates/rsd-reconvergent-segment-dominance.md)
falsifier: on the vector envs (Deep Sea Treasure, Resource Gathering), instrument exit-hash bucket occupancy and per-bucket per-action sample counts; if after the seeding phase the median action at the median visited state has fewer than two distinct exit-hash buckets with ≥ 2 samples each, the concordance count is structurally zero and CEC cannot improve over random — and we abandon the primitive. Behaviorally, CEC must beat random on at least Deep Sea Treasure (where the cumulant-to-exit is informative on the step-penalty channel even before the treasure channel fires) within the 120s vector stage.

## Mechanism

CEC's primitive is a per-(state-hash, action) bag of vector samples *indexed by trajectory exit*. An exit is the observation hash at the next moment the episode resets — terminal step, time limit, or door-induced reset, whichever comes first. For every step (s_t, a_t) of every collected episode, we record `(exit_hash := H(o_T), Δc := Σ_{u=t}^{T-1} v_u)` where `v_u` is the per-step vector signal from `info["vector"]`. The bag for (s, a) is therefore `B(s,a) = { (b, Δc) }` partitioned by exit-hash bucket b. Concordance between actions a and a' at state s is `C(s; a, a') = Σ_{b ∈ B(s,a) ∩ B(s,a')} 1[ mean_{Δc ∈ B(s,a,b)}(Δc) ≻ mean_{Δc ∈ B(s,a',b)}(Δc) ] − reverse`, where `≻` is the strict coordinate-wise Pareto order on `R^k`. The improvement operator nudges the policy logit at state s by `α · Σ_{a'} C(s; a, a') / |A|` — a single per-state per-action scalar derived without any scalar reward weighting; the "signal" is the count of buckets in which a Pareto-dominates a', never the magnitude of any one channel. No critic, no return target, no Bellman backup, no elite trajectory selection.

## Required candidate shape

1. **Experience object:** Completed episodes with per-step vector feedback `v_u` (from `info["vector"]`), the visited observation hashes `H(o_u)`, and the terminal observation hash `H(o_T)`. No demonstrations, no reset hooks beyond the standard episode-reset boundary.
2. **Core primitive:** The per-(state-hash, action) Pareto-bucket multiset `B(s,a) = { (exit_hash_b, mean Δc_b) }` and the cross-action concordance count `C(s; a, a')`. The mathematical object is a labeled multiset of vector-valued points in `R^k` — *not* a return distribution (one bucket per exit, not one mass at the realized scalar return) and *not* a successor feature (no expectation of cumulants, only a Pareto comparison of bucket-conditional means).
3. **Improvement operator:** `logit(s, a) ← logit(s, a) + α · Σ_{a' ≠ a} C(s; a, a')` after each completed episode. Update is purely *partial-order* signed: the magnitude of the concordance is a count of dominance events across exit-hash buckets, never a function of the cumulant magnitudes themselves.
4. **Execution rule:** Sample actions from softmax over the maintained logits, with a small fixed temperature. No conditioning on goals, return targets, or scalar weights.
5. **Vector feedback rule:** Vector signals are kept vector-valued throughout. The Pareto comparison `mean Δc_b(a) ≻ mean Δc_b(a')` is the only operation reducing vectors to a Boolean — and that Boolean is then *counted across exit-hash buckets*, not summed over channels. No fixed weighting, no learned scalarization.
6. **Rollout-cost discipline:** One environment step per primitive update increment; one episode per fully-formed `Δc` sample. No counterfactual replays, no simulator branches, no verifier calls. A logit update is performed once per completed episode (cost: linear in episode length to record samples, sub-linear in distinct-state-hash count to update logits). At deployment, a single forward pass picks the action.
7. **Nearest-neighbor novelty audit:** Closest priors are VCC (`worklogs/candidates/vcc-vector-cumulant-confluence.md`) and RSD (`worklogs/candidates/rsd-reconvergent-segment-dominance.md`). VCC indexes by the *running global cumulant bucket* and compares cross-action front-margins on continuation deltas — it suffers when the running cumulant is uninformative (k_eff = 1 case). CEC indexes by the *trajectory exit-observation hash*, which is a *reset-structural* coordinate guaranteed to be well-defined every episode and informative even on terminal-only-reward channels (because the *non-reward* vector channels — step counter, position deltas — accumulate distinctly under different exits). RSD indexes by *(start, end)* observation-hash pairs of intra-trajectory segments and requires *closed* witness pairs to form between any two trajectories; CEC requires only that *some* exits are reached repeatedly across episodes from the same state, which is far weaker. From the disqualifier list, CEC is *not* return-to-go (return-to-go is a scalar; CEC keeps the cumulant vector-valued and partitions it by exit), *not* a GVF (GVFs are scalar/vector cumulant *expectations* per state; CEC's central object is a multiset of bucket-conditional Pareto-comparable points, with a *partial-order count* operator on top), *not* HER (no goal relabeling — exits are the actual terminal observations encountered, not pretended targets), *not* a successor feature (no inner product with a learned weight vector and no expectation), and *not* count-based exploration (the count is across *exit-hash buckets where one action dominates another*, never a count of state visits).
8. **Predicted failure modes:**
   - **Singleton-exit collapse.** If every episode in the substrate ends at a unique exit-hash, every bucket is a singleton and no within-bucket Pareto comparison can be made — CEC's signal is zero. Diagnostic: median bucket size after the first `N` episodes. (Predicted on Resource Gathering if the home cell observation is not stable; predicted not to apply on Deep Sea Treasure where exit positions are a small finite set.)
   - **Same-exit determinism.** If from a given state s the policy already reaches only one exit-hash regardless of action (the agent is already committed), the cross-action concordance is uncomputable. Mitigated by softmax sampling temperature.
   - **Stochastic transitions.** Under noisy dynamics the bucket-mean cumulant is high-variance; small Pareto-dominance margins flip sign with sample noise. CEC will need bucket-sample-count thresholds to fire only on robust dominance. Predicted to fail outright on heavily stochastic envs without thresholds.
   - **Per-channel-magnitude-degenerate exits.** If across all exits the cumulant differs only in the step-counter channel (everyone takes the same path until termination), CEC degenerates to a "shorter-is-better" preferer — useful but trivial.
   - **State-hash collision.** As in SIT/RSD, if `H(o)` has high collision rate, the concordance pools across genuinely different states. Diagnostic: estimated collision rate via paired-step observation comparison.
9. **Side-information channel:** **Reachability/reset structure** (the exit observation is a reset-defined coordinate the environment exposes naturally) and **vector diagnostics** (per-step `info["vector"]`). Both are explicitly on the allowed side-info list. No event traces, no dynamics model, no demonstrations, no language description, no verifier feedback.
10. **Monotonic improvement claim:** Under (a) stable observation-hashing, (b) ≥ 2 samples per (state, action, exit-hash) bucket, and (c) deterministic transitions, repeatedly applying `logit(s,a) += α · Σ_{a'} C(s; a, a')` weakly increases the population of trajectories whose per-channel cumulant Pareto-dominates the population mean *within every reached exit-hash bucket*. Equivalently: at fixed exit-hash, the per-channel mean cumulant is monotonically non-decreasing in the partial order. The proof sketch is the same partial-order argument that makes Pareto-coordinate-wise voting consistent: each component of the signed concordance count can only flip from supporting `a` to supporting `a'` when the bucket-conditional means' Pareto relation flips, which under stable transitions and growing samples is a one-way change in the dominant direction. The claim is *conditional* on bucket multiplicity ≥ 2 — which is precisely the falsifier above.

## Why it is not VCC / RSD / return-to-go

Versus **VCC** (alive-weak): VCC indexes by the agent's *running global cumulant bucket* — the cumulative vector signal so far in the current episode. That bucket is sparse on terminal-only-reward channels and explodes when over-quantized. CEC indexes by the *terminal observation hash* of the episode, which is a reset-structural coordinate the environment provides for free, naturally aggregating across states that lead to the same exit. The cumulant-to-exit `Δc` is never used as the bucketing key; it is the per-bucket payload over which Pareto comparison is performed.

Versus **RSD** (alive-weak): RSD compares *closed parallel edges* sharing both start and end observation hashes within or across trajectories — a far rarer event than reaching the same terminal. CEC requires only that the *exit* hash be shared, which happens by construction whenever two episodes terminate similarly (almost always, on substrate envs with finite goal/lava cells).

Versus **return-to-go / GVFs / successor features**: those collapse the cumulant trace to a scalar (RTG), an unconditional vector expectation (GVF), or a linear functional thereof (successor feature with weight `w`). CEC does none of those: it keeps the cumulant vector-valued, partitions it by exit-hash bucket *before* any aggregation, and reduces only via per-bucket Pareto dominance to a count — there is no point at which a scalar return, a linear projection, or a value estimate appears.

## Why it scales beyond the substrate

The exit-observation-hash side-information channel generalizes naturally: at LLM-tool-use scale, the natural "exit" of a sub-episode is the *outcome label* of the verification call (compiled / didn't, test passed / failed, user accepted / rejected) — these are coarse categorical buckets that aggregate millions of distinct full-action paragraphs into a small finite set. The per-step vector cumulant becomes per-step diagnostics (token cost, latency, safety violations, intermediate test scores). Per-(state, action) Pareto-bucket comparison continues to work — the action distribution at a context is over paragraphs, but the *bucket-conditional Pareto dominance* is over the same `R^k` cumulant, and only the count of dominance events ever updates logits. At 20k-action horizons in continuous-control, the same primitive operates if exits are defined by reset-events or by observation-revisit modulo a learned hash; the partial-order voting mechanism does not depend on horizon length, only on bucket multiplicity. The mechanism's sample efficiency comes from the fact that a single completed trajectory contributes O(T) (state, action, exit, Δc) samples — every step learns from the eventual exit — without any counterfactual rollouts, branches, or extra calls.
