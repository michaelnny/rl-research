## Reasoning

CEC's central primitive — a per-(state-hash, action) multiset of vector cumulants indexed by *exit-observation-hash bucket*, with logit updates driven by signed Pareto-dominance counts across those buckets — is structurally distinct from all disqualifier families and all prior attempts. The key structural novelty is the choice of bucketing coordinate: instead of hashing the visited observation mid-trajectory (as FED #15 did, leading to sparse bucket occupancy), CEC hashes the *terminal episode observation*. Every completed episode contributes exactly one exit-hash sample, so bucket occupancy grows as O(episodes) rather than O(episodes × 1/|reachable_states|). The improvement operator is a partial-order vote count: the number of exit-hash buckets where action a's bucket-conditional mean cumulant coordinate-wise dominates action a', minus the reverse — no scalar reward weighting, no Bellman backup, no critic, no linear functional of the cumulant vector. This is not a return-to-go rebadge (RTG is a scalar; CEC keeps the vector and partitions by exit identity before any aggregation), not a GVF (GVFs are unconditional cumulant expectations or linear functionals; CEC uses per-bucket conditional means compared by partial order, never a linear projection), not HER (exits are actual terminal observations, not relabeled virtual goals), and not distributional RL (the bucket key is the exit observation's identity hash, not a quantile of the scalar return). All required-candidate-shape slots are filled with substantive answers: a single primitive plus a single composition law (concordance count), a conditional monotonic improvement claim, a named falsifier with a specific diagnostic, explicit predicted failure modes, and a declared side-information channel (reachability/reset structure + vector diagnostics).

The nearest prior-attempt risk is FED (#15), which used the same "empirical Pareto front / outcome-multiset indexed by obs-hash" family and scored 0.0 across all envs due to bucket sparsity. CEC's architectural response — using exit-hash (one per episode) rather than visited-state-hash (many per episode, most unique) — is a genuine structural change in where sample mass accumulates, not a vocabulary rename. The falsifier provided (check median bucket size after the seeding phase; abandon if median is < 2 distinct exit-hash buckets per action with ≥ 2 samples) makes CEC directly testable against that same failure mode and allows early termination if the fix does not hold.

## Risks the Engineer should be aware of

- The sample-accumulation argument depends on the substrate's exit-hash space being small (finite goal/lava cells on MiniGrid envs). On Resource Gathering, if the home-cell observation is not stable across resets, the exit-hash space may be effectively unbounded — the hypothesis acknowledges this as the "singleton-exit collapse" failure mode, so instrument bucket occupancy early and terminate fast if it fires.
- The monotonic improvement claim is conditional on deterministic transitions and bucket multiplicity ≥ 2; on the two stochastic-transition envs the update may be sign-noisy. Consider gating logit updates on a minimum per-bucket sample count (the hypothesis mentions this mitigation but does not specify the threshold — pin it before the run).
- On DoorKey and KeyCorridor (sparse, long-horizon, non-vector envs), the non-reward vector channels (step counter, position deltas) provide the only pre-reward cumulant signal. The hypothesis argues these channels are informative; verify that `info["vector"]` on the sparse envs actually contains step-counter or position information, not just the scalar reward repeated.
