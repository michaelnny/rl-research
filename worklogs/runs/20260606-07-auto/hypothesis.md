# 20260606-07-auto — CRP: Channel Rank-Position Concordance

## Research Gate

primitive: per-(state-cluster, action, channel) empirical mean of *within-trajectory rank-percentile* of that channel's firing magnitude at the step where the action was invoked, with the rank computed only over steps in the trajectory at which the channel was nonzero
improvement_operator: at each decision step, nudge policy logits toward actions whose per-channel rank-percentile vector is Pareto-non-dominated on positive-trend channels and Pareto-non-dominated on inverse-rank for negative-trend channels (channel trend = sign of its buffer-wide mean firing magnitude)
side_information: vector diagnostics, transition geometry (used only to define a coarse state-cluster via the same observation-hash mechanism the substrate already exposes)
nearest_prior_or_disqualifier: GVFs / successor features with renamed cumulants (disqualifier family); also adjacent to #14 Primal Behavior Flow Pivot and to VCC (alive-weak)
falsifier: on every panel env we record the fraction of (cluster, action, channel) cells whose realized rank-percentile distribution is non-degenerate (i.e., not concentrated at a single value) after the seeding phase; the candidate is falsified if (a) more than 80 percent of cells degenerate to constant rank-percentile across all channels, or (b) the panel result is ≤ random on every vector env. Either outcome means rank-position carries no signal beyond what magnitude-integrals (successor features / return-to-go) already encode.

## Mechanism

For every completed trajectory τ of length T, for every channel m, define the *firing-step set* `S_m(τ) = {t : v_t[m] ≠ 0}`. For each step t at which channel m fires, define its *within-trajectory rank-percentile* `ρ_t[m] = (rank of v_t[m] within {v_{t'}[m] : t' ∈ S_m(τ)}) / |S_m(τ)|`, broken arbitrarily at ties. The primitive is the running mean `R[s, a, m] = E_τ[ρ_t[m] | cluster(o_t) = s, a_t = a, t ∈ S_m(τ)]`, accumulated incrementally across the rollout buffer; missing cells (no observation) are flagged as undefined and excluded from comparison. The improvement operator, executed once per gradient step, is: for each cluster s with at least two visited actions, compute the channel trend vector `σ ∈ {-1, +1}^k` from the buffer-wide mean of each channel's per-step magnitude; form `R̃[s, a, m] = R[s, a, m]` if `σ_m = +1` else `1 - R[s, a, m]`; mark action `a` at cluster `s` as preferred iff `R̃[s, a, :]` is Pareto-non-dominated by every other action `a'` at the same cluster (coordinate-wise ≥, strict on at least one channel where both are defined); apply a fixed-magnitude logit nudge α toward all preferred actions at every visited cluster. No critic, no Bellman backup, no scalar advantage, no reward weight, no elite cloning. Rank-percentile is unitless and channel-magnitude-invariant by construction; the comparison law is Pareto on a positional statistic, not on a magnitude integral.

## Required candidate shape

1. **Experience object:** Ordinary on-policy rollouts. Each step records `(o_t, a_t, v_t)` with `v_t ∈ R^k` the vector channel reading. Trajectories are processed in full at episode end so that within-trajectory ranks are exact.
2. **Core primitive:** The tensor `R[s, a, m]` — per (state-cluster, action, channel) running mean of within-trajectory rank-percentile, restricted to firing steps. This is a position-in-time statistic, not a magnitude integral. Two policies can have identical successor features and identical return-to-go yet produce disjoint `R` tensors (e.g. one fires channel m early-ranked at action `a`, the other late-ranked at `a`, with the same total mass).
3. **Improvement operator:** Pareto-rank across actions within a cluster on the trend-corrected rank vector `R̃[s, a, :]`; deliver a fixed-α logit nudge at every observed (s, a) where `a` is non-dominated. Updates are applied uniformly; no per-trajectory reweighting; no off-policy correction; no critic interpolation.
4. **Execution rule:** Greedy on the current (nudged) policy logits with a small uniform exploration floor `ε`. No best-of-N, no MCTS, no verifier replay; one online action per step.
5. **Vector feedback rule:** Channels are kept separate at every stage. Trend signs `σ_m` are computed independently per channel and used only as a *direction-of-rank* selector; they are never combined into a scalar weight, and the improvement operator is Pareto-non-domination over the trend-corrected rank vector. On envs with k=1 the operator degenerates to "prefer actions whose firing-step ranks are early" — this case is explicitly flagged as a degenerate scalarization-equivalent and we do not claim novelty in that regime.
6. **Rollout-cost discipline:** One rollout per episode, no counterfactuals, no verifier calls, no simulator branches, no replay augmentation. A seeding phase of `N_seed` random episodes (counted in the budget) precedes the operator firing — needed because the trend vector and the rank distributions both require a populated buffer. Per-update cost is one pass over the trajectory to compute ranks and one tensor accumulation.
7. **Nearest-neighbor novelty audit:** Closest disqualifier is GVFs / successor features. Successor features and return-to-go integrate channel *magnitude* over future steps; CRP integrates *rank-position* of the firing step within its own channel's firing-set, which is unitless and invariant to channel scaling. Two actions with identical successor features can have orthogonal CRP signatures (early-ranked vs late-ranked firings of the same total mass). Closest alive candidate is VCC (vector-cumulant confluence), which buckets by cumulant magnitude and compares Pareto continuation fronts — VCC is a magnitude-integral statistic; CRP is a rank-position statistic. Closest among the failed family is FED (#15) and CEC (#18), which gate updates on observation-hash bucket population; CRP is gated on per-trajectory rank computation, which fires from the very first trajectory containing a non-degenerate firing-set in any channel — independent of cross-trajectory hash collisions.
8. **Predicted failure modes:**
   - Envs where every channel is either uniform-per-step (step penalty) or fires only at termination: ranks degenerate to (constant 0.5 with arbitrary tie-breaks) or (constant 1.0 at the terminal step), and the operator fires no useful nudge. Deep Sea Treasure may sit on this edge case if the only non-step channel is terminal-only — falsifier (a) checks for this.
   - Envs with deterministic, monotone progress (DeepSea-like): rank-percentile is highly correlated with step-index, and the operator may collapse to "prefer late-step actions" or its reverse — a positional bias, not a per-channel structural signal. Mitigation: detrend by partial rank conditional on bucketed step-index quantile; if even that fails, the candidate is dead on monotone-progress envs.
   - Stochastic transitions (Resource Gathering): channel firings move in time across rollouts of the same (s, a) by random transitions, blurring rank-percentile means; matches the predicted-and-observed failure mode of CWAI and DPC on RG.
   - Coarse state-cluster (observation-hash collisions): if cluster granularity is too fine, R cells are singletons and Pareto comparison is unreliable; if too coarse, rank averaging mushes across genuinely different states.
9. **Side-information channel:** vector diagnostics (the per-step channel vector v_t is required and is the substrate's `info["vector"]`); transition geometry (only a coarse observation-hash for clustering, no learned dynamics, no event lens).
10. **Monotonic improvement claim:** Assume stationary clusters and a fixed channel-trend sign vector σ. Define Φ(π) = Σ_s d_π(s) · max_a (number of channels m on which action a is non-dominated at cluster s under R̃, weighted by σ_m sign-frequency). Then a fixed-α logit nudge toward Pareto-non-dominated actions monotonically increases Φ in expectation under the discrete-update / small-step approximation, because each accepted nudge moves probability mass onto an action whose trend-corrected rank vector is non-dominated and the trend vector itself is held constant within a window. This is a much weaker claim than Bellman optimality and is explicitly local; the falsifier (b) checks whether the local improvement transfers to panel score.

## Why it is not GVFs / successor features (the named disqualifier)

GVFs and successor features compute `ψ(s, a) = E[Σ_t γ^t φ(s_t)]` — a discounted *magnitude integral* of future cumulant firings. CRP's primitive is the *within-trajectory rank-percentile* of the firing step, which is unitless, magnitude-invariant, and not an integral of anything. Two policies that yield identical successor features (same expected discounted channel mass) can have CRP tensors that disagree on every cell — for example, policy A fires channel m at step 5 of a 10-step episode (rank percentile 0.5) and policy B fires the same channel mass at step 9 (rank percentile 0.9); GVF/SF cannot distinguish them, CRP separates them. Likewise, scaling channel m by 100x leaves CRP unchanged but multiplies the SF cell by 100. Under variable renaming this distinction holds: CRP cannot be expressed as `E[Σ γ^t φ(s_t)]` for any feature map φ because rank is non-additive across trajectories — rank requires the full per-trajectory firing set, not a discounted sum.

## Why it scales beyond the substrate

At a 20k-action horizon, magnitude-integrals like return-to-go and successor features become essentially zero noise under terminal-only reward (the Q≈0-everywhere failure mode). Rank-percentile, by contrast, is *defined per channel's own firing set* — it is independent of how often the channel fires and how sparse the reward is. A channel that fires once at step 19,800 of a 20,000-step episode has rank percentile 1.0 at that step regardless of horizon length; nothing in the primitive grows or shrinks with horizon. For "generate a paragraph" actions, state-clusters become learned embeddings of the prompt context (any frozen pretrained encoder works) and ranks are over channels of vector feedback (success / safety / latency / preference / correctness / cost); the operator says "prefer paragraph-actions whose feedback ranks early-firing on positive-trend channels and late-firing on negative-trend channels among this prompt cluster's history." Rank is a natural unit when channels are heterogeneously scaled (latency in seconds, validity in {0,1}, preference in [-1,1]) — the magnitude-invariance is structural, not an engineering hack — exactly the regime where fixed scalar weights blow up. The Pareto comparison law inherits the same multi-channel respect at any k.
