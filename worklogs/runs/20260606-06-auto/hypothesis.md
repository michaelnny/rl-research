# 20260606-06-auto — CWAI: Channel-Wise Action Influence

## Research Gate

primitive: per-(state, action) channel-decomposed Jacobian of a self-supervised next-vector-observation predictor with respect to the action input
improvement_operator: at each state, nudge policy logits toward actions whose channel-Jacobian column-norm vector is Pareto-non-dominated by other actions' channel-Jacobian column-norm vectors at the same state
side_information: learned dynamics (a self-supervised forward model trained jointly on every transition; no reward in the model's loss)
nearest_prior_or_disqualifier: RND / curiosity (named disqualifier) and empowerment / mutual-information exploration (adjacent literature, not in our prior_attempts list but tonally near)
falsifier: on the substrate vector envs (Deep Sea Treasure, Resource Gathering), if the per-channel Jacobian column-norm vector has rank-1 structure across actions (all actions' Jacobian profiles collinear up to scale) for >70% of decision steps, the Pareto gate degenerates to scalar-norm argmax and CWAI is a curiosity rebadge — kill the family. Equivalently: if forward-model loss curves are flat (model not learning) within the 120 s budget, the Jacobian is noise and the operator does nothing.

## Mechanism

A small forward model `f_θ: (o_t, a_t) → ô_{t+1}` is trained online with mean-squared error on the **vector observation** (the per-channel observation, not reward). At each decision time, given current observation `o_t`, we compute for each candidate action `a` the column-wise Jacobian `J(a) = ∂ f_θ(o_t, a) / ∂ e(a)` where `e(a)` is the action's embedding. We aggregate this into a **per-channel influence vector** `g_a ∈ ℝ^k` whose `m`-th component is `‖J(a)[m, :]‖_2` — the Frobenius norm of the rows of the Jacobian corresponding to channel `m` of the next observation. The single primitive is the matrix `G ∈ ℝ^{|A| × k}` of per-(action, channel) influence at this state. The improvement operator nudges policy logits `π_φ(o_t)` at this state toward the **Pareto-non-dominated rows** of `G`: action `a` receives positive logit weight `+α · (rows-dominated-by-a − rows-dominating-a)` where dominance is coordinate-wise over channels and ties are broken by total norm. No reward enters the primitive or the operator. The forward model trains on every transition; the policy updates whenever the Pareto count is non-zero. There are no buckets, no observation hashes, no trajectory pairing, no replay-elite cloning, no return-to-go.

## Required candidate shape

1. **Experience object:** ordinary per-step transitions `(o_t, a_t, o_{t+1})` and the vector-feedback channel readouts `v_t ∈ ℝ^k` exposed by `info["vector"]` (treated as additional observation channels appended to `o_{t+1}`, **not** as reward). Trajectory IDs, returns, terminal events are not used by the primitive.

2. **Core primitive:** the per-(state, action, channel) **influence matrix** `G(o_t)[a, m] = ‖∂ f_θ(o_t, a)[m] / ∂ e(a)‖_2` derived from a continually-trained next-vector-observation predictor. This is one mathematical object: a Jacobian-norm matrix at the operating point.

3. **Improvement operator:** for each state visited, compute `G(o_t)`, find the Pareto-non-dominated subset `P ⊂ A` over rows of `G`, and apply policy logit update `Δ ℓ_a = α · (D⁺(a) − D⁻(a))` where `D⁺(a) = |{a' : G[a, :] ≻ G[a', :]}|` and `D⁻(a)` is the reverse count. The forward model itself is updated by ordinary supervised regression on observed transitions — that is a *component*, not the improvement primitive.

4. **Execution rule:** sample actions from `softmax(π_φ(o_t))` during data collection; at evaluation, sample temperature is annealed but never argmax (the channel-influence frontier is not a value function and argmax over it is type-incoherent). Actions with `G[a, :] = 0` (Jacobian dead-channel for this state) are deprioritized to avoid trivial loops.

5. **Vector feedback rule:** vector channels enter as **predicted output dimensions** of the forward model. Each channel keeps its own Jacobian column-norm; channels never collapse to a scalar. The Pareto rule is over channels coordinate-wise, never over a weighted sum. If a channel is terminal-only (e.g., DST treasure), its predicted next-step value is structurally near-zero except near the terminal step, and its Jacobian column-norm is correspondingly small until terminal-region experience exists — but the *other* channels (step-count, position) still carry information and the Pareto rule remains well-defined; CWAI does not require all channels to be active simultaneously.

6. **Rollout-cost discipline:** *exactly one* environment step per primitive update. The Jacobian computation is local (one autograd through the forward model at the current state, batched over `|A|` candidate actions). No counterfactual rollouts, no simulator branching, no verifier calls. Policy update happens online; forward-model update happens online; both per step. No replay-multiplier on the rollout count.

7. **Nearest-neighbor novelty audit:** the closest prior families are (a) **RND / curiosity** — disqualified family — and (b) **empowerment** (literature). RND uses *prediction error magnitude* as a scalar novelty bonus and is a count-based exploration rebadge under variable renaming. CWAI does not use prediction error; it uses the **input-Jacobian column norm**, a structural property of the learned mapping at the current operating point that is non-zero even when prediction error is zero (a perfectly-fit model can still have action-sensitive Jacobian). Empowerment integrates mutual information `I(A_{t:t+k}; S_{t+k})` over action sequences and requires sampling-based MI estimators; CWAI is a one-step local Jacobian with no MI estimator, no integration, no action-sequence rollout. The closest prior in our `prior_attempts.md` is **#14 Primal Behavior Flow Pivot**, which pivoted to occupancy `μ_π` without exposing new side information — CWAI exposes new side information (the Jacobian of a forward model) that is invisible to value, flow, advantage, and return-to-go centers.

8. **Predicted failure modes:**
   - **Forward-model under-training:** if the model's parameters have not yet converged on the visited subset, the Jacobian is gradient noise and the Pareto rule is a coin flip. Falsifiable by logging forward-model train-loss; if loss is flat, CWAI is operating on noise.
   - **Channel-collinearity collapse:** if all channels are scalar multiples of one (e.g., one channel is just step count, another is reward-per-step in a 1D bandit-ish env), the Jacobian column norms are collinear across channels and the Pareto rule degenerates to scalar-norm argmax. CWAI then reduces to "pick the action the model is most sensitive to" — a curiosity rebadge.
   - **Stochastic-transition collapse:** with non-trivial transition noise, the model fits the conditional mean and the input-Jacobian magnitude shrinks toward the noise floor. CWAI is predicted to weaken on RG (which has stochastic transitions).
   - **Action-embedding flat:** if action one-hots feed directly into the model without a learned embedding (so `e(a)` is the standard basis), the Jacobian column for `a` is just the model's `a`-input column gradient — fine in principle, but in practice all column norms tend to be of similar magnitude until the model differentiates actions. Mitigated by training a small action embedding alongside the model.
   - **Long-horizon credit:** CWAI is a one-step primitive. If the channel that carries terminal reward only changes on the very last step of the episode, the Jacobian on that channel is zero everywhere except near termination. The argument is that *other observation channels* (position, key-carried, door-open) carry intermediate informational structure and CWAI shapes policy on those channels alone until terminal experience accumulates. If the substrate's vector envs do not expose any non-terminal informative channels, CWAI is silent — falsifiable.

9. **Side-information channel:** **learned dynamics** (an explicit forward model is maintained as a component, but its role is to expose the input-Jacobian as a primitive — not to plan, not to roll out). Secondarily, **vector diagnostics** (per-channel observation readouts via `info["vector"]` enter the model's output, never the reward).

10. **Monotonic improvement claim:** under the assumption that the forward model `f_θ` is well-fit on the visited region and the action embeddings differentiate actions, the CWAI logit update monotonically increases the *expected channel-influence frontier coverage* of the policy — i.e., the expected per-channel maximum Jacobian column-norm over actions sampled from `π_φ`, integrated over visited states. This is a Pareto-improvement on the per-channel frontier of action sensitivity, not on reward; the bridge to reward is that channels are real environment readouts so frontier-covering actions probabilistically reach informative regions of state space faster than uniform-random.

## Why it is not RND / curiosity / empowerment

RND and curiosity-by-prediction-error reduce, under variable renaming, to count-based exploration: visit-bonus proportional to model error; CWAI does not use error at all and a perfectly-fit model still produces a non-trivial influence matrix `G`. Empowerment requires an MI estimator over action-sequence ↔ state-sequence pairs and is a multi-step integrated quantity; CWAI is a single autograd column-norm at the current state, requires no sampling, no estimator, no integration. The structural distinction holds under variable renaming: replace "Jacobian column norm" with any monotone function of the local linearization of the forward map, and the primitive is still distinct from "novelty" (a scalar function of error) and from "MI" (an integral over action distributions).

## Why it scales beyond the substrate

At 20k-action horizon, CWAI's per-step cost is one forward-model Jacobian — `O(|A| × dim(observation))` with vmap; this is the same order as a one-step value backup and far cheaper than any rollout-based planner. For "generate a paragraph" action spaces, the action embedding `e(a)` is the policy's own continuous token-embedding (which all LLM-based policies have); the Jacobian column is `∂ f_θ(o_t, e(a)) / ∂ e(a)`, well-defined for any continuous embedding. The channel-decomposition extends naturally: vector feedback at LLM-tool-use scale is `(success, cost, safety, latency, validity, correctness)` — the forward model predicts *each of those channels' next value* and the Jacobian is computed per channel. The Pareto-non-dominance rule scales linearly in `k` (the number of channels) and is cheap regardless of `|A|` (we only ever evaluate it on the candidate-action set the policy is willing to consider — sampled if `|A|` is enormous). The primitive does not depend on hash collisions, observation revisits, or trajectory pairings — failure modes that have killed every prior FED-family candidate at long horizon — because each (state, action) is scored *independently* against the forward model's local linearization.
