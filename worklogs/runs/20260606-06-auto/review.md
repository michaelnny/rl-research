## Reasoning

CWAI's central primitive — the per-(action, channel) Jacobian column-norm matrix `G(o_t)[a,m] = ‖∂f_θ(o_t,a)[m]/∂e(a)‖_2` of a jointly-trained forward model — is structurally distinct from every disqualifier family and from all 21 prior attempts. The key identity check that matters: RND/curiosity use *prediction error magnitude* as a scalar novelty signal; CWAI uses the *input-Jacobian* of the forward model, a local linearization property that is nonzero even when prediction error is zero (a perfectly-fit model can still respond differentially across actions and channels). That is not a renaming of novelty — it is a different mathematical quantity computed from the same forward model. The improvement operator (Pareto-non-dominance count over rows of `G`) is also not a scalarization: channels never collapse to a weighted sum, the dominance rule is coordinate-wise, and the falsifier explicitly checks for rank-1 collapse. The sprint-4 bootstrap-wall failure mode (#15–21) does not apply in the same form because CWAI fires per-step once the forward model has any non-trivial gradient, rather than requiring observation-hash bucket collisions to accumulate before the operator can fire. All required candidate-shape slots are filled with substantive answers: primitive, improvement operator, side-information channel (learned dynamics + vector diagnostics), rollout-cost accounting (exactly one env step per primitive update, no replay multiplier), monotonic improvement claim, and five named predicted failure modes each with a stated falsifier.

The structural distinction from nearest prior attempt #14 (Primal Behavior Flow Pivot) is also sound: #14 pivoted from value to occupancy flow without exposing new side information; CWAI exposes new side information (the Jacobian of a forward model at each operating point) that is invisible to value, flow, and return-to-go centers. The mechanism is one primitive and one operator, not a component stack. The only open empirical question — whether early-training Jacobian noise renders the Pareto rule a coin-flip before the forward model converges — is acknowledged as the primary predicted failure mode with a concrete falsifier (monitor forward-model train-loss; if flat, kill the family).

## Risks the Engineer should be aware of

- The forward model trains on `(o_t, a_t) → o_{t+1}` and the vector-channel readouts from `info["vector"]` are appended to `o_{t+1}` as additional prediction targets. Confirm that `info["vector"]` is available at every step on all five panel envs before the panel run, and that those channels are not accidentally used as reward (the hypothesis says they enter only as predicted output dimensions, never as reward — the Engineer must enforce this).
- On Deep Sea Treasure, the treasure channel is terminal-only: the Jacobian for that channel is near-zero everywhere except near termination. The hypothesis's argument that other channels (position, step-count) carry non-terminal structure is plausible but untested — if the substrate's vector envs expose only one non-trivially-active channel until termination, the Pareto frontier degenerates. The Engineer should log per-channel Jacobian column-norm distributions to verify the multi-channel structure is real.
- The `|A|`-batched Jacobian computation via `vmap` is the hot path. On MiniGrid's small discrete action sets this is cheap, but the Engineer should profile it early to confirm it stays within the 120 s/env budget.
