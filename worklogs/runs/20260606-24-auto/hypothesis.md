# 20260606-24-auto — IXC: Inter-Channel Cross-Lag Action Signature

## Research Gate

primitive: per-action 3-tensor `Λ[a, m, m', ℓ]` of empirical cross-channel firing-coincidence rates at lag `ℓ` (positive and negative), computed across the replay buffer with NO observation hash, NO state cluster, and NO trajectory pairing
improvement_operator: Pareto-non-dominance vote over actions on the channel-pair-coupling-margin vector `μ[a, m, m'] = max_{ℓ>0} Λ[a, m, m', ℓ] − max_{ℓ<0} Λ[a, m, m', ℓ]` flattened over (m, m') pairs, with lexicographic dimension-compression: keep only the top-`B` (m, m') pairs by buffer-wide variance to avoid Pareto-front saturation
side_information: vector diagnostics, transition geometry
nearest_prior_or_disqualifier: GVFs / successor features (disqualifier family) and #14 Primal Behavior Flow Pivot (sprint 3); also ACS (#25) for the dimension-saturation lesson
falsifier: on at least one panel env (DST, RG, DoorKey-8x8, KeyCorridor) the candidate beats the strong baseline; otherwise, log per-(action, channel-pair) variance histogram and Pareto-vote firing rate to determine whether the cross-lag tensor degenerated to a single dominant pair (collapse to univariate-coupling case) or saturated the Pareto front (too many dimensions)

## Mechanism

For each transition (o_t, a_t, v_t, ...) in the replay buffer, accumulate into a per-action 3-tensor `Λ[a, m, m', ℓ]` the indicator-product `1[v_t[m] > 0] · 1[v_{t+ℓ}[m'] > 0]` for `ℓ ∈ {±1, ±2, ±4, ±8}` (log-spaced bilateral lags), summed over all timesteps where `a_t = a` and the lag is in-bounds. After normalization by the per-action timestep count, this is the empirical probability that channel `m'` fires at offset `ℓ` from a step where action `a` was taken AND channel `m` fired. The **coupling-margin tensor** is `μ[a, m, m'] = max_{ℓ>0} Λ[a, m, m', ℓ] − max_{ℓ<0} Λ[a, m, m', ℓ]` — positive when `a` is followed by m'-after-m (forward asymmetry), negative when m'-before-m. The improvement operator selects the top-`B = 8` channel-pairs (m, m') by buffer-wide variance of `μ[·, m, m']` (so action-discriminating pairs only — pairs whose coupling sign is the same for all actions are filtered), then applies a Pareto-non-dominance logit nudge: at decision time, for each candidate action `a` count `n_a − m_a` where `n_a` is the number of OTHER actions whose `μ[a', :]` row is coordinate-wise dominated by `μ[a, :]` and `m_a` is the converse count. Logit nudge is `α · (n_a − m_a)` with α a small constant. No state index, no Bellman backup, no scalar reward weighting, no critic, no return-to-go. The primitive is the asymmetric cross-channel coupling under action conditioning; the improvement is a Pareto vote on coupling sign.

## Required candidate shape

1. **Experience object:** ordinary on-policy replay buffer of (o_t, a_t, v_t, done_t) transitions with vector channel signals from `info["vector"]`. No hashing, no clustering, no pair-extraction, no hindsight goal relabeling.
2. **Core primitive:** per-action 3-tensor `Λ[a, m, m', ℓ]` of empirical cross-channel firing-coincidence rates at log-spaced bilateral lags, normalized by per-action timestep count. The coupling-margin tensor `μ[a, m, m'] = max_{ℓ>0} Λ − max_{ℓ<0} Λ` summarizes forward-asymmetry of channel-pair coupling under action conditioning.
3. **Improvement operator:** filter to the top-B channel-pairs by buffer-wide variance of `μ[·, m, m']` (B=8) to keep only action-discriminating dimensions; at each decision step compute logit nudge `α · (n_a − m_a)` where Pareto-non-dominance is computed on the B-dim filtered `μ[a]` row across actions.
4. **Execution rule:** sample actions from `softmax(z_a + α · (n_a − m_a))` where `z_a` are policy logits from a small MLP trained via... (a) a self-supervised next-step cumulant prediction head (BCE on each `1[v_{t+1}[m] > 0]`), so the trunk learns task-relevant features without any reward optimization, plus (b) a low-amplitude logit-anchor (`L2` to a uniform target) to prevent action collapse before the Pareto vote populates. NO PG update. NO Q learning. The trunk's role is feature extraction only.
5. **Vector feedback rule:** `info["vector"]` enters the primitive as binary firing indicators per channel; channels are NEVER summed, weighted, or scalarized. Pareto-non-dominance on the B-dim filtered coupling-margin vector handles the multi-channel structure. The variance-filter (top-B) prevents Pareto-front saturation when k(k-1) channel-pairs is large.
6. **Rollout-cost discipline:** one env interaction per step. The Pareto vote is computed online from the buffer, no counterfactual rollouts, no verifier calls, no model unrolls at decision time. Buffer is bounded (FIFO, e.g. last 8 episodes for the lag tensor — small to keep memory manageable since `Λ` is `|A| × k × k × 2L` floats, with L=4 lags ≈ a few thousand floats total).
7. **Nearest-neighbor novelty audit:** closest prior is GVFs/successor features (disqualifier) and Primal Behavior Flow Pivot (#14). GVFs predict per-channel expected cumulants; successor features predict expected discounted cumulant-trajectories; both are *expectations* of magnitude. IXC's primitive is the *cross-lag asymmetry of firing coincidences* — an antisymmetric statistic in lag. It is NOT a cumulant prediction or a magnitude expectation: it can be non-zero even when both channels fire with equal expected count under action `a`, as long as their *temporal ordering* differs. The variance-filter further prunes pairs that don't action-discriminate, so the operator is non-equivalent to any fixed-weight `wᵀr` scalarization.
8. **Predicted failure modes:** (a) on terminal-only channels (DST treasure, RG resources/goal) the cross-lag coincidence between distinct channels may be near-zero throughout the episode, leaving only the step-penalty self-coupling `Λ[a, sp, sp, ℓ]` populated — which is a function of episode length under action `a` (i.e., shortest-path-to-terminal preference, the universal step-penalty failure mode); (b) the variance filter may keep only the step-penalty self-coupling pair if all other pairs are silent, collapsing to single-pair Pareto vote (univariate scalar) — `falsifier triggered`; (c) on RG, stochastic transitions inject lag-noise into the cross-lag coincidence rates, but because we use `max_{ℓ>0}` rather than a specific lag, the operator should be robust to small lag-jitter (test prediction); (d) early in training, the buffer is small and `Λ[a]` is high-variance, causing Pareto vote churn — mitigated by the logit-anchor in (4); (e) Pareto-front saturation in B=8 dims is possible — variance-filter is the front-compression mechanism (lesson from ACS #25 collapse).
9. **Side-information channel:** vector diagnostics (env's `info["vector"]` is consumed as the per-step k-vector firing pattern); transition geometry (lag structure within the episode timestep sequence). Both are explicitly enumerated in the substrate; no hand-engineered event lens, no demonstrations, no language description, no learned dynamics model.
10. **Monotonic improvement claim:** *under the assumption that channel-pair forward-coupling sign is a noisy ordinal predictor of long-horizon channel-firing causal direction*, the Pareto vote on `μ[a]` rows strictly increases the policy's mass on actions that produce *forward-causal channel-pair structure non-dominated by alternatives*, and this property is monotonically preserved as buffer mass grows (because empirical coupling rates concentrate around their population values by Hoeffding). Specifically, in expectation over buffer realizations, the Pareto-non-dominance count `n_a − m_a` for any action `a` whose population-level `μ` row is strictly Pareto-dominant converges to `|A| − 1`, so the logit nudge for that action grows monotonically with buffer mass. The proxy improved is **the fraction of actions whose action-conditioned forward-cross-coupling row is non-dominated**, which is a measurable diagnostic at every training step.

## Why it is not GVFs / successor features (or Primal Behavior Flow #14)

GVFs and successor features predict **per-channel expected (discounted) cumulants** — magnitude expectations conditioned on state and policy. Their action-improvement step is a scalar argmax (or weighted) projection: `argmax_a wᵀ ψ(s, a)` for a fixed or learned `w`. IXC's primitive is the **lag-asymmetric coincidence rate** between distinct channels under action conditioning — an antisymmetric tensor in lag, not an expectation of magnitude. A GVF that predicted `E[v[m] | s, a]` would be invariant under reordering of channel-firing times within the episode; IXC depends precisely on that ordering and is non-zero when channels fire with equal expected count but in different orders. Primal Behavior Flow (#14) operates on the policy's occupancy `μ_π(s, a)` recovered by normalization — a univariate-per-(state,action) statistic, not a channel-pair coupling. IXC's coupling-margin is irreducible to either object under variable renaming because no scalar function of `E[v[m] | s, a]` yields the antisymmetric `max_{ℓ>0} − max_{ℓ<0}` quantity.

## Why it scales beyond the substrate

When the horizon grows to 20k actions and the action space is "generate a paragraph" or "call a tool", the per-action 3-tensor `Λ[a, m, m', ℓ]` does not grow with horizon — it only grows with `|A|`, `k`, `k`, and the number of log-spaced lags `2L`. For a paragraph-generation policy with discrete tokens, the primitive aggregates token-action statistics over the episode; for a tool-call policy with structured action spaces, the primitive operates on an action-cluster index (cluster by tool name or LLM-embedding) — the cross-channel coupling under action-cluster conditioning. Vector feedback (success, cost, safety, latency, energy, validity, correctness, preference) at length 6 produces a 36-pair channel-pair tensor; the variance filter (top-B) prunes this to 8 informative pairs without scalarization. Long horizon helps the primitive because more steps per episode means more lag-coincidence samples per action, and the log-spaced lag scheme `{±1, ±2, ±4, ±8}` extends naturally to `{±1, ±2, ±4, ±8, ±16, ±64, ±256, ±1024}` for 20k-step horizons — the operator's compute is `O(|A| · k² · L · buffer_size)` per update with buffer_size bounded by recent episodes, which is decoupled from horizon length.
