## Reasoning

IXC's central primitive is the lag-asymmetric cross-channel firing-coincidence tensor `Λ[a, m, m', ℓ]`, with the improvement operator defined as Pareto-non-dominance on the coupling-margin `μ[a, m, m'] = max_{ℓ>0} Λ − max_{ℓ<0} Λ`. This is structurally the same family as PICAV (#16) — "signed cross-channel temporal-ordering moment." PICAV used `v_t[j]·Δn_t[k] − v_t[k]·Δn_t[j]` (antisymmetric cross-channel instantaneous contribution); IXC uses `max_{ℓ>0} Λ − max_{ℓ<0} Λ` (antisymmetric cross-channel lag coincidence rate). Both are antisymmetric statistics that capture whether channel `m` tends to precede or follow channel `m'` under action `a`. PICAV's ruling states explicitly: "on Deep Sea Treasure the treasure channel fires only at the terminal step, making all pair entries zero throughout the episode (same bootstrap collapse as FED)." IXC's own failure mode (a) acknowledges exactly this: "on terminal-only channels the cross-lag coincidence between distinct channels may be near-zero throughout the episode," and failure mode (b) predicts the variance filter then retains only the step-penalty self-coupling, collapsing the Pareto vote to a scalar. The difference between IXC and PICAV — log-spaced bilateral lags with `max` over positive lags, plus variance-filtered top-B pair selection — does not change the structural identity: when any vector channel fires only at the terminal step, the cross-channel entries involving that channel are zero for all lag values `ℓ`, and the improvement operator silently degrades to scalar step-penalty optimization. This is a rebadge of the "signed cross-channel temporal-ordering moment" family ruled out by PICAV (#16).

The hypothesis also does not escape the GVFs/successor features disqualifier at the structural level. IXC's improvement operator selects actions by their action-conditioned channel-pair coupling profile — which is equivalent, under variable renaming, to a multi-channel cumulant statistic (coincidence rate is a second-order cumulant of the binary firing process). The claimed distinction — that IXC is antisymmetric in lag while GVFs are expectations of magnitude — is a real formal difference, but does not yield a structurally new family if the antisymmetric statistic collapses to zero on the substrate envs (as PICAV demonstrated and the hypothesis predicts). A primitive that is formally distinct but empirically silent on the target envs for the same structural reason as a prior failed family remains a rebadge of that family's failure mode, not a novel direction.

## Risks the Engineer should be aware of

- The hypothesis's own failure modes (a) and (b) predict exactly the PICAV bootstrap collapse on DST and RG; running the panel would confirm this ruling rather than test a genuinely novel mechanism.
- The self-supervised next-step cumulant prediction head (execution rule slot 4) introduces a training signal via BCE on `1[v_{t+1}[m] > 0]` — but for terminal-only channels this BCE target is zero on every non-terminal step, so the trunk learns nothing about those channels until the first rewarded trajectory. This does not resolve the bootstrap wall.
- The logit-anchor (`L2` to uniform) is listed as a bootstrap mitigation, but it only prevents action collapse — it does not provide any information about which actions lead to reward, and its presence does not change the structural verdict.
