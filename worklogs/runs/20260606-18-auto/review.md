## Reasoning

PFA's central primitive is the signed 2-D cross-product `A_m = p_m(o_t)·q_m(o_{t+1}) − p_m(o_{t+1})·q_m(o_t)`, where `p_m` and `q_m` are learned short- and long-horizon firing-probability heads per channel. This is genuinely bilinear — it cannot be expressed as a difference of any scalar function of a single observation (ruling out CID-canonical as a renaming) and it is not a discounted cumulant at any horizon (ruling out GVF/successor features: GVFs accumulate `E[Σ γ^τ φ(s_{t+τ})]` and extract policy improvement via linear reward prediction; PFA's heads output marginal firing probabilities, and the operator consumes only the signed area on the observed transition, never the head's expected-value output at decision time). The improvement operator is a Pareto-non-dominance logit nudge over the k-dimensional signed-area row per action under coordinate-wise partial order, with no scalar collapse at any point. This is structurally distinct from the entire Sprint 4 family (FED #15, PICAV #16, CHX #17, CEC #18, CWTP #19, LRA #20, TPP #21, CRP #22, TCP #23, PCR #24, ACS #25, TRAC #26, PCGA #27, ARP #28) and from all named disqualifiers. The bootstrap-wall risk is partially mitigated: unlike FED/CEC/TPP/ARP, PFA's heads receive supervision from the step-penalty channel on every step (it fires at every transition), so `p_m` and `q_m` can develop non-trivial horizon separation before any rewarded trajectory appears — the heads do not need a rewarded trajectory to hold non-trivial values. The hypothesis declares this explicitly as falsifiable failure mode (c). All required candidate-shape slots have substantive answers: primitive, improvement operator, side-information channel, monotonic improvement claim (behavioral Pareto-front concentration with honest caveat that return monotonicity is empirical), and five concrete predicted failure modes with testable diagnostic metrics.

The structural distinction claim ("rotational vs translational quantity") holds under scrutiny: the cross-product form couples the two horizons multiplicatively, so it genuinely measures curvature of the trajectory in imminence space rather than translation, and this is not reachable by any scalar renaming of CID-canonical's log-likelihood ratio. The k-vector Pareto logit nudge does not reduce to scalar channel optimization at any point (contrast with TCP #23 which collapsed to singleton-channel scalar maximization on terminal-only substrates). The hypothesis's own failure-mode taxonomy is the most thorough of any Sprint 4 candidate reviewed, suggesting the researcher has internalized the prior failure patterns.

## Risks the Engineer should be aware of

- The cluster-collision bottleneck (TRAC #26 failure mode) applies to the `Ā[c, a, m]` table: the heads can train on every transition, but the Pareto vote over `Ā` requires (cluster, action) revisitation to accumulate stable estimates. On DoorKey-8x8 and KeyCorridor with partially observable changing state, cluster collisions under uniform exploration may remain sparse within 120 s — verify that `Ā` cell counts exceed the hypothesis's implied threshold before trusting the Pareto vote.
- The step-penalty channel is argued to rescue the bootstrap problem because it fires on every step. On Resource Gathering, however, there are multiple always-firing channels (step penalties in both penalty dimensions), and the stochastic transitions may cause `p_m ≈ q_m` for those channels (failure mode b) — the signed area may be near-zero despite non-trivial channel activity, leaving only the terminal reward channel with the same bootstrap collapse as the FED family. Log `Var(p_m − q_m)` per channel early in training to verify the two horizons have actually separated.
- The fixed-radius hash cluster assignment `c(o)` depends on policy-trunk activations that shift during training. As the policy improves, cluster identities change, potentially invalidating accumulated `Ā` entries from early exploration. Consider whether a staleness-weighted decay on `Ā` cells is needed, or whether the running mean is robust to this drift.
