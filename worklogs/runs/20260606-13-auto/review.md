## Reasoning

TRAC's central primitive is `R[c,a,m] = JSD(H_fire[c,a,m] || H_nofire[c,a,m])` — a Jensen-Shannon divergence between two empirical successor-cluster distributions conditioned on whether a vector-feedback channel fired in a post-action window. This is structurally distinct from every disqualifier family. Successor features / GVFs predict an expected discounted cumulant vector under the policy and extract behavior by inner product with a weight vector; TRAC computes a divergence between two empirical conditional distributions over discrete future-state clusters partitioned by a Bernoulli firing event — there is no discount, no TD-bootstrap, no cumulant expectation, and no weight-vector extraction. Against FED/CEC/TPP/PCR (sprint-4 bootstrap-wall family), those primitives were gated on reward correlation (terminal outcome hashes, cross-trajectory hash collisions, terminal sign gates); TRAC's JSD fires on *any* channel firing within the window, including step-penalty channels that fire on every step, meaning H_fire and H_nofire accumulate non-empty support from trajectory 1 without waiting for a rewarded trajectory. Against CWAI (alive-promising), CWAI's primitive is the column-norm of a learned forward-model Jacobian — a local differential of predicted observation in action-embedding space; TRAC's primitive is an empirical integral (JSD over two histogram distributions) over realized transitions — these cannot be reduced to each other under variable renaming. The improvement operator (Pareto-non-dominance logit nudge over the k-dimensional JSD row) does not collapse to scalar-weighted log-prob, does not use a critic or advantage, and does not scalarize vector feedback. All required-candidate-shape slots are substantively filled: primitive with precise formula, improvement operator with explicit update rule, side-information channel declared as {vector diagnostics, transition geometry}, concrete predicted failure modes tied to the mechanism's structural constraints, and a monotonic improvement claim stated in the infinite-sample limit.

The hypothesis is honest about the bootstrap risk it shares with prior sprint-4 attempts: if fewer than 20% of (c,a,m) cells accumulate sufficient sample mass within the 120 s budget, the operator is silent. However, the structural argument for why this should be less severe than FED/CEC is plausible: the firing event is a within-window Bernoulli over any channel, not a cross-trajectory terminal event, so even channels like the step-penalty that fire densely will seed H_fire/H_nofire from early exploration. Whether that is sufficient on the panel's sparse envs (DoorKey, KeyCorridor) where the navigation structure may not produce enough (cluster, action) collisions within budget is an empirical question the falsifier correctly anticipates.

## Risks the Engineer should be aware of

- The "channel fired in window [t+1, t+W]" partition requires buffering W steps of future observations per active (c,a) cell before the histogram entry is finalized; the Engineer must implement this correctly as a rolling buffer rather than an immediate update, or the JSD will be computed on incomplete entries.
- On Resource Gathering and Deep Sea Treasure, the reward channel fires only at termination, so H_fire for that channel will be empty until the first successful trajectory — the hypothesis predicts this and accepts it; however, the Pareto-non-dominance count still operates on the step-penalty channel's non-degenerate JSD from early on, so the operator is not fully silent as it was for FED/CEC. The Engineer should log per-channel cell fill rates to verify this prediction holds.
- The cluster count C is a free parameter. Too coarse fuses distinct states; too fine leaves every cell a singleton. The hypothesis does not specify a default; the Engineer should choose a value empirically on the simplest panel env first (DoorKey-5x5) and document the sensitivity, since the falsifier for cluster granularity is stated but no default is given.
