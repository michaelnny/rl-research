# 20260606-19-auto — CPR (Channel-Posterior Chebyshev Reweight)

## Research Gate

primitive: per-channel empirical replay-reweighted action posterior `π̂_m(a|o)`, one such posterior per vector-feedback channel `m ∈ {1..k}`.
improvement_operator: minimize the **max-over-channels** KL divergence `sup_m KL(π̂_m(·|o) || π_θ(·|o))` — a Chebyshev (worst-channel) projection of the policy onto the joint constraint set defined by all channel posteriors.
side_information: vector diagnostics (per-step `Δc[m]` increments, used as importance weights for k separate empirical posteriors — not as reward).
nearest_prior_or_disqualifier: scalar-weighted log-prob update (PPO/REINFORCE family); also adjacent to GVFs/successor features.
falsifier: if the panel run shows that on Deep Sea Treasure or Resource Gathering one channel's KL term dominates `sup_m` for >95% of optimization steps (logged via per-channel KL trace), the operator has reduced to single-channel weighted cloning and CPR is a scalarization rebadge in disguise.

## Mechanism

For each vector-feedback channel `m`, define the channel-`m` reweighted empirical action distribution `π̂_m(a|o)` — the distribution you get by re-weighting replay transitions `(o_t, a_t)` with weight `w_m(t) = max(0, Δc_t[m] − μ_m)/σ_m` (channel-standardized positive part of the channel increment) and renormalizing per observation-cluster. This is a probability distribution over actions whose mass is concentrated on the actions that *empirically co-occur with channel `m` firing*. The primitive is the k-tuple `(π̂_1, ..., π̂_k)`. The improvement operator updates `θ` by minimizing `L(θ) = sup_{m: σ_m > 0} KL(π̂_m(·|o) || π_θ(·|o))` averaged over a batch of observations sampled from replay. Equivalently, at each step the gradient is computed only with respect to the *currently dominant* channel `m* = argmax_m KL(π̂_m || π_θ)` — a min-max projection. There is no scalar reward, no critic, no return computation, no fixed channel-weighting `w`, and no per-trajectory return ranking; the only objects are the k empirical action posteriors and the sup-norm distance from the policy to them.

## Required candidate shape

1. **Experience object:** standard replay buffer of `(o_t, a_t, v_{t+1})` transitions with per-step vector feedback `v_{t+1} ∈ R^k` (= `info["vector"]` on vector envs; one-hot reward channel + step-penalty on sparse envs). No goal labels, no event lens, no demonstrations.

2. **Core primitive:** the k-tuple of per-channel empirical action posteriors `π̂_m(a|o) ∝ Σ_{(o_t,a_t) ∈ replay, c(o_t)=c(o)} w_m(t) · 1[a_t = a]`, where `w_m(t) = ReLU((Δc_t[m] − μ_m)/σ_m)` is a channel-standardized positive increment and `c(·)` is a coarse observation-cluster (small hash or k-means on observation embeddings; `c` only buys cell density, not the primitive).

3. **Improvement operator:** Chebyshev (sup-norm) policy projection — at each gradient step, sample a batch of observations, identify per-observation `m*(o) = argmax_m KL(π̂_m(·|o) || π_θ(·|o))`, and take a gradient step on `KL(π̂_{m*}(·|o) || π_θ(·|o))` (or, in a smooth relaxation, on a softmax over m of the KL terms with temperature `β` annealed from soft to hard). This is *not* a weighted sum of channel objectives; the channel that wins the argmax provides 100% of the gradient at that step.

4. **Execution rule:** on-policy sampling from `π_θ(·|o_t)` with a small entropy bonus floor for exploration. No conditioning on a return-to-go scalar, no preference token, no goal embedding.

5. **Vector feedback rule:** each channel of `v_{t+1}` defines a *separate* empirical posterior; the channels are never combined by `wᵀv`. The aggregation across channels happens at the *operator* level (sup-norm) and is a discrete max over channel-conditioned KL terms — there is no fixed scalar weight on channels, learned or hand-set. A negative-channel (e.g. step-penalty as an unwanted signal) is handled by sign-flipping its `w_m` so the posterior concentrates on the *avoidance* actions; this sign assignment is a per-channel polarity flag, not a tunable weight.

6. **Rollout-cost discipline:** one env step per env step; no counterfactual rollouts; no simulator branching; no verifier calls. Per gradient update: one batch of replay transitions, k empirical posteriors recomputed lazily (running sums updated online; no full re-pass). Per accepted improvement: same as one gradient step. At deployment: one env step per action.

7. **Nearest-neighbor novelty audit:** the closest disqualifier is **scalar-weighted log-prob update (REINFORCE/PPO/GRPO)**, where the per-trajectory return weight is replaced by a per-step-per-channel weight `w_m(t)`. The structural distinction is the **sup-norm aggregation across channels**: REINFORCE/PPO collapse vector reward to `wᵀr` for fixed `w` — there exists no `w` such that `wᵀr`-policy-gradient is equivalent to `min_θ sup_m KL(π̂_m || π_θ)`, because the latter is a min-max projection onto a Chebyshev center of k empirical distributions, which is a non-linear functional of `(π̂_1, ..., π̂_k)` that cannot be expressed as a linear combination. Closest *alive* candidates are CWAI (Jacobian column-norm — a parameter-space gradient object) and DPC (sign-vote at first-divergence states) — CPR uses neither gradient norms nor pair divergences; it is a replay-reweighting primitive.

8. **Predicted failure modes:**
   - **Single-channel domination (own falsifier):** if one channel's KL dominates `sup_m` for >95% of steps, CPR collapses to single-channel weighted cloning. Most likely on terminal-only-reward envs early in training, where treasure-channel posterior is degenerate (single-singleton) until success occurs.
   - **Cluster collisions sparse:** if `c(o)` produces near-singleton clusters (every observation is its own bucket on long-horizon partial-obs envs), the empirical posteriors degenerate and KL computation is unstable.
   - **Stochastic transitions:** high-variance `Δc_t[m]` makes `π̂_m` noisy; the sup-norm amplifies the noisiest channel rather than the most informative one.
   - **Min-max instability:** the argmax-channel switches rapidly across gradient steps, causing oscillation; mitigated by softmax-temperature relaxation.
   - **Polarity ambiguity:** channels for which ReLU-ed positive increments and ReLU-ed negative increments both carry information (e.g. a channel that fires for both gain and loss) need both polarities tracked; the prototype assumes monotone polarity.

9. **Side-information channel:** **vector diagnostics** — exclusively. The per-step vector `v_{t+1}` is the only side information; observation cluster `c(o)` is a coverage device, not new side info. No event traces, no object state, no demonstrations, no learned dynamics, no language. The `v` channel comes from `info["vector"]` exactly as the substrate provides it; there is no hand-designed lens.

10. **Monotonic improvement claim:** under fixed `(π̂_1, ..., π̂_k)`, `sup_m KL(π̂_m || π_θ)` is convex in the policy logits (sup of convex KLs in log-policy space is convex), so projected gradient descent monotonically reduces the worst-channel KL in expectation under the batch distribution. Convergence drives `π_θ` to the **Chebyshev center** of the k empirical posteriors — the unique policy that is closest in worst case to every channel's empirical behavior. This is the formal monotonic-improvement claim: the worst-channel deviation from empirical behavior is non-increasing per gradient step, and the fixed point is the policy that tolerates no channel as an outlier. Crucially, this differs from `wᵀr`-maximization, which has no analogous worst-case guarantee.

## Why it is not scalar-weighted log-prob update (PPO/REINFORCE/GRPO)

REINFORCE/PPO/GRPO compute a per-trajectory or per-step *scalar* weight (the return, advantage, or shaped reward) and use it as the multiplier on `∇ log π(a|o)`. CPR computes k *separate* empirical action posteriors and minimizes the Chebyshev (sup-norm) distance from `π_θ` to all of them simultaneously — there is no scalar weight on actions, and there is no fixed combination of channels (the `argmax_m KL` switches per observation per step). Under variable renaming, REINFORCE on a fixed-weight vector reward `wᵀv` produces a single per-step scalar weight; CPR produces k separate posteriors and a min-max projection — these are functionally different objects (a Chebyshev center is not an expectation under any fixed `w`), so the structural distinction holds and is not a notational shift.

## Why it scales beyond the substrate

In the modern long-horizon setting, "actions" are paragraphs or tool calls, and channels are correctness, latency, safety, validity, cost, preference. CPR's primitive — per-channel reweighted action posterior — generalizes verbatim: for an LLM policy and a 6-channel vector feedback, you maintain six channel-conditional posteriors over generated paragraph IDs (or, more practically, six channel-conditional preference KLs over candidate continuations) and project the policy via sup-norm onto their Chebyshev center. The min-max aggregation explicitly refuses to trade off correctness for latency or vice versa — the formal property the long-horizon vector-feedback setting demands. Bootstrap at 20k action horizons remains an open problem (the `π̂_m` posteriors need *some* per-channel firing in replay to be non-degenerate), but the structural primitive does not depend on bootstrapping a value function over a 20k-step horizon — only on accumulating one-step `Δc_t` records, which fire densely on at least the cost/latency/validity channels regardless of horizon. This is the substrate-to-target story: dense channels carry the early signal, sparse channels engage as soon as they fire once, and the sup-norm aggregation does not collapse no matter how many channels are added.
