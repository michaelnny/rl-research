## Reasoning

CPR's central primitive — the k-tuple of per-channel reweighted empirical action posteriors `(π̂_1, ..., π̂_k)` — and its improvement operator — Chebyshev (sup-norm) projection minimizing `sup_m KL(π̂_m || π_θ)` via dynamic argmax selection of the worst-channel at each gradient step — are structurally distinct from every disqualifier family. The nearest disqualifier is scalar-weighted log-prob (REINFORCE/PPO/GRPO). Each CPR gradient step does produce a weighted `∇ log π` update, but the weight vector at each step is drawn from whichever single channel `m*` currently maximizes KL — this adversarial selection is not equivalent to `wᵀr` for any fixed or learned `w`, because no linear combination of channels can replicate a per-step argmax that switches `m*` across observations. The hypothesis makes the mathematical argument precisely: a Chebyshev center of k distributions is a non-linear functional of `(π̂_1, ..., π̂_k)` that has no `wᵀr` representation. None of the Sprint-4 failures are structural matches: FED/CEC/TPP/PCR/TRAC were all silent before reward fired; CPR's operator is active through the step-penalty channel immediately, with the treasure channel engaging once it fires (bootstrap acknowledged as the own falsifier). Nearest alive candidate CWAI uses parameter-space gradient norms; DPC uses pair-divergence at state forks — neither is CPR's replay-reweighting posterior construction.

All required candidate-shape slots are substantively filled: the primitive is the k-tuple of channel posteriors (not a stack of named components); the improvement operator is a min-max KL projection with a stated convexity-based monotonic improvement claim; the side-information channel is declared as vector diagnostics exclusively (`info["vector"]`, no hand-engineered events); five distinct predicted failure modes are given (single-channel domination, cluster collisions, stochastic-transition amplification, min-max oscillation, polarity ambiguity); the nearest prior or disqualifier is named with an explicit structural-distinction argument; and the rollout-cost discipline is accounted without counterfactual branching.

## Risks the Engineer should be aware of

- The step-penalty channel is structurally always-negative and monotone, so its `π̂_step` posterior will immediately concentrate on short-episode actions. If `m*` resolves to the step-penalty channel >95% of gradient steps on DoorKey/KeyCorridor (loggable via per-channel KL trace), the operator reduces to single-channel weighted cloning — the hypothesis's own falsifier. The Engineer should instrument this trace from the first run.
- The observation-cluster function `c(o)` (small hash or k-means) must be dense enough for empirical posteriors to be non-degenerate (slot 8b), but compact enough for revisitation on long-horizon partial-obs envs. The prototype choice of cluster granularity is the single most implementation-sensitive parameter and should be reported with the result.
- Polarity assignment (slot 8e) assumes each channel is monotone-positive or monotone-negative. The step-penalty channel has known negative polarity; if a channel carries both gain and loss signals (possible on Resource Gathering where the agent can gain or lose resource increments), sign-flipped `w_m` and standard `w_m` both capture only half the posterior mass. The Engineer should verify per-channel polarity before training begins.
