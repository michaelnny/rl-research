## Reasoning

BLIC's central primitive — the per-(cluster, action, channel) running empirical mean of `δ_m = q_m(o_{t+1}) − q_m(o_t)` computed on *actually-observed* replay transitions, with improvement operator as Pareto non-dominance *count* over the k-vector IC[s,a,:] — is structurally distinct from every disqualifier family and every prior attempt in `prior_attempts.md`. The GVF/successor-features disqualifier does not apply: GVFs learn an expected discounted cumulant and extract behavior by maximizing a linear combination; BLIC's learned objects (q_m classifiers) are components, and the operator uses only the sign-and-coordinate structure of dominance counts over the IC tensor, never maximizing any function of q_m's output magnitude or any weighted combination of channel values. ACCD (#34) is the nearest prior attempt, but ACCD computed a signed shift between two separately-trained marginal classifiers (action-conditional vs. unconditional); BLIC computes the shift on actually-observed next-observations from replay and aggregates by cluster-conditional mean — the action discrimination comes from which (s,a) cells accumulate transitions, not from counterfactual marginal comparison. The bootstrap-wall concern that killed FED/CEC/PCR/PHI/ACCD is partially mitigated here because `δ_m` for the step-penalty channel (which fires every step) is non-degenerate from the first interaction, providing a non-trivial IC signal and a non-silent Pareto vote before any reward channel fires. All required candidate shape slots are filled with substantive answers: the primitive is precisely defined, the improvement operator has an explicit update rule, the side-information channel is named (`[vector diagnostics, learned dynamics]`), a partial-order monotonic improvement claim is stated under named asymptotic conditions, and three concrete falsifiers are given.

The structural distinction holds under scrutiny: the primitives that failed in prior sprint-4 attempts either needed reward correlation to bootstrap (FED, CEC, ARP, PCR, TRAC, CSD, ACCD), collapsed to scalar on terminal-only channels (CHX, CRP, PICAV, ATP, PRAR, ACFC), or suffered cluster-cell-revisitation starvation (TRAC, CSD, HRC). BLIC's key structural gamble is that the step-penalty channel's IC entries provide non-trivial vector separation across actions from episode 1, keeping the Pareto operator active before any rare-event channel fires, while the cluster-conditioning is sufficient to differentiate actions within each cell. Whether this gamble survives on the substrate is an empirical question — which is precisely the right state for a `novel-direction` verdict.

## Risks the Engineer should be aware of

- The step-penalty channel dominance risk is real: if `IC[s, a, step-penalty]` is large and consistent while treasure/reward-channel IC entries remain near zero throughout the bootstrap window, the Pareto vote reduces to scalar step-penalty minimization, reproducing the ATP (#31) and PRAR (#36) failure exactly. The hypothesis's own falsifier (a) acknowledges this; instrument the per-channel IC magnitude ratio in logs from the first training minute.
- The cluster-revisitation bottleneck that killed TRAC (#26) and CSD (#32) applies here too, but with a different binding constraint: BLIC needs (cluster, action) cell mass to stabilize IC[s,a,:], not to accumulate rare-event observations. On DoorKey-8x8 / KeyCorridor this may still be sparse within 120 s; log the fraction of decision steps where the dominance margin is non-zero per env per minute as instructed by falsifier (b).
- The base policy is updated via cross-entropy of selected action vs. the nudged categorical (with stop-gradient on the nudge). This is effectively supervised imitation of the nudged distribution, which is a form of behavioral cloning on self-generated nudged actions. Verify this does not reduce to top-k trajectory cloning in the limit where α is large and the policy's own logits are overwhelmed by the nudge term.
