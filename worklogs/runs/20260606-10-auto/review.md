## Reasoning

PCR's central primitive — the per-(cluster, action) commitment-recovery vector `R[c,a] ∈ R^L` measuring step-lag until the policy's own modal action is re-confirmed at L alignment thresholds — is structurally distinct from every disqualifier family. The central learned object is not an expected return, advantage, Q-value, or return-to-go; it is a dispersion measurement over the policy's self-consistency along realized trajectories, with no Bellman recursion and no scalar magnitude mediating the update. The improvement operator is a binary Pareto-meet sign derived from the product partial order on (recovery-vector, terminal-outcome-vector) space, explicitly not scalar-weighted. Compared to the closest prior (#12 PEO), PEO applied scalar edit-ES over a hand-designed semantic edit basis; PCR has no edit basis and makes no counterfactual edit — it passively reads the policy's own logit sequences along existing trajectories. Compared to the actor-critic disqualifier, there is no critic supplying a magnitude weight to the policy gradient. The side-information channel (transition geometry: the policy's own per-step action-logit output, computed for free during action selection) is a legitimately new channel not exploited by any sprint-3 or sprint-4 candidate. All required-candidate-shape slots are filled with substantive, specific answers: the primitive is precisely defined, the improvement operator has a concrete update rule, the monotonic improvement claim is stated with its stationarity assumption, the falsifiers are measurable, and the rollout-cost discipline is honest.

The hypothesis correctly identifies its most dangerous failure mode: collinearity of the L alignment thresholds could collapse the Pareto test to scalar argmin (a "minimal disruption" rebadge). The mitigation — including thresholds designed to disagree — is sensible but needs to survive in practice. The terminal-outcome gate (slot 3) is the hypothesis's key guard against pure "commit to whatever is least disruptive regardless of outcome," which would be an empty primitive; the gate's reliance on terminal vector outcomes means the bootstrap risk identified for FED/CEC/TPP family (#15, #18, #21) applies here too, but in a weaker form: PCR's recovery primitive R fires on *every* step regardless of terminal reward (since it reads action-logit self-consistency, not reward channels), so the operator has candidate actions to score even before any rewarded trajectory is collected. The gate determines the *sign* of the nudge but not whether R is informative — this is the correct structural separation from the FED family.

## Risks the Engineer should be aware of

- The terminal-outcome sign gate fires only when the buffer contains at least two action-distinct trajectories through the same cluster c, at least one of which reached a Pareto-better terminal outcome. On early exploration when all trajectories are zero-reward, the gate is silent for every cluster, leaving the recovery primitive R uninformative about *direction* of improvement (it can rank actions by self-disruption but cannot sign the nudge). The Engineer should verify that the gate fires often enough to produce a non-trivial update before the reward bootstrap wall is crossed — the falsifier condition (b) is the right check.
- Cluster collapse on near-uniform logits (e.g., early DoorKey or any env where the policy has not yet differentiated) is a genuine risk: if 90%+ of steps fall into one cluster bucket, R[c,a] degenerates and the operator applies a global recovery preference that may be worse than doing nothing. The sign-pattern quantization should be tested across entropy levels.
- The commitment-recovery vector's magnitude carries no outcome information — only the binary Pareto-meet sign does. This means the step size `α` in `softmax(logits + α · pcr_nudge)` is a free hyperparameter with no principled scale from R; tuning sensitivity could be high.
