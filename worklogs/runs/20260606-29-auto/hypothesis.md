# 20260606-29-auto — HRC: Horizon-Recursive Concordance

## Research Gate

primitive: per-(state-cluster, action) L-vector of self-concordance fractions at exponentially-spaced horizons, plus a separate k-vector of per-channel terminal-sign-conditional action propensities; both reward-independent at the operator level.
improvement_operator: meet-of-two-Pareto-orders logit nudge — count an action's Pareto-non-dominance margin on the L-horizon concordance vector AND on the k-channel sign-conditional propensity vector; nudge logits by the product of margins (zero on either side cancels).
side_information: [transition geometry, vector diagnostics]
nearest_prior_or_disqualifier: candidate #24 PCR (Policy Commitment Recovery) — closest in spirit (policy-self-comparison primitive); also adjacent to FFTV (#9 in candidates) and DPC.
falsifier: (a) on a long-horizon sparse env (DoorKey-8x8 / KeyCorridor) the L-horizon concordance vector should differentiate actions from episode 1 with non-zero per-step Pareto-margin firing rate (≥ 5 % of decision steps); if the firing rate is below 1 % within the 120 s budget, the primitive is silent in the same way PCR was. (b) on the vector envs, the channel-side margin should fire on at least one channel after the first rewarded trajectory; if the channel side stays universally silent, the meet operator collapses to pure horizon-concordance which is a self-consistency-only signal with no reward coupling.

## Mechanism

Maintain two reward-independent statistics per (state-cluster s, action a). The first is a **horizon-concordance vector** `K[s,a,:] ∈ [0,1]^L` whose `l`-th entry is the empirical fraction of buffer transitions `(o_t = s, a_t = a, …, o_{t+h_l}, a_{t+h_l})` for which the **current snapshot policy's argmax** at `o_{t+h_l}` matches the actually-taken `a_{t+h_l}`. Horizons `h_1 < … < h_L` are exponentially spaced (e.g., 1, 4, 16, 64, 256). The second is a **channel-sign propensity vector** `P[s,a,:] ∈ [-1,1]^k` whose `m`-th entry is the difference between the empirical fractions of trajectories taking action `a` at `s` whose terminal cumulant in channel `m` exceeds the running per-channel median, vs. those that don't — but **clipped to zero whenever the channel-`m` cumulant variance across trajectories is below a self-tuned threshold** (so silent channels contribute a hard zero rather than noise). The improvement operator computes two Pareto-non-dominance margins separately — `n_a^K - m_a^K` over the L-vector `K[s,a,:]`, and `n_a^P - m_a^P` over the k-vector `P[s,a,:]` — and nudges `π_θ(a|s)` logits by `α · (n_a^K - m_a^K) · max(n_a^P - m_a^P, 1)` (the channel side is a multiplier with floor 1, so even a fully-silent channel side does not silence the operator; the operator escalates as channels become discriminating). This is a single primitive (a doubly-Pareto-vote on a paired statistic) with one composition law (product of margins with floor-1 channel multiplier).

## Required candidate shape

1. **Experience object:** ordinary trajectories with their per-step `info["vector"]` channels and the terminal cumulant; replayed transitions are tagged with the snapshot policy's argmax at each step (computed cheaply on a stale forward pass during replay).
2. **Core primitive:** the paired statistic `(K[s,a,:], P[s,a,:])` with `K ∈ [0,1]^L` (self-concordance fractions at L horizons) and `P ∈ [-1,1]^k` (sign-conditional channel propensity, clipped on low-variance channels). The primitive is a per-(cluster, action) point in the product space `[0,1]^L × [-1,1]^k`.
3. **Improvement operator:** at each decision step, compute Pareto-non-dominance margins `(Δ_K, Δ_P)` over the action set on the K-vector and on the P-vector independently; nudge logits by `α · Δ_K · max(Δ_P, 1)`. Logit update is additive, no Bellman backup, no scalar reward weight, no critic, no return-to-go.
4. **Execution rule:** sample `a ~ softmax(z + α · Δ_K · max(Δ_P, 1))` where `z` are base logits from the policy network; entropy floor at low magnitude to avoid premature collapse.
5. **Vector feedback rule:** the k channels enter only through the P-vector (sign-conditional propensities, hard-clipped to zero on low-variance channels), and the per-channel margins enter via a coordinate-wise Pareto vote — never combined with a fixed weight. Channels are also handled at different rates by the variance gate: noisy channels are silenced naturally, informative channels accumulate margin.
6. **Rollout-cost discipline:** one environment step per stored transition; one snapshot-policy forward pass per replayed transition (amortized in batched buffer scans, no per-decision counterfactual rollouts). At deployment: one network forward pass + a constant-time table lookup per decision. No verifier calls, no simulator branches, no rollout-per-update.
7. **Nearest-neighbor novelty audit:** closest prior is candidate **#24 PCR** (Policy Commitment Recovery): both use snapshot-policy self-concordance as a reward-independent primitive. PCR collapsed because its improvement operator was *gated* by a terminal-outcome sign that stayed universally silent in the bootstrap window. HRC differs structurally in two ways: (i) the operator is a *product of margins* with a floor-1 channel multiplier, so the horizon side fires even when the channel side is silent — there is no AND-gate that can starve the operator; (ii) HRC carries an L-horizon vector instead of PCR's L-threshold scalar, exposing **multi-scale** disagreement structure (long-horizon disagreement with my own future actions vs. short-horizon disagreement) that PCR's single recovery-latency scalar collapsed into a single number.
8. **Predicted failure modes:** (a) on near-deterministic short-trajectory envs, the snapshot policy's argmax stabilizes within a few episodes and `K[s,a,:]` saturates near 1.0 for every action — the horizon Pareto margin goes to zero and the operator silences (a self-consistency-saturation failure); (b) under heavy stochasticity (RG), the snapshot's argmax may differ from the actually-taken action just because of sampling noise, polluting the K-vector with random concordance — could degrade to noise; (c) the cluster-population bottleneck (KTAC's failure mode) still applies — if state clusters do not reach revisitation mass, K and P stay sparse; (d) if all L horizons agree (high inter-horizon correlation), the horizon Pareto vote effectively collapses to a 1-D rank, reducing to a pseudo-scalar concordance maximization; (e) for environments with `k = 1` channels (DoorKey, KeyCorridor), the channel side margin is at most ±1, making the multiplier at most 2 — safe but uninformative on the channel side.
9. **Side-information channel:** **transition geometry** (state-cluster assignment via online k-means on policy-trunk embeddings) and **vector diagnostics** (per-channel `info["vector"]` signal with self-tuned variance gate). No event lens, no hand-engineered automaton, no demonstrations.
10. **Monotonic improvement claim:** under the assumption that the snapshot policy is improving in the sense that its long-horizon argmax sequence becomes increasingly self-consistent on Pareto-non-dominated channel signs, the meet-of-Pareto-orders operator monotonically increases the policy's mass on actions that are simultaneously (i) self-recommended at multiple horizons and (ii) Pareto-non-dominated on the active (non-silenced) channels. Specifically, the expected one-step KL between consecutive snapshot policies is non-negative whenever both Pareto orders agree, and zero whenever either order is empty — a guarded monotonicity that explicitly degrades to no-op rather than to a wrong direction.

## Why it is not PCR / FFTV / DPC

PCR (#24) used a single recovery-latency scalar per (cluster, action) — a 1-D primitive with an AND-gate on terminal outcome sign; it collapsed because the gate was silent in bootstrap. HRC carries an L-vector (multi-scale disagreement) and uses a *product-with-floor* composition, so the horizon side fires unilaterally when the channel side is silent. FFTV used per-channel timing dispersion (IQR of first-firing-time offsets) — channel-only structure. HRC's primitive is *self-policy concordance*, not channel-firing timing; the K-vector is reward-free in a structurally stronger sense (it would fire on a no-reward env). DPC's pairwise-divergence-state sign votes operate over trajectory pairs and require divergence-event sample mass; HRC operates per-(cluster, action) directly with no pairing requirement, and its concordance is a unary statistic per replay transition (one snapshot-argmax check per stored step) rather than a pair-conditioned signed vote.

## Why it scales beyond the substrate

At a 20k-action horizon, exponentially-spaced horizons `h_l ∈ {1, 4, 16, 64, 256, 1024, 4096, 16384}` cover the entire range with `L = 8` slots — a constant cost per replay transition. The snapshot-argmax check is one extra forward pass that batches naturally with policy training; on a transformer policy generating paragraphs this becomes "would the current model still pick this paragraph if it saw the same context?" — a cheap consistency check that does not require regenerating the paragraph or scoring its quality. For 6-channel vector feedback (success, cost, safety, latency, energy, validity), the channel-side Pareto vote operates exactly as on the substrate's k=2 case but with more channels, providing finer-grained discrimination; the variance gate naturally silences uninformative channels (e.g., constant-cost contexts) without manual reweighting. The meet-of-Pareto-orders structure means an LLM-with-tools system gets two orthogonal pressures at every step — internal self-consistency and external multi-channel feedback — combined without a fixed scalar weight. Crucially, the horizon-vector primitive does not require reward to fire, so the operator remains active during the long pre-reward stretches that characterize 20k-action episodes.
