## Reasoning

HRC's structural distinction from PCR (#24) is real and load-bearing: PCR's improvement operator was an AND-gate (both the recovery-latency scalar and the terminal-outcome sign had to be non-zero), which collapsed silently during bootstrap. HRC replaces this with a product-with-floor-1 composition — `α · Δ_K · max(Δ_P, 1)` — where the horizon-concordance margin `Δ_K` fires from episode 1 without any reward signal, and the channel-side multiplier degrades to the identity (floor 1) when channels are silent rather than zeroing the operator out. The K-vector itself is a genuine multi-scale primitive: tracking snapshot-policy argmax self-concordance at L exponentially-spaced horizons is reward-free in a structurally stronger sense than PCR's recovery-latency (it would fire on a zero-reward environment). Neither the K-vector nor the product-with-floor composition reduces under variable renaming to any disqualifier family: there is no Bellman backup, no TD error, no scalar return weight, no actor-critic, no elite trajectory cloning, no verifier, and no scalarization of the channel side (the P-vector enters only through a coordinate-wise Pareto vote with a variance gate, never as w^T r). The structural-distinction paragraph (§"Why it is not PCR / FFTV / DPC") is specific and quotes concrete structural differences, not vocabulary differences.

All required candidate-shape slots carry substantive answers: the primitive is precisely defined as the paired per-(cluster, action) statistic `(K[s,a,:], P[s,a,:])` with explicit update rules; the improvement operator is `α · Δ_K · max(Δ_P, 1)` with both Pareto-margin computations spelled out; the execution rule names the logit-additive sampling scheme with an entropy floor; the vector feedback rule specifies coordinate-wise Pareto with a variance gate and explicit hard-zero on low-variance channels; rollout cost is accounted for (one snapshot forward pass per replayed transition, amortized); predicted failure modes are enumerated (5 distinct modes including self-consistency saturation, stochastic noise pollution, cluster-revisitation bottleneck, horizon-correlation collapse, and k=1 channel uninformativeness); the monotonic improvement claim is stated as guarded monotonicity with an explicit no-op degradation path; and side-information channels are named (transition geometry + vector diagnostics).

## Risks the Engineer should be aware of

- The cluster-revisitation bottleneck (failure mode c) is the same one that killed TRAC (#26) and CSD (#32) — if state clusters do not reach sufficient revisitation mass within 120 s, both K and P stay sparse. The K-side does not require reward to fire but still requires the same (cluster, action) cell to be visited multiple times. Monitor K-cell fill rate as a diagnostic during the run and verify that K Pareto margins are firing at the claimed ≥ 5% rate on DoorKey-8x8 within the budget.

- The `P[s,a,:]` variance gate and its self-tuning threshold need careful calibration. On Deep Sea Treasure the step-penalty channel will have near-zero cross-trajectory variance (constant -1 per step), so it must be silenced; the treasure channel will have nonzero variance only after the first rewarded trajectory. The floor-1 multiplier should handle this gracefully, but if the variance threshold is set too low it may admit the constant step-penalty channel into the Pareto vote, partially reintroducing the scalar-minimization failure seen in ATP (#31) and CHX (#17).

- On envs with k=1 effective channels (DoorKey, KeyCorridor), the hypothesis correctly notes the channel multiplier is at most 2 and contributes no discrimination — the operator runs entirely on the K-vector horizon concordance. This is safe but means those envs are a test of the K-primitive alone, and if K-margin firing rate is low (failure mode a — saturation) the operator will produce near-uniform nudges. Consider logging both components' contribution separately.
