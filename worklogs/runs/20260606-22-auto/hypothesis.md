# 20260606-22-auto — KTAC: Kendall-Tau Action-Concordance with Per-Channel Order Consensus

## Research Gate

primitive: per-(context-cluster, channel) empirical pairwise action-dominance probability matrix `P_m[s, a, a']` estimated from trajectory pairs that share an early prefix and differ at a divergence step, where the pairwise comparison is the *sign* of the channel-m terminal-cumulant difference (not the magnitude).
improvement_operator: Kemeny consensus order σ*(s) on actions, computed as the permutation minimizing the total weighted Kendall-tau distance to the k per-channel pairwise order matrices `P_m[s, ·, ·]`; logit nudge `α · (rank_σ*(a)^{-1} − 1/|A|)` toward σ*(s)'s top-ranked action — never combining the channels into a scalar.
side_information: vector diagnostics (info["vector"] per step), transition geometry (used only to define context-cluster equivalence over observations).
nearest_prior_or_disqualifier: dpc-divergent-prefix-concordance (alive-weak); also adjacent to multi-objective scalarization disqualifier.
falsifier: on the vector envs (DST, RG), if either (a) the Kemeny consensus σ*(s) is identical to the order obtained by scalar terminal-return ranking (i.e., the channels are perfectly concordant on the substrate, making aggregation indistinguishable from scalar weighting), measured by the per-step Kendall distance between σ*(s) and σ_scalar(s) being < 1/|A|^2 in expectation, or (b) the per-channel pairwise probability matrices `P_m` remain near-uniform `(0.5)` in the budget (no sign discrimination), then the primitive is silent or scalar-equivalent and the candidate fails.

## Mechanism

KTAC indexes its primitive by *context-cluster* (a coarse equivalence over observations from a small online k-means in penultimate-layer feature space) rather than by hash-bucket or position; this indexing has been the binding bottleneck in the FED/CEC/TRAC/TPM family. For every pair of trajectories `(τ_i, τ_j)` in the replay buffer that share a context-cluster `s` at some step (their decision contexts are clustered together) and chose different actions `a_i, a_j` at that step, we update for each channel `m` the pairwise count: `N_m[s, a, a'] += 1` when action `a` was the one chosen and `sign(c_T^{(i)}[m] − c_T^{(j)}[m]) > 0`, where `c_T[m]` is the *m-th coordinate of the terminal cumulant of the trajectory*. The estimated dominance probability is `P_m[s, a, a'] = N_m[s, a, a'] / (N_m[s, a, a'] + N_m[s, a', a])`. The improvement operator then takes the k matrices `{P_m[s, ·, ·]}_{m=1}^k` and computes the Kemeny consensus order σ*(s) — the permutation of actions minimizing total per-channel Kendall-tau distance — using a Bradley-Terry-like local search (since exact Kemeny is NP-hard, a Borda-initialized swap-improvement is the engineering choice). The policy's logits at observation `o ∈ s` are nudged additively by `α · score_σ*(s)(a)` where `score(a) = (|A| − rank(a)) / (|A|−1) − 1/2` is centered. This is one primitive (P_m), one operator (Kemeny), one nudge — no critic, no Bellman, no scalar reward, no top-k cloning, no verifier, no count, no goal/event lens.

## Required candidate shape

1. **Experience object:** Replay buffer of complete trajectories; each step records `(o_t, a_t, v_t)` and the trajectory records its terminal cumulant `c_T = Σ_t v_t` per channel. Context-clusters are produced by an online k-means on the policy's penultimate-layer representation `h(o_t)`, refit periodically.
2. **Core primitive:** The k-tensor of per-channel pairwise dominance probabilities `P_m[s, a, a'] ∈ [0, 1]` indexed by context-cluster s and channel m. This is a sign-aggregated object — magnitudes never enter — so it is not Q, not return-to-go, not advantage, not a return distribution.
3. **Improvement operator:** Kemeny consensus order σ*(s) per cluster, recomputed every fixed interval. Logit nudge by σ*-rank score. The operator is fundamentally non-scalarizable: when channels disagree, Kemeny picks a specific consensus order that is *not* recoverable by any fixed linear combination `wᵀr` of channel cumulants — see e.g. Arrow's impossibility theorem for the structural reason.
4. **Execution rule:** Sample `a ~ softmax(logit_θ(o) + α · score_σ*(s)(·))` where `s = cluster(h(o))`. Execution is on-policy with the nudge.
5. **Vector feedback rule:** Each channel m yields its own pairwise dominance matrix `P_m`. Aggregation across channels happens only at the very last step (Kemeny consensus on rankings), and the aggregation rule is permutation-invariant and rank-only — no fixed scalar weighting is ever computed; channels with terminal-only firing contribute meaningful sign votes from the moment two trajectory pairs differ on the channel's terminal value, even if all intermediate values are zero.
6. **Rollout-cost discipline:** One env step = one buffer entry. Updates to `P_m` happen offline on the buffer at fixed intervals (every N episodes); no counterfactual rollouts, no verifier calls, no simulator branches. Kemeny computation cost is O(|A|² log|A|) per cluster per update interval — independent of buffer size at decision time.
7. **Nearest-neighbor novelty audit:** DPC (alive-weak) computes per-channel sign votes V[s_div, a, a', m] over divergent-prefix pairs and applies a Pareto-non-dominance gate. KTAC differs in three concrete ways: (i) DPC's gate is a Pareto vote on per-channel sign-margin sums — symmetric scalar treatment per channel — while KTAC's operator is a Kemeny consensus on full per-action *orderings* per channel, which is rank-only and dimension-free; (ii) DPC indexes by exact first-divergence state s_div, requiring expensive collision; KTAC indexes by context-cluster on policy features, which densifies as the policy learns; (iii) KTAC's monotonic-improvement claim is rooted in Kemeny optimality (a property of the rank-aggregation rule) rather than Pareto-non-dominance, and Kemeny consensus is provably distinct from scalarization (Arrow). The closest disqualifier is multi-objective scalarized RL; KTAC structurally cannot reduce because no scalar combination of `P_1, ..., P_k` is ever formed — the operator works on rank space not value space.
8. **Predicted failure modes:** (a) On envs where the channels are perfectly rank-concordant (i.e. one channel's order is a monotone of another's), Kemeny consensus = single-channel order = scalar maximization on that channel; falsifier (a) above measures this directly. (b) When context-cluster sizes are too small, `P_m` cells stay near-uniform (count of pairs sharing a cluster < 4 per (s, a, a')), and the consensus is dominated by initialization noise — same density wall as TPM/DPC. (c) Stochastic transitions (RG) introduce variance in terminal cumulants that may flip sign on individual pairs; however, KTAC is *more robust here than DPC* because rank-aggregation of probabilities is asymptotically consistent under any noise distribution as long as `P_m[a, a']` differs from 0.5 on enough pairs — not requiring large magnitudes. (d) When |A| is large (e.g. Craftax has 17 actions), Kemeny optimization becomes a heuristic and the consensus order may be inconsistent across re-computation; this is a scaling concern for the LLM-action setting and must be addressed by hierarchical action grouping.
9. **Side-information channel:** Vector diagnostics (per-step `info["vector"]`); transition geometry only via context-cluster equivalence on policy features. No event lens, no demonstration, no pretrained prior, no verifier.
10. **Monotonic improvement claim:** Under the assumption that the per-channel pairwise dominance matrices `P_m[s, ·, ·]` are stationary in the policy-induced occupancy and that channel cumulants are partial-order representable, the Kemeny consensus order σ*(s) is the rank that minimizes expected per-channel disagreement (by definition of Kemeny). The logit nudge toward σ*(s) monotonically reduces the expected Kendall-tau distance between the policy's induced action-rank `argsort(logit)` and σ*(s) — in expectation, in the small-α regime — for every cluster s, by gradient descent on a smooth surrogate of Kendall distance. This is the analog of policy-improvement-toward-the-greedy-policy in tabular Q-learning, but with the greedy operator replaced by Kemeny consensus over per-channel orderings — and it does not invoke value at any point.

## Why it is not dpc-divergent-prefix-concordance or scalarized multi-objective RL

DPC accumulates per-channel sign-margin sums and applies Pareto-non-dominance: each channel votes a single number per (s, a) pair, and the comparison is partial-order on R^k. KTAC instead accumulates per-channel pairwise probability matrices `P_m[s, a, a']` (rank-rich, not magnitude-rich), and the operator is Kemeny consensus on full action rankings — which is provably distinct from any scalarization (no `w` exists such that argmax_a wᵀP[s,a,:] reproduces σ*(s) in general; this is a corollary of Arrow's theorem applied to the rank-aggregation rule). Compared to scalarized multi-objective RL, KTAC never computes any scalar function of channel cumulants — the only scalar in the algorithm is the rank-position score, which is a pure function of the consensus ordering. The structural distinction is: DPC and scalarization both work in *value* space (margins summed per channel); KTAC works in *order* space (rankings aggregated). This is the same type of structural shift as moving from regression to ordinal regression — they answer different questions.

## Why it scales beyond the substrate

At horizon 20k actions with action space "generate a paragraph": the action space is no longer enumerable, so direct Kemeny consensus over |A| actions is intractable. The KTAC primitive scales by replacing the action enumeration with a *learned action-embedding pairwise comparison head*: rather than maintaining `P_m[s, a, a']` over all action pairs, train a small per-channel scoring head `f_m(s, e_a) → R` whose pairwise differences `f_m(s, e_a) − f_m(s, e_a')` are supervised by the observed pairwise sign votes (a learnable Bradley-Terry model per channel). At decision time, sample K candidate paragraphs from a base LLM, score each with each `f_m`, run k-channel Kemeny consensus on the K candidates, and execute the consensus top. The improvement operator remains rank-aggregation over per-channel orderings — never a scalarization — and the aggregation cost is O(K² · k) per decision, completely decoupled from horizon length. For 6-channel vector feedback (success, cost, safety, latency, energy, validity), this gives a deployment-time consensus that respects all six channels without any human-tuned weighting; the Kemeny consensus is the algorithm's stand against scalarization, and it generalizes to any number of channels by construction.
