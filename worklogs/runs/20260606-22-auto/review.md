## Reasoning

KTAC's structural primitive — a per-channel pairwise dominance probability matrix `P_m[s, a, a']` accumulated from trajectory pairs sharing a context-cluster, with the improvement operator being Kemeny consensus over per-channel action rankings — is genuinely distinct from the disqualifier families and from all 32 prior attempts. The critical structural claim is that the operator works entirely in rank/order space, never forming any scalar function of channel cumulants. The Kemeny consensus is provably not recoverable by any `w^T r` scalarization (Arrow's impossibility theorem for the rank-aggregation domain), and the hypothesis makes this point precisely rather than merely asserting "we don't use value vocabulary." The nearest prior, DPC (alive-weak), works in value-margin space per channel (summing sign margins, then applying Pareto-non-dominance), whereas KTAC works in pairwise-probability space per channel and aggregates via rank consensus — a structurally different composition law. The nearest disqualifier families (scalarized multi-objective RL, preference optimization / RLHF) both reduce to a scalar function of channel outcomes; KTAC explicitly does not. All required-candidate-shape slots are filled with substantive answers: the experience object, the core primitive, the improvement operator with an update rule, the vector feedback rule, the rollout-cost accounting, the monotonic improvement claim, the falsifier, predicted failure modes, and the side-information channel declaration.

The main structural risk is the cross-trajectory bootstrap dependency: terminal cumulant sign votes are only informative after trajectory pairs that share a context-cluster and differ on a terminal channel have been collected. However, the hypothesis distinguishes this from the FED/CEC/hash-collision family in a concrete way — context-cluster assignment is based on learned policy-feature k-means that densifies as the policy explores, rather than on fixed observation hashes that require exact collisions. The hypothesis predicts the failure mode explicitly (item 8b: cell counts stay near-0.5 when cluster populations are too small), gives a measurable falsifier (Kendall distance between `σ*(s)` and `σ_scalar(s)` < 1/|A|^2), and explains the mechanism by which this differs from CEC/CWTP in kind, not just in naming.

## Risks the Engineer should be aware of

- The bootstrap dependency on terminal cumulant sign votes means KTAC inherits a version of the FED/CEC bootstrap wall before any rewarded trajectory pair sharing a cluster is collected; the context-cluster densification story needs to be verified empirically — on DoorKey and KeyCorridor the first rewarded trajectory may not happen within the initial exploration budget, leaving `P_m` uniform throughout.
- The improvement operator's monotonic improvement claim (item 10) holds only "under the assumption that per-channel pairwise dominance matrices `P_m[s, ·, ·]` are stationary in the policy-induced occupancy" — but the occupancy changes as logit nudges update, so stationarity is violated; this is acknowledged in the "small-α regime" qualifier but the regime boundaries are not specified, which could make the nudge magnitude `α` a fragile hyperparameter.
- The Kemeny consensus is computed via a local-search heuristic (Borda-initialized swap improvement), which may produce inconsistent orderings across re-computation intervals when `P_m` cells are noisy; the predicted failure mode (d) names this for large |A| but it may also manifest at |A|=4 (DoorKey) when cluster sample sizes are small.
