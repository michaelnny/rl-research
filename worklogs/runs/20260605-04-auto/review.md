## Reasoning

FED's central primitive — the empirical per-channel outcome-multiset `F(u, a)` and its Pareto frontier `P(u, a)` indexed by observation-hash buckets — is structurally distinct from every disqualifier family listed in `prior_attempts.md`. The improvement operator is a binary indicator over whether an action's conditional outcome multiset *extends the empirical Pareto attainment set without contracting it*, gating a logit nudge `π(a|u) ← π(a|u) · exp(η · I[...])`. This is not a scalar-weighted log-prob update (there is no advantage or return-to-go weighting), not a value backup (no Bellman operator, no learned Q or V), not scalarized vector reward (`wᵀr` with any fixed or learned `w` cannot express Pareto-front set-extension — correctly argued in the hypothesis), not GVF/successor features (no cumulant projection; the raw vector multiset is preserved), and not a Pareto-scalarization variant (the partial-order set-extension criterion strictly subsumes any linear ordering). The structural distinction from the nearest prior SIT is sharp: SIT copies and grafts whole suffixes across observation-equivalent nodes with a verification rollout; FED operates locally on accumulated action-conditional vector signatures at a single context with no trajectory copying, no graft, and no extra rollout. All required-candidate-shape slots are substantively filled: the primitive is named and defined, the improvement operator has a precise update rule, the side-information channel is `vector diagnostics + transition geometry`, five concrete predicted failure modes are given, and the monotonic improvement claim is stated as non-contraction of the empirical attainment set `A_t` in the set-inclusion order.

The hypothesis also satisfies the mission's anti-pattern checklist: it does not "avoid value vocabulary" as a stand-in for novelty — it explains what role the Pareto-frontier plays as a partial-order-valued future compression object replacing the scalar value, and it explains why this exposes new usable side information (per-channel vector diagnostics) before reward correlation exists (vector signal starts at step 1, not only at terminal reward events). The monotonic improvement claim is honest — it is stated under the assumption of stationary vector-outcome distributions and large enough per-bucket sample budget, not claimed unconditionally.

## Risks the Engineer should be aware of

- The observation-hash bucket scheme must achieve sufficient collision rate (>1% within a rollout) on the panel envs for the empirical Pareto front to accumulate meaningful sample mass; the hypothesis correctly names this as failure mode (b), but the Engineer should verify bucket occupancy statistics early and expose them in logging before drawing any inference from null Pareto updates.
- On DoorKey-6x6 (single-channel, sparse scalar reward) the improvement operator degenerates to empirical max-extension — the hypothesis explicitly predicts weakness there. The Engineer should confirm the vector envs (Deep-Sea Treasure, Resource-Gathering) are the primary signal carriers and not weight the DoorKey score equally when diagnosing the run.
- The side-information channel includes `transition geometry` (observation-hash buckets), which is not hand-engineered domain structure but is a lossy hash of the raw observation. On partially observable or pixel-based envs this hash may be too coarse to discriminate states; the Engineer should log hash collision rates and consider a learned encoder fingerprint as a drop-in replacement if bucket entropy is too low.
