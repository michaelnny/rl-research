## Reasoning

CHX's core primitive — the per-step L2 distance by which removing a cumulant-trace point would shrink the convex hull of the within-trajectory cumulant trace in R^k — is structurally distinct from every disqualifier family and all prior attempts #01–#16. It is not a value, advantage, return, density, count, or novelty signal: it is a purely geometric, within-trajectory measure of how much a single step extends the attained vector-outcome region. The hypothesis explicitly handles the closest border cases: the weight `(\hat h_t - 1/T)` is a non-linear functional of the full trace and cannot be written as `w^T v_t` for any fixed or learned `w` (the convex hull in R^k is not reproduced by any linear scalarization), ruling out the "scalarized vector-reward" disqualifier; VCC and FED both require cross-trajectory Pareto comparisons or obs-hash buckets, which CHX entirely omits. The update form `Σ_t (\hat h_t - 1/T) · ∇ log π(a_t|s_t)` superficially resembles REINFORCE with per-step weights, but the mission permits existing update machinery as a component — the explanatory primitive is the hull-contribution geometry, which is doing something the standard return-weighted update cannot: rewarding steps that extend vector-outcome coverage *within a single trajectory* without ever computing a scalar return or bootstrapping from a value estimate. All required candidate-shape slots are filled with substantive answers: primitive, improvement operator, experience object, execution rule, vector feedback rule, rollout-cost discipline, predicted failure modes (including the honest k_eff=1 self-disqualification check), monotonic improvement claim, and side-information channel.

The monotonic improvement claim is conditional but well-stated: under the assumption that `E[h_t | a_t]` is non-uniform across actions in state `s_t`, the surrogate increases expected per-trajectory hull-volume in R^k — a multi-channel coverage objective that is conceptually well-defined beyond the substrate (hull geometry scales to k≥3 at T=20k). The predicted failure modes are detailed and falsifiable, and the nearest-prior novelty audit is explicit and accurate.

## Risks the Engineer should be aware of

- On the sparse envs (DoorKey, KeyCorridor), the per-step `info["vector"]` signal may have effective rank k_eff=1 or near-zero variance on most steps (e.g., a step-penalty channel that is constant and a reward channel that fires only at termination). The hypothesis explicitly flags this as a self-disqualifying degenerate case; the Engineer should instrument effective rank of the cumulant trace early and abort/flag if k_eff=1 throughout training.
- The update is REINFORCE-style with no baseline, making variance a practical concern at long horizons. The centered weight `(\hat h_t - 1/T)` provides a zero-mean baseline within the trajectory, which helps, but the Engineer may want to track gradient norm and clip if needed.
- Hull computation via qhull at T=20k with k≥4 can hit worst-case exponential facet counts in high dimension; the O(T log T) claim holds only for k≤3. For k>3 channels, the Engineer should verify that scipy.spatial.ConvexHull terminates within budget or fall back to an approximate hull (e.g., random projections to R^3 sub-spaces).
