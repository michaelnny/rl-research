# 20260605-08-auto — CHX (Cumulant-Hull Extremality)

## Research Gate

primitive: per-step convex-hull extremality of the vector-cumulant trace within a single trajectory
improvement_operator: increase log-prob of an action proportional to the geometric "hull contribution" its step makes to the trajectory's cumulant trace in `R^k`; decrease for interior steps that are interpolation-dominated by their neighbors. No reward weighting, no cross-trajectory comparison, no obs-hash bucket, no critic.
side_information: vector diagnostics
nearest_prior_or_disqualifier: VCC (vector-cumulant confluence, candidate file) and PTW (#parked); also adjacent to scalarized vector-reward maximization and to advantage-weighted log-prob.
falsifier: scores at or below random on the vector envs (Deep Sea Treasure, Resource Gathering); on the sparse envs (DoorKey, KeyCorridor) where the harness `info["vector"]` is informative, scores no better than random; or, instrumented diagnostics show that hull membership is uniformly random with respect to action identity (the operator is comparing geometry that is not action-conditional).

## Mechanism

For a rolled-out trajectory `τ = (s_0,a_0,v_0), (s_1,a_1,v_1), …, (s_T,a_T,v_T)` where `v_t ∈ R^k` is the per-step vector signal from `info["vector"]`, define the cumulant trace `c_t = Σ_{s≤t} v_s ∈ R^k` (with `c_{-1} = 0`). Compute the convex hull `H(τ) = ConvHull({c_{-1}, c_0, …, c_T})` in `R^k`. For each step `t`, define its **hull contribution** `h_t = max(0, dist(c_t, ConvHull({c_{-1}, …, c_T} \ {c_t})))` — the L2 distance by which the hull would shrink if step `t`'s cumulant point were removed (zero if `c_t` is interior). Standardize: `\hat h_t = h_t / (Σ_t h_t + ε)`. The improvement operator is one supervised update per trajectory: `θ ← θ + α · Σ_t \hat h_t · ∇_θ log π_θ(a_t | s_t) − β · Σ_t (1/T − \hat h_t) · ∇_θ log π_θ(a_t | s_t)` — equivalently, weight each (s_t, a_t) by the centered hull share `\hat h_t − 1/T`. The primitive is purely *geometric* over the trajectory's own outcome-cumulant trace; the operator increases probability of actions whose steps push the trace into previously-unattained directions in vector-outcome space, and decreases probability of actions whose steps are convex combinations of their neighbors.

## Required candidate shape

1. **Experience object:** Single rolled-out trajectory with per-step vector signal `v_t ∈ R^k` from `info["vector"]`. No replay, no donor pool, no obs-hash index, no terminal-reward special-case.
2. **Core primitive:** The per-step hull contribution `h_t = dist(c_t, ConvHull(\{c_{-1},…,c_T\} \\ \{c_t\}))` of the cumulant trace `c_t = Σ_{s≤t} v_s` in `R^k`. This is a within-trajectory geometric quantity — not a value, advantage, return, scalar reward weight, density, count, or learned cumulant; not a comparison against any other trajectory or any other action.
3. **Improvement operator:** One natural-gradient-style policy update per trajectory: each step `(s_t, a_t)` enters the surrogate loss with weight `(\hat h_t − 1/T)`, where `\hat h_t = h_t / Σ_t h_t`. Steps on the hull get positive weight; interior steps get negative weight. No critic, no Bellman recursion, no scalarized reward.
4. **Execution rule:** Sample from `π_θ(· | s_t)` greedily during evaluation; during data-collection, sample with a small entropy floor (Boltzmann at fixed temperature). No conditioning on side-information at decision time; the side-information is consumed only by the update operator.
5. **Vector feedback rule:** The full `v_t ∈ R^k` is consumed component-wise to define points in `R^k` — never collapsed to `w^T v`. Channels with different scales are MAD-standardized once at init from the first batch of rollouts, then frozen (the standardizer is a fixed normalizer, not a learned weighting). Pareto / Hull operations are performed on the standardized vectors; this preserves multi-channel structure because the hull in `R^k` is invariant to per-axis scale but not to mixing across axes.
6. **Rollout-cost discipline:** One environment trajectory per accepted update. No counterfactual rollouts, no donor segments, no verification rollouts, no simulator branching, no verifier calls. The hull computation is `O(T log T)` for k≤3 (qhull) and amortized cheap; the update is one backward pass.
7. **Nearest-neighbor novelty audit:** Closest are (a) VCC (candidate file) which builds Pareto fronts across actions within cumulant-buckets to derive a dominance margin used as a logit nudge, and (b) the disqualifier "scalarized vector-reward maximization." CHX neither buckets across trajectories (no cross-trajectory Pareto comparison; no bucketing) nor scalarizes the vector signal (the weight is the geometric distance-to-hull, computed in `R^k`, which is *not* expressible as `w^T v` for any fixed or learned `w`). It also is not advantage-weighted log-prob: the weight `\hat h_t − 1/T` is a function only of where the cumulant trace bends in vector space, not of any scalar reward or value estimate.
8. **Predicted failure modes:**
   - On envs with effectively k=1 vector signal (a single channel with non-trivial variance and others identically zero), the hull degenerates to a 1-D segment whose extremes are just the min and max steps — this *is* a return-to-go rebadge. The Engineer must instrument effective rank of the cumulant trace and flag k_eff=1 as a failure.
   - On highly stochastic transitions, the cumulant trace's hull fluctuates per rollout for the same policy; `Var(\hat h_t)` will be high and updates noisy.
   - The hull-extremality of step `t` may correlate with the action choice at `t` only through environment dynamics, not policy choice (the hull bends because the *state* afforded a salient outcome, not because the *action* caused it). This is the credit-assignment-by-correlation hazard. Instrumentation: ablate to a state-only weighted update and check the action-specific component of the gradient.
   - On envs where every step has approximately equal vector contribution (e.g., constant per-step cost), the hull degenerates to a near-line in the cost direction with all other channels zero, and the per-step weights converge to `1/T` (operator is a no-op).
9. **Side-information channel:** vector diagnostics — specifically the panel `info["vector"]` per-step signal that is already plumbed for vector envs and is the explicit side-information lever the substrate exposes. No event lens, no transition geometry index, no demonstrations.
10. **Monotonic improvement claim:** Within the on-policy distribution and assuming the hull-contribution `h_t` of step `t` is statistically associated with action `a_t` (i.e., the per-action conditional `E[h_t | a_t]` is non-uniform across actions in state `s_t`), the update increases the policy's expected per-trajectory hull-volume in vector-cumulant space — equivalently, the expected `k`-volume of the convex hull of the cumulant trace. This is monotonic in expectation under standard policy-gradient regularity (small step `α`, bounded gradient norm) because the surrogate loss is the empirical expectation of `(\hat h_t − 1/T) · log π(a_t|s_t)`, whose policy-gradient is positive in the direction of expected hull-volume increase. The improved object is *vector-outcome diversity per trajectory* — a multi-channel exploration measure — not scalar return.

## Why it is not VCC or scalarized vector-reward maximization

VCC (candidate file, `vcc-vector-cumulant-confluence.md`) buckets trajectories by quantized cumulant and derives logit nudges from cross-action Pareto-front dominance margins computed *across* trajectories within a bucket. CHX has no buckets, no cross-trajectory comparison, and no Pareto front: the entire signal comes from the within-trajectory geometric shape of one rollout's cumulant trace. Against the "scalarized vector-reward" disqualifier, the weight `\hat h_t` is the distance from a point to the convex hull of its leave-one-out — a non-linear functional of the entire vector trace that cannot be written as `w^T v_t` for any fixed or learned `w` (you cannot reproduce convex-hull membership with a linear scalarization, period). Against advantage-weighted log-prob, the weight depends on no scalar reward signal at any step and contains no temporal-difference recursion; the trajectory's *terminal reward is unused*, only the per-step vector-cumulant geometry is consumed.

## Why it scales beyond the substrate

At 20k-action horizon with k≥3 vector channels (cost, latency, validity, success, safety, energy), the convex hull of the cumulant trace in `R^k` remains a well-defined geometric object whose extremal points are exactly the steps where the policy entered previously-unattained outcome regions — this is the per-step "novelty" measure that an LLM-with-tools agent already needs (which tool call genuinely opened a new outcome direction vs. which one was a wasted retry?). The hull computation is `O(T log T)` even at T=20k. The primitive does not depend on the action being a discrete primitive: for `a = "generate a paragraph"`, the same operator weights the log-prob of *that paragraph* by the geometric hull contribution of *that paragraph's outcome vector* — the action just has to be log-probable under the policy and have an associated vector signal. Vector feedback is the native input format, not a translation: an LLM agent already gets multi-channel feedback (validity check, cost in tokens, latency, safety verdict, user preference); CHX consumes those as the unmixed `R^k` vector and weights paragraph-actions by their hull-contribution to the conversation's outcome trajectory.
