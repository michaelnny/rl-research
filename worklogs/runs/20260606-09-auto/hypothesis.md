# 20260606-09-auto — FFTV (First-Firing-Time Variance Lattice)

## Research Gate

primitive: per-(state, action, channel) empirical *distribution* of within-episode time-to-first-firing for channel `m`, summarized by a robust dispersion functional (interquartile range) — a `|S|·|A|·k` tensor of dispersion scalars `D[s, a, m]`, with the per-(s,a) vector row `D[s, a, :] ∈ ℝ^k` treated as a Pareto-lattice element.
improvement_operator: at each state `s`, nudge policy logits toward actions `a` whose dispersion row `D[s, a, :]` is Pareto-non-dominated in the natural product partial order on `ℝ^k` (no other action has dispersion ≥ in every channel, > in at least one).
side_information: vector diagnostics (per-step channel firings via `info["vector"]`); transition geometry (state-hash for indexing).
nearest_prior_or_disqualifier: #14 Primal Behavior Flow Pivot / GVFs / Successor Features (disqualifier family); also adjacent to RSD (candidate) and DPC (candidate).
falsifier: if on the vector envs (DST and RG) the Pareto-non-dominance gate fires at < 5 % of decision steps after the first 1000 episodes, OR the cells where it does fire are ones whose dispersion vector is Pareto-incomparable in only the always-firing step-penalty channel (so the operator is effectively a single-channel dispersion maximizer = curiosity rebadge), the primitive is dead. Concretely: a per-channel ablation that zeroes out all but the step-penalty channel must score ≪ the full-channel run; if the two scores are within noise the operator is curiosity.

## Mechanism

For each transition step `t` in each rolled-out episode `τ`, record the **time-to-first-firing offset** `T_m(τ, t) = min{ t' − t : t' ≥ t, v_{t'}[m] ≠ 0 }` for every channel `m ∈ {1, …, k}` (set to a sentinel `T_max` if channel `m` never fires later in this episode). For each (state-hash, action) cell `(s, a)`, accumulate the empirical multiset `M[s, a, m] = { T_m(τ, t) : τ visited s and took a at step t }`. The **primitive** is the dispersion vector `D[s, a, m] = IQR(M[s, a, m])` once `|M[s, a, m]| ≥ 4` (otherwise undefined → action excluded from the comparison). The **improvement operator**, evaluated at every decision step, computes the per-action `k`-vector `D[s, ·, :]` over the legal actions, finds the Pareto-non-dominated subset `P(s) = { a : ¬∃ a'  D[s, a', :] ≥ D[s, a, :] strictly }`, and applies a logit nudge `Δlogit(a) = α · 𝟙[a ∈ P(s)] · 1/|P(s)|` (uniform mass spread over the Pareto front). No scalar reward weight, no Bellman target, no critic, no return-to-go, no scalarization of the dispersion vector.

## Required candidate shape

1. **Experience object:** Per-step records `(s_t, a_t, v_t)` from rolled-out episodes, where `v_t ∈ ℝ^k` is the vector signal returned by `info["vector"]`. From these we extract per-cell time-to-first-firing offsets; nothing else is read.
2. **Core primitive:** The per-(state-hash, action, channel) IQR `D[s, a, m]` of the empirical first-firing-time distribution. Each row `D[s, a, :]` is treated as a point in the partial-order lattice `(ℝ^k, ≤)`. This is *not* a value, return, advantage, return-to-go, distributional return, or successor feature — it is a per-channel **dispersion** of a *time-to-event* distribution, which carries no reward-magnitude information (the IQR is invariant to the sign or magnitude of `v_{t'}[m]` — only the *timing* of nonzero firings matters).
3. **Improvement operator:** Pareto-non-dominance vote on the dispersion lattice. At decision time we compute the Pareto front of legal actions w.r.t. their dispersion rows and add `α/|P(s)|` to the logit of each Pareto-non-dominated action. The update is applied as a scheduled offset on top of the (unbiased) softmax sampling distribution, with `α` decayed inversely with `|M[s, a, :]|` so that early-stage noisy IQR estimates contribute less.
4. **Execution rule:** Standard softmax sampling over `logits + Δlogit`, where the base logits come from a small policy network trained as a residual on the dispersion-front signal (technically: minimize cross-entropy between the policy distribution and the indicator distribution over `P(s)` for visited states, with a uniform-prior regularizer). At deployment we keep the same softmax — no greedy switch, since the dispersion vector is intrinsically about *preserving optionality*, and committing to argmax would defeat the preservation rationale.
5. **Vector feedback rule:** The `k` channels are *never* combined. Each channel contributes one coordinate to the dispersion vector. The Pareto-non-dominance test is the only cross-channel computation, and it cannot be expressed as `wᵀD` for any fixed or adaptive `w` because Pareto-non-dominance is not a linear functional. On envs whose channels include a step-penalty term that fires every step, that channel's IQR is identically `0` and is dropped from the dominance test (analogous to ignoring a constant feature in a regression); the remaining `k−1` channels carry the partial order.
6. **Rollout-cost discipline:** **Zero counterfactual rollouts.** The primitive is updated entirely from on-policy ordinary trajectories. One environment step → one update to up to `k` cells (the `(s, a, m)` cells for the current step). At deployment, one decision = one Pareto-front computation over `|A|` action rows of length `k`, which is `O(|A|² · k)` and trivially cheap for our action spaces. No simulator branching, no verifier calls, no paired replays.
7. **Nearest-neighbor novelty audit:** Closest priors are (i) **#14 Primal Behavior Flow Pivot** and the GVF/successor-feature disqualifier family; (ii) **RSD** and **DPC** in the candidates folder. Distinction: GVFs/SF predict the *expected* discounted sum of cumulants `E[Σ γ^t φ(s_t) | s, a]` and use it under a scalar-weight `wᵀψ(s,a)` action selection (a scalarization). FFTV predicts the *intra-episode dispersion* (IQR) of *first-firing latencies*, which is (a) not a successor feature — it is a higher-order moment of an event-time distribution, not an expected cumulant; (b) used through Pareto-non-dominance, never weighted; (c) reward-magnitude-blind by construction. Distinction from RSD: RSD compares *segment cumulants* between paths sharing both endpoints; FFTV does not require segment endpoints to match across trajectories — it simply summarizes within-trajectory event timings. Distinction from DPC: DPC sign-votes on cumulant differences at first-divergence states between trajectory pairs; FFTV requires no pairing at all — every visit to `(s, a)` contributes a single time-to-firing record per channel, and the only cross-trajectory operation is computing the IQR of the resulting multiset.
8. **Predicted failure modes:**
   - **Terminal-only-channel collapse:** on DST/RG the treasure-/resource-channel fires only at the terminal step in successful episodes and never in failed ones, so `T_m` is either `T_remaining` or `T_max`. Once both classes appear the IQR is informative; **before** a single success the channel's IQR is zero (all `T_max`) and it is dropped → the dominance test runs over only the always-firing channels, which collapses to step-penalty IQR maximization (curiosity rebadge). The bootstrap success threshold is the killer.
   - **Deterministic-stationary-policy collapse:** in highly deterministic envs, `T_m(τ, t)` is identical across visits to the same `(s, a)` cell, so IQR ≈ 0 for every action and every cell — Pareto front is the entire action set, operator is a no-op. Mitigation: epsilon-greedy injects variance, but if `ε` is too small the operator stays silent.
   - **Hash collision starvation:** at long horizons, observation hashes for non-trivial environments may have so many unique values that `|M[s, a, m]| ≥ 4` is rarely met. Mitigation: hash truncation / state aggregation, but this is the FED-family bootstrap wall returning by another name.
   - **Optionality is the wrong proxy:** an action that maximizes future-firing-time variance might just be a stalling action (high IQR because half the time it makes no progress and half the time it does). On RG this would manifest as preferring resource-pickup-then-meander over efficient routes.
9. **Side-information channel:** **vector diagnostics** (per-step `info["vector"]` is the only source of channel-firing events) and **transition geometry** (state-hash bucketing for the `(s, a)` cell index). No demonstrations, no pretrained priors, no reward-model, no environment instrumentation beyond what `info["vector"]` already exposes.
10. **Monotonic improvement claim:** Define the policy's **per-channel reach-spread** at state `s` as `R_m(s, π) = E_{a ∼ π(·|s)}[ IQR of T_m | s, a ]` and the policy's **reach-spread vector** `R(s, π) = (R_1, …, R_k)`. The improvement operator monotonically increases `R(s, π)` in the Pareto-lattice sense at every visited state in expectation: the logit nudge places strictly higher mass on Pareto-non-dominated actions, which by definition have dispersion vectors that are not Pareto-dominated, so the expectation under the post-update policy is Pareto-≥ the expectation under the pre-update policy at every channel `m`, with strict inequality on at least one channel whenever the Pareto front is a proper subset of the action set. The condition for this monotonicity is (a) the empirical IQR estimates are unbiased (true asymptotically as `|M[s,a,m]| → ∞`), and (b) the policy has nonzero exploration probability on every legal action (so all cells fill).

## Why it is not GVFs / Successor Features (#14 family)

GVFs and successor features predict an *expected discounted sum of feature activations* `ψ(s,a) = E[Σ γ^t φ(s_t)]`, a vector of *expected magnitudes*, and the standard policy-improvement step at decision time forms `wᵀψ(s,a)` (a scalarization) for either a fixed `w` (multi-objective with preference vector) or a learned `w` (preference-RL). FFTV's primitive is the **IQR of an event-time distribution per channel** — not an expectation, not a sum, not a magnitude — and the improvement step is a Pareto-non-dominance vote that has no representation as `wᵀD` for any `w` (the Pareto operator is not linear). Under variable renaming, FFTV's `D[s, a, :]` cannot be matched to any successor-feature object because dispersion of first-firing times is a higher-order statistic whose computation requires the *full empirical distribution* of `T_m` per cell, not just its mean. The pivot exposes new side information (per-channel firing-time *variability*, not magnitude) that the GVF center cannot represent.

## Why it scales beyond the substrate

At a 20k-action horizon with terminal-only reward, the cheap channels in a vector-feedback setting (per-step cost, latency, validity, safety violations) all fire densely and contribute non-degenerate IQR rows from the very first episode — the dispersion lattice is populated long before the precious channel (success at the end) ever fires. The primitive thus operates throughout the horizon's exploration phase using **dispersion in the cheap channels as a Pareto proxy for keeping options open in the precious channel**, on the inductive prior that an action which collapses the firing-time distribution of latency/cost simultaneously narrows the future-trajectory distribution for the success channel. For LLM-tool-use, channels become "tool-call validity," "API-error firing-time," "user-clarification-firing-time," "task-completion-firing-time," etc.; the same primitive applies — `T_m(τ, t)` is "how many tokens until the next channel-`m` event," IQR is computed over the empirical multiset, and the action `= "generate paragraph"` is selected from a Pareto-non-dominated action set under the partial order on dispersion. The action-space size does not enter the primitive's complexity (which is `O(|A|² · k)` per decision over the *currently active* `|A|` candidate generations, e.g. a beam-of-`|A|` proposals); it scales by replacing the discrete-action enumeration with a candidate-proposal step. The primitive is reward-magnitude-blind, so it does not collapse under terminal-only success; it operates on event timings, which are rich in long-horizon settings.
