## Reasoning

FFTV's primitive is the per-(state-hash, action, channel) IQR of the empirical distribution of *time-to-first-firing offsets* — a higher-order moment of an event-timing distribution — used via Pareto-non-dominance over the resulting dispersion vector. This is structurally distinct from the GVF/successor-feature family (#14 disqualifier): GVFs compute expected discounted sums of cumulant magnitudes and select via `wᵀψ(s,a)` (a linear scalarization); FFTV computes a dispersion statistic (IQR) of event latencies and selects via Pareto-non-dominance, which is not expressible as any `wᵀD`. The primitive is also reward-magnitude-blind by construction (IQR is invariant to the sign or scale of `v_t[m]`), which is a genuine new side-information channel (timing variability rather than magnitude). The method is also structurally distinct from the entire sprint-4 cluster (#15–#23, the "empirical Pareto-front / multiset / hash-collision-gated" family): all prior sprint-4 attempts were silent until hash collisions or terminal channel firings accumulated enough mass; FFTV's dispersion primitive fires from the first episode on the dense channels (step-penalty, validity, latency) because every step contributes a `T_m` record regardless of whether the precious terminal channel has ever fired. The required-candidate-shape slots are all substantively filled: a single composition law (IQR → dispersion vector → Pareto front → uniform logit nudge), a monotonic improvement claim with stated conditions (section 10), declared side-information channels (vector diagnostics + transition geometry), zero counterfactual rollouts (section 6), concrete predicted failure modes (section 8), and a specific falsifier distinguishing multi-channel from single-channel (curiosity) operation.

The remaining structural risk is the collapse scenario the hypothesis itself predicts: on Deep Sea Treasure and Resource Gathering, if the single always-firing step-penalty channel dominates all IQR comparisons, the Pareto-non-dominance test reduces to maximizing step-penalty IQR alone, which is indistinguishable from a novelty/curiosity signal (#5 BCE, #3 KERNEL disqualifier family). The falsifier is well-posed (per-channel ablation must score substantially below the full-channel run), but if that falsifier fires the primitive is dead on these envs. The hypothesis acknowledges this as the "terminal-only-channel collapse" failure mode and correctly notes that the bootstrap success threshold is the killer.

## Risks the Engineer should be aware of

- The improvement operator is silent (Pareto front = entire action set) until `|M[s,a,m]| ≥ 4` per cell per channel. On partially observable envs with large observation spaces (KeyCorridor, Craftax) hash starvation is likely — the Engineer should implement the hash-truncation / state-aggregation mitigation and monitor how often the operator actually fires vs. remains silent during training.
- The step-penalty channel (firing every step) produces a non-degenerate IQR from episode 1, but the precious channel (treasure/resource) has IQR = 0 until first success. The Pareto-non-dominance operator will therefore run on `k−1` channels until first success, and whether that is genuinely multi-dimensional or effectively curiosity depends on what the non-penalty channels are in each env — verify the `info["vector"]` schema for DST and RG before concluding the falsifier test is meaningful.
- The monotonic improvement claim (section 10) holds asymptotically as `|M| → ∞`; in the finite-sample regime early IQR estimates are noisy and the `α` decay schedule is doing significant work. The Engineer should log how quickly `α` decays to verify the operator is still active during the exploration phase rather than being silenced by its own confidence weighting.
