# 20260606-23-auto — ACFC: Action-Frequency / Channel-Frequency Concordance

## Research Gate

primitive: per-(action, channel) signed-concordance matrix `C[a,m] ∈ [-1,1]` formed by averaging, over **trajectory pairs** drawn from a small replay buffer, the product `sign(Δfreq_a) · sign(Δcount_m)` where `Δfreq_a` is the difference in normalized action-`a` frequency between the two trajectories and `Δcount_m` is the difference in total channel-`m` firing count between the two trajectories.
improvement_operator: at every decision step, add a state-independent logit bias `b[a] = α · (n_a^{dom} − n_a^{sub})` to the policy network's logits, where `n_a^{dom}` counts actions `a'` whose concordance row `C[a',:]` is coordinate-wise Pareto-dominated by `C[a,:]` over channels and `n_a^{sub}` counts the reverse. The state-conditional structure of behavior is carried by the policy network; the primitive supplies a global vector-channel-Pareto-respecting action prior.
side_information: vector diagnostics (per-step `info["vector"]`) + transition geometry (action-frequency proportions are computed from the executed action sequence). No observation hash, no clustering, no forward model, no reward labels.
nearest_prior_or_disqualifier: DPC (worklogs/candidates/dpc-divergent-prefix-concordance.md, sign-vote on trajectory-pair first-divergence indexed by state-hash) and KTAC (cluster-conditioned pairwise channel rankings).
falsifier: ACFC fails if either (i) within the 120 s budget, the global concordance matrix `C[a,m]` does not separate actions enough to produce non-zero Pareto domination counts on a majority of decision steps (operator silent), or (ii) the dominance pattern is induced almost entirely by the universal step-penalty channel, in which case ablating the step-penalty channel from `C` should not change the panel score — if it does change, the channel is the only carrier and ACFC is a step-penalty-rebadge.

## Mechanism

ACFC indexes co-variation **across whole episodes**, not across states or state-clusters. Maintain a rolling buffer of `B` recent completed episodes. For each unordered pair `(τ_i, τ_j)` in the buffer, compute the per-action frequency vectors `f^i, f^j ∈ Δ^{|A|}` (action counts divided by episode length) and the per-channel total firing-count vectors `g^i, g^j ∈ R^k` (sum of per-step `v_t` over the episode, or for binary channels the count of firings). Then accumulate `C[a, m] = mean_{i<j} sign(f^i_a − f^j_a) · sign(g^i_m − g^j_m)`, with ties (zero difference) contributing zero. At every decision step the live policy outputs network logits `ℓ_θ(o_t)`; ACFC adds the state-independent bias `b[a] = α · (n_a^{dom}(C) − n_a^{sub}(C))` computed by Pareto comparing rows of `C`. Action sampling is `softmax(ℓ_θ(o_t) + b)`. The network is trained on the **biased policy's own** behavior via a small entropy-regularized supervised target: at every step the supervised target distribution is `softmax(ℓ_θ(o_t) + b)` itself, projected through a stop-gradient on `b`, with no scalar reward weighting and no critic. The primitive `C` and the operator (Pareto domination count) are the entire learning signal. The action-frequency / channel-count signals are reward-independent and dense — every episode contributes a non-zero column to the pair statistic regardless of whether reward was observed — so the operator is not silent during the bootstrap window.

## Required candidate shape

1. **Experience object:** A small ring buffer (size `B ≈ 64`) of completed episodes, each represented as the action sequence (for action-frequency vectors) and the per-step `info["vector"]` sequence (for channel-count vectors). No state-hash, no cluster index, no observation embedding, no reward labels.
2. **Core primitive:** `C[a, m] = mean_{i<j ∈ buffer} sign(f^i_a − f^j_a) · sign(g^i_m − g^j_m)` ∈ R^{|A|×k}. This is the cross-episode rank-1 concordance between the action-frequency profile and the channel-firing-count profile. It is not a Q, V, advantage, return-to-go, return distribution, successor feature, or reward model: it is a sign-correlation between two empirical histograms (over actions and over channels) computed across whole episodes.
3. **Improvement operator:** `b[a] = α · (n_a^{dom} − n_a^{sub})` where `n_a^{dom} = #{a' : ∀m, C[a,m] ≥ C[a',m] ∧ ∃m, C[a,m] > C[a',m]}`. The agent's network is trained to imitate `softmax(ℓ_θ + sg(b))` via cross-entropy on collected steps. No scalar reward weighting, no Bellman, no critic, no advantage. The bias `b` is recomputed at the end of every episode batch from the updated `C`.
4. **Execution rule:** Sample `a ∼ softmax(ℓ_θ(o_t) + b)` at every step. The bias is state-independent (one vector of size `|A|`); state-conditioning is carried by `ℓ_θ(o_t)` whose parameters are updated by the supervised imitation target above.
5. **Vector feedback rule:** The primitive `C[a, m]` retains all `k` channels; the operator is Pareto-non-dominance over channels, never collapsed to a scalar. No fixed or learned channel weights. On envs with `k = 1` ACFC degenerates to a coordinate-wise rank-1 partial order on a scalar, which is total — this is the explicit `k = 1` audit point.
6. **Rollout-cost discipline:** One rollout per environment step, exactly one update per completed episode (recompute `C` and `b` from the current buffer, run one supervised epoch over the buffer). No counterfactual rollouts, no simulator branches, no verifier calls. Replay buffer holds `B = 64` episodes; older episodes are evicted FIFO. At deployment, `b` is fixed; one forward pass per action.
7. **Nearest-neighbor novelty audit:** Closest prior is **DPC** (worklogs/candidates/dpc-divergent-prefix-concordance.md). DPC indexes sign-votes by **state-of-first-divergence** of a trajectory pair and signs by terminal-cumulant difference. ACFC indexes sign-votes by **action-frequency-difference of the whole trajectory** (not by state) and signs by **channel-firing-count-difference of the whole trajectory** (not by terminal cumulant). Two independent structural shifts: (a) the index is action-side (frequency profile), not state-side (divergence hash) — eliminating hash-collision/cluster-revisitation bootstrap walls; (b) the sign is channel-firing-count, not terminal cumulant — providing dense signal even on sparse-reward envs because the step-penalty channel and any other non-terminal channel contribute on every episode. Second-closest prior is **KTAC** (cluster-conditioned pairwise rankings); ACFC has no clustering and no per-channel ranking — just sign concordance.
8. **Predicted failure modes:**
   - (a) **Collinear-channel collapse on RG.** If on Resource Gathering the only channel that varies meaningfully across episodes within budget is step-penalty (because gold/gem channels are terminal-only and never fire before first success), `C[a, :]` becomes effectively rank-1 and Pareto domination reduces to step-penalty maximization (i.e. fastest-termination preference). Detection: ablate the step-penalty channel; if the Pareto vote pattern collapses, the failure mode is confirmed.
   - (b) **Identical-frequency degeneracy.** When a policy is nearly deterministic, all episodes have the same action-frequency vector, so `Δfreq_a = 0` for every pair and `C` is identically zero. ACFC must therefore start from a high-entropy policy or include an explicit minimum-entropy floor on the action distribution.
   - (c) **Sign-concordance washout under high stochasticity.** On envs with stochastic transitions where a fixed action sequence yields highly variable channel counts, the sign of `Δcount_m` is dominated by transition noise rather than action choice; `C` regresses to zero. RG is the most likely venue for this; the run will either confirm or refute.
   - (d) **k=1 collapse.** On envs without vector feedback (or where only one channel is informative), Pareto-non-dominance reduces to scalar maximization — possible scalarization rebadge. The DST/RG vector envs are the discriminating tests.
   - (e) **Aggregation grain mismatch.** Whole-episode aggregation may be too coarse to discriminate actions on long-horizon envs where a few critical actions are needed. If so, episode-windowed variants (e.g. half-episode, quartile-windowed) become a follow-up. The first run uses whole-episode for simplicity.
9. **Side-information channel:** **vector diagnostics** (per-step `info["vector"]` driving channel-firing counts) and **transition geometry** (action sequences from the executed trajectory). No observation embedding, no reward labels, no learned dynamics, no event traces, no demonstrations.
10. **Monotonic improvement claim:** Under the assumption that channel-firing-count differences are sign-concordant with action-frequency differences (the empirical concordance matrix `C` is consistent), the Pareto-non-dominance bias `b[a]` is a non-strict-improvement direction in the **partial order on channel-firing-count expectations**: increasing the relative frequency of any Pareto-dominant action in `C` raises the expected channel-count vector under a coordinate-wise partial order, in expectation, in the limit of infinite buffer size and stationary transition dynamics. Concretely: if `C[a, :]` strictly dominates `C[a', :]`, then increasing `f_a` and decreasing `f_{a'}` cannot strictly decrease any `E[g_m]` and strictly increases at least one. This is the partial-order analogue of Bellman improvement on a multi-criterion expectation; the channel weights are never collapsed to scalar.

## Why it is not DPC

DPC accumulates sign-votes only at **state-of-first-divergence** events of trajectory pairs and signs the votes by terminal cumulant. The primitive is silent at any state where pair-divergence-event records are sparse, and on RG it failed because divergence events cannot be reliably attributed to channel-specific outcomes when transitions are stochastic. ACFC drops the state-side index entirely — its primitive is a single global `|A|×k` matrix accumulated over **whole-episode statistics** of action and channel frequencies — so it is not subject to divergence-state-cell sparsity, and it signs by **firing-count** differences rather than terminal-cumulant differences, which gives non-trivial signal from non-terminal channels (step-penalty, validity, intermediate event channels) before any reward is observed. Under variable renaming DPC's inner statistic is `sign(c_T(τ_i) − c_T(τ_j))[m]` indexed by `(s_div, a, a')`; ACFC's is `sign(g^i_m − g^j_m) · sign(f^i_a − f^j_a)` indexed only by `(a, m)` — different index, different sign source.

## Why it scales beyond the substrate

At 20k-action horizons, the **whole-episode action-frequency vector** is exactly the kind of statistic that becomes more reliable, not less, as horizon grows: longer episodes yield more action samples, so frequency estimates concentrate. Channel-firing counts likewise concentrate at long horizon; sign-concordance is a robust statistic that is invariant to magnitude scaling and to per-episode length normalization. On LLM-tool-use settings where actions are "generate a paragraph" or "call API X", the action-frequency vector is over the tool/template inventory (or over a small embedding-cluster id of paragraph type — the only auxiliary needed is a discrete partition of the action space, which any LLM agent already exposes via tool names or template IDs). The channel-firing-count vector is the per-step vector feedback (success, cost, safety, latency, validity, preference) summed over the episode, which is already provided by modern tool-using agentic systems. The primitive is `O(|A|·k)` storage and `O(B²·|A|·k)` recomputation per buffer-update — independent of horizon. The Pareto-non-dominance operator is `O(|A|²·k)` at decision time. None of this scales adversely with horizon, and none of it requires per-step state hashing, clustering, dense rewards, forward models, verifiers, or hand-engineered event lenses — exactly the components that would otherwise be infeasible at 20k-action LLM-agent scale.
