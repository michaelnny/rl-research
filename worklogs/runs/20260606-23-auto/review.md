## Reasoning

ACFC's central primitive is a cross-episode sign-concordance matrix `C[a,m]` formed by averaging, over trajectory pairs in a ring buffer, the product `sign(Δfreq_a) · sign(Δcount_m)` — where both dimensions are whole-episode aggregates (action-frequency profile on the action side, channel-firing-count on the channel side). This is structurally distinct from every disqualifier family and every prior attempt. It is not a Q/V/advantage/return (no temporal recursion, no scalar future compression). It is not a scalar-weighted log-prob update (the improvement operator is a Pareto-non-dominance count over concordance rows, never a scalar weight multiplied against log-prob). It is not elite cloning or CEM (no trajectory selection by return rank). It is not count-based exploration (the bias `b[a]` is not a novelty bonus; it is a directional signal in action-frequency space derived from channel co-variation). The nearest prior is DPC, and the structural separation is genuine: DPC is indexed by state-of-first-divergence and signed by terminal cumulant — both of which create the state-hash bootstrap wall that killed DPC and the entire FED/CEC/TPP/CWTP family. ACFC drops the state-side index entirely and signs by channel-firing-count over the whole episode, making the primitive dense from the first episode pair even before any reward is observed (the step-penalty channel and any non-terminal channel contribute non-zero signs on every episode). All required-candidate-shape slots are substantively filled: primitive, improvement operator, execution rule, vector-feedback rule, rollout-cost discipline, side-information channel declaration, monotonic improvement claim under the partial-order assumption, and five concrete predicted failure modes each with a named ablation falsifier.

The monotonic improvement claim is appropriately scoped: it holds under the assumption that channel-firing-count differences are sign-concordant with action-frequency differences in expectation, in the large-buffer limit, under stationary dynamics. The improvement operator then defines a partial-order improvement step on the expected channel-count vector — the channel-Pareto analogue of policy improvement. The hypothesis is honest that this is asymptotic and assumption-dependent, and the failure modes spell out exactly when those assumptions break (collinear-channel collapse, identical-frequency degeneracy, transition noise washout, k=1 scalar collapse, whole-episode coarseness).

## Risks the Engineer should be aware of

- The k=1 degenerate case (failure mode d) is a live risk on DoorKey and KeyCorridor if the step-penalty channel is the only informative one throughout the budget. The hypothesis's own ablation (remove step-penalty from C; check whether Pareto vote pattern collapses) is the correct gate — the Engineer should instrument this ablation before treating any panel score as evidence of the mechanism working.
- The supervised imitation target (train network to imitate `softmax(ℓ_θ + sg(b))`) is an unusual update rule with no convergence guarantee beyond the stationary-dynamics assumption. On envs where `b` shifts rapidly (e.g. when the first successful episode arrives and flips sign concordances), the imitation target can chase a moving signal and cause instability. An adaptive step-size or EMA on `b` before applying the imitation target may be needed.
- On Resource Gathering, the gold and gem channels are terminal-only (failure mode a); if `C` degenerates to step-penalty rank-1, the run confirms the collinear-channel collapse and rules out this family on purely terminal-only vector channels — that would be a clean falsification result worth recording even if the overall panel score is low.
