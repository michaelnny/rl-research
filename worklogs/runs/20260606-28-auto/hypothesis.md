# 20260606-28-auto — PRAR (Policy-Regime Antisymmetric Residual)

## Research Gate

primitive: per-(policy-regime, action, channel) episode-position-antisymmetric firing residual `F[r, a, m] ∈ R`, accumulated over completed episodes, where the regime tag is computed from the policy's own action-distribution shape at decision time and the residual is a fixed antisymmetric within-episode-position weighting of channel deviations from per-episode means
improvement_operator: at decision time, Pareto-non-dominance vote on the k-vector `F[r_t, a, :]` across the legal action set; logit nudge `α (D_+(a) − D_-(a))` where `D_+(a)` is the count of legal actions coordinate-wise dominated by `a` and `D_-(a)` is the count dominating it
side_information: vector diagnostics (per-step `info["vector"]`) + the policy's own action-distribution shape (regime classifier is computed from `π(·|o_t)`, not from environment instrumentation)
nearest_prior_or_disqualifier: KTAC (worklogs/candidates/ktac-kendall-tau-action-concordance.md) — both pivot indexing away from state-clusters; KTAC indexes by penultimate-feature k-means clusters, PRAR indexes by policy-distribution-shape regimes (entropy/top-prob/top-2-gap bins), and the primitive object is fundamentally different (position-weighted residual vs. pairwise dominance probability matrix)
falsifier: on the vector envs (DST, RG), if the regime classifier maps ≥ 90% of decision steps into a single regime within the 120 s budget, OR if the per-(regime, action) cell-population diagnostic shows < 5 episodes per cell at end-of-budget, the primitive is silent and PRAR should score at-or-below-random — concretely, PRAR scoring < random on both vector envs falsifies the regime-as-replacement-for-state-cluster claim

## Mechanism

The primitive is `F[r, a, m] = (1/N[r,a]) Σ_{episodes E} Σ_{t : regime(π_E, o_t)=r, a_t=a} w(t / T_E) · (v_t[m] − μ_E[m])` where `μ_E[m] = (1/T_E) Σ_{s} v_s[m]` is episode E's mean of channel m, `w(s) = 2s − 1` is a fixed antisymmetric position-weight on `s ∈ [0,1]`, and `N[r,a]` counts contributing (episode, step) pairs. The regime tag is `regime(π, o) = (bin(H(π)), bin(max_a π(a|o)), bin(top1_prob − top2_prob))` — a small discrete code (e.g. 3×3×3 = 27 regimes) computed from the policy's distribution shape with no observation features. F is non-zero from episode 1 on every channel: for terminal-only channels `μ_E[m] ≈ 1/T_E` and the late firing carries `w(1) = 1` while early non-firings carry `w(s<0.5) < 0`, so F encodes whether action `a` in regime `r` co-occurs with **late-episode firings** of channel m (which on terminal-only-reward envs is precisely "this action-in-regime tends to end up in successful episodes"); for dense always-on channels (step-penalty) μ_E ≈ −1 and constant deviation cancels, so F encodes higher-order temporal structure of when the action-regime co-occurs with within-episode firing-rate departures. The improvement operator is the standard Pareto-non-dominance vote in coordinate-wise partial order on R^k, applied to F[r_t, a, :] at decision time; logits are nudged by `α (D_+(a) − D_-(a))`. There is no critic, no Bellman backup, no scalarization, no cluster k-means, and no observation hash; the regime is a function of the policy's own output distribution.

## Required candidate shape

1. **Experience object:** completed episodes, each consisting of a sequence of `(o_t, a_t, π(·|o_t), v_t)` tuples up to terminal step `T_E`. The policy distribution `π(·|o_t)` is recorded at decision time so the regime tag can be computed online or replayed offline; the vector `v_t = info["vector"]` is the k-channel feedback signal.

2. **Core primitive:** the regime-action-channel residual tensor `F ∈ R^{R × |A| × k}` with `F[r, a, m] = (1/N[r,a]) Σ_E Σ_{t: regime(π_E, o_t)=r, a_t=a} w(t/T_E) (v_t[m] − μ_E[m])`. Updated incrementally as a running mean. The **regime** is `regime(π, o) = (H_bin, p_top1_bin, gap_bin)` where each bin is a small fixed-edge histogram (e.g. 3 quantile bins each, total 27 regimes). The **antisymmetric weight** `w(s) = 2s − 1` is fixed.

3. **Improvement operator:** at each decision step, compute `r_t = regime(π_θ(·|o_t))`, look up F[r_t, a, :] for each action a, count Pareto-non-dominance margins `D_+(a) = #{a' ≠ a : F[r_t, a, :] ≻ F[r_t, a', :] coordinate-wise (≥ all, > some)}` and `D_-(a) = #{a' ≠ a : F[r_t, a', :] ≻ F[r_t, a, :]}`, then nudge raw logits by `α (D_+(a) − D_-(a))`. No supervised target; no log-prob weighting by scalar; the operator updates *only the policy logits*, never any value or auxiliary head.

4. **Execution rule:** sample actions from `softmax(logits + α·nudge)` where `nudge[a] = D_+(a) − D_-(a)`. Logit storage between decisions is unchanged; the nudge is applied per-step, not as a stored bias. There is no greedy mode; sampling is stochastic throughout training and at evaluation with temperature τ=1.

5. **Vector feedback rule:** k-vector `v_t` is consumed entry-wise into F's third axis; channel-independent residuals are aggregated independently per channel. The Pareto-non-dominance comparison is the only cross-channel operation, and it is the coordinate-wise partial order on R^k — no fixed weights, no learned weights, no sup-norm. F is updated per-channel from per-channel residuals; channels are never combined into a scalar prior to the Pareto vote.

6. **Rollout-cost discipline:** one environment step per F update (incremental running-mean accumulation). Zero counterfactual rollouts, zero verifier calls, zero simulator branches. Per accepted improvement: F is updated continuously per step; logit nudges happen per decision step at no extra cost. At deployment: no F updates, just the same logit-nudge readout from the frozen F tensor — one tensor-lookup and Pareto-vote per decision.

7. **Nearest-neighbor novelty audit:** Closest priors are (a) KTAC (alive-weak, worklogs/candidates/ktac-kendall-tau-action-concordance.md), which uses k-means context-clusters and Kemeny consensus over per-channel pairwise dominance rankings — different indexing (state-feature clusters, not policy-shape regimes) and different operator (rank-aggregation, not Pareto-non-dominance count); and (b) DPC (alive-weak, worklogs/candidates/dpc-divergent-prefix-concordance.md), which uses trajectory-pair first-divergence sign-vote indexed by divergence-state hash — different indexing (event-based, not regime-based) and different primitive (per-pair sign vote, not position-weighted episode-mean residual). Most importantly, neither uses **policy-distribution-shape** as the indexing axis or **antisymmetric within-episode-position weighting** as the residual structure; both of those are the load-bearing structural choices.

8. **Predicted failure modes:** (a) Regime collapse — if the policy's distribution shape is nearly constant throughout training (e.g. always near-uniform during early exploration), every step maps to the same regime, F reduces to per-(action, channel) global position-weighted residual, and informativeness drops to a scalar-channel rank. Diagnostic: regime-occupancy histogram should show ≥ 3 regimes with ≥ 5% mass each. (b) Cell starvation — if regime × action cells are too sparsely populated within budget, the running mean is dominated by the first 1–2 episodes and is high-variance noise; mitigated by Laplace smoothing on F and a minimum-sample gate (`α=0` for cells with `N[r,a] < 5`). (c) Antisymmetric-weight cancellation on dense always-on channels — if `v_t[m]` is constant within an episode (e.g. step-penalty fires every step with magnitude 1), `v_t[m] − μ_E[m] = 0` and that channel's F entry is zero; this is a feature for the step-penalty channel (it should not contribute) but it shrinks `k_eff` to terminal-only channels which on the substrate is k_eff=1, reducing the Pareto vote to scalar comparison on the surviving channel. Diagnostic: per-channel non-zero-fraction of F entries — if only 1 channel has non-zero F entries, the operator has structurally collapsed. (d) Stochastic transitions on RG — terminal-only treasure/gold/gem channels with stochastic transitions still have non-zero `μ_E` and `v_t` deviations, but the stochasticity inflates within-cell variance of F and may require larger N[r,a] before the Pareto signal emerges; concretely, RG may fail unless cell-mass diagnostics show ≥ 20 episodes per active cell. (e) Action-symmetry break by regime — if regime classification is itself a deterministic function of the policy shape and the policy shape is downstream of the policy parameters that the operator updates, there is feedback: nudging logits changes the regime, which changes F readouts, which changes nudges. Stability requires α small enough that regime drift is slower than F's running-mean update rate; concretely α ≤ 0.05 / k.

9. **Side-information channel:** vector diagnostics (per-step `info["vector"]`) + the policy's own internal action-distribution shape. The regime classifier is **not** environment instrumentation — it reads `π_θ(·|o_t)` which is computed by the agent itself, not provided by the env. No event-trace lens, no hand-engineered segment boundaries, no demonstrations, no transition-geometry hashing.

10. **Monotonic improvement claim:** under fixed F (no further updates), the logit-nudge step monotonically increases the expected coordinate-wise dominance count `Σ_a π(a|o) · D_+(a)` over the current regime's F slice — this is a finite-step deterministic argmax-like move on a fixed discrete partial order, monotone by construction. With F updating concurrently, monotonicity holds in expectation over a full pass when the policy's regime distribution is approximately stationary (regime drift slower than F update rate), giving an **expected coordinate-wise non-dominance margin improvement per regime** as the monotonic invariant. The claim is **not** monotonic improvement of any scalar reward, return-to-go, or value — it is monotonic improvement of a vector-Pareto-ranked statistic.

## Why it is not KTAC / DPC / scalarized vector reward

KTAC (worklogs/candidates/ktac-kendall-tau-action-concordance.md) indexes by k-means context-clusters and computes pairwise dominance probabilities then Kemeny rank-aggregates them into a consensus permutation. PRAR indexes by **policy-shape regime** (a function of the agent's own output distribution, populated every step regardless of state-feature similarity) and uses antisymmetric position-weighted firing residuals as the primitive — neither the index nor the primitive matches under variable renaming. DPC indexes by trajectory-pair first-divergence-state hashes and uses sign-votes from terminal cumulant differences; PRAR has no pair structure, no divergence-state index, and the residual is per-step position-weighted, not per-trajectory-pair sign. Against the **scalarized vector reward** disqualifier: PRAR maintains F per-channel and the Pareto-non-dominance count is invariant under any monotone rescaling of any single channel — it is not equivalent to any fixed `w^T r` weighting because the dominance partial order is preserved under per-channel monotone transformation while linear scalarization is not.

## Why it scales beyond the substrate

The regime-as-index property is the load-bearing scalability claim. At LLM scale, an "action" is "generate a paragraph" and the policy is a transformer producing a token-distribution; the regime can be computed from any cheap feature of that distribution (token-level entropy, top-token-probability, top-2-margin, or distribution-over-canonical-prompt-templates) without enumerating actions. F's third axis remains tiny (k = 6–10 for typical vector feedback: success, cost, safety, latency, validity, preference) and the regime axis stays fixed-cardinality regardless of horizon. For 20k-action horizons, the antisymmetric position-weight `w(t/T)` scales naturally because it is normalized by `T_E`; episode position is the only horizon-coupled quantity and it enters as a normalized scalar. The operator's per-decision cost is `O(R · |A| · k)` for the Pareto vote — not per-state cost, not per-trajectory cost — so it remains tractable when the action space is "any sequence of tokens" (one only needs to compute F[r, a, :] for the *sampled* candidate actions at decision time, not enumerate the full action space).
