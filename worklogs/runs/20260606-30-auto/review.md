## Reasoning

CSA's central primitive — the per-channel Fiedler vector of a channel-reweighted observation-transition Laplacian, with per-(action, channel) Fiedler-ascent `Phi[a, m]` derived from the second eigenvector's global structure — is structurally distinct from every disqualifier family and every prior attempt. The nearest named disqualifier is GVFs / successor features, and the hypothesis's own structural-distinction paragraph correctly identifies the key difference: successor features define a per-state expected future cumulant `E[sum gamma^t v_t[m] | s_0, pi]`, which is computed by Bellman recursion and subsequently scalarized via `w^T psi`. The Fiedler vector satisfies `L_m f_m = lambda_2 f_m` — a global eigenvector equation over the whole empirical transition graph, not a per-state recursion — and `Phi[a, m]` is the average step that action `a` induces along this global partition coordinate. The improvement operator (Pareto-non-dominance count `n_a - m_a` over `Phi[a, :] ∈ R^k`) does not reduce to `argmax_a w^T Phi[a, :]` for any fixed or learned `w`, and does not satisfy any Bellman equation. The distinction from #14 Primal Behavior Flow also holds: occupancy flow `mu_pi(s,a)` is a per-state-action local object tied to a specific policy, while the Fiedler vector encodes the dominant bottleneck cut of the full empirical graph regardless of the generating policy. All required candidate-shape slots are filled with substantive content: the primitive is precisely stated, the improvement operator has a concrete update rule, the side-information channels (transition geometry and vector diagnostics) are explicitly named and non-trivially used, four predicted failure modes with falsifiers are given, the monotonic improvement claim is articulated (per-channel structural alignment `A_m(pi)` is increased coordinate-wise under small enough `alpha` when the eigenvalue gap is non-degenerate), and the scaling argument beyond the substrate is provided.

The principal open question is whether the step-penalty channel's Laplacian yields a non-degenerate Fiedler partition early enough to guide exploration on long-horizon sparse envs — the hypothesis acknowledges this as failure mode (a) with an instrumented falsifier (per-channel eigenvalue gap logged every K episodes, abort if gap < 1e-6 on >= k-1 channels for the full budget). If the step-penalty channel alone produces a Fiedler vector that encodes meaningful topology of the visited state space before any reward appears, CSA structurally bypasses the FED-family bootstrap wall; if not, the candidate collapses to single-channel scalar Fiedler-ascent maximization as stated. The falsifier is honest and the candidate is evaluable.

## Risks the Engineer should be aware of

- The per-channel eigenvalue gap falsifier (gap < 1e-6 on >= k-1 channels for the full budget) should be instrumented from episode 1, not deferred to post-hoc analysis; the 120 s budget may expire before the falsifier condition is even checkable if Lanczos is not set up early.
- Observation-hash bucket revisitation (failure mode b) on MiniGrid long corridors may be structurally too sparse to support a non-trivial Laplacian partition — the mean-bucket-revisit-count diagnostic (threshold 2.0) should be logged per-env, not just in aggregate, since DoorKey-8x8 and KeyCorridor may differ substantially.
- The `delta_logit` is action-indexed but not state-indexed at the read site (`Phi` is action-conditioned only); on envs where optimal action varies significantly by state (KeyCorridor has state-dependent sub-goal requirements), a global Phi[a, :] that pools over all states may wash out the useful per-state structure and degrade below the entropy-regularized base policy.
