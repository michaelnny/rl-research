# 20260606-30-auto — CSA (Channel-Spectral Action-Influence)

## Research Gate

primitive: per-channel Fiedler vector of a channel-reweighted observation-transition Laplacian; per-(action, channel) expected Fiedler-ascent induced by the action.
improvement_operator: Pareto-non-dominance logit nudge over the k-vector of per-channel Fiedler-ascents Phi[a, :].
side_information: [transition geometry, vector diagnostics].
nearest_prior_or_disqualifier: GVFs / successor features (disqualifier family) and #14 Primal Behavior Flow Pivot.
falsifier: if for every panel env, fewer than 2 channels yield a non-degenerate Fiedler vector within the 120 s budget (numerical-rank check on the channel-reweighted Laplacian, eigenvalue gap < 1e-6 or top-eigenvector collinear with the all-ones vector), the operator collapses to single-channel scalar Fiedler-ascent maximization (a scalarized graph-flow rebadge) and the candidate is dead.

## Mechanism

Maintain an empirical observation-transition multigraph G = (V, E) over an online-grown vocabulary of observation-hash buckets V; each edge (u -> v) carries a k-vector of accumulated channel signals
`W_m(u, v) = sum_{(o_t, o_{t+1}) -> (u, v)} v_t[m]`
(plus a base count W_0(u,v) for the unweighted backbone). For each channel m, form the symmetrized weighted Laplacian L_m = D_m - (W_m + W_m^T)/2 (over the W_m + epsilon * W_0 backbone to keep the graph connected before the channel fires anywhere) and extract the Fiedler vector f_m in R^|V| — the eigenvector of the second-smallest eigenvalue. The central per-(action, channel) primitive is
`Phi[a, m] = (1/N_a) sum_{(o_t, a_t = a, o_{t+1})} ( f_m( h(o_{t+1}) ) - f_m( h(o_t)) )`
the average Fiedler-coordinate ascent action a induces on the channel-m-weighted graph. The improvement operator at decision time reads off the current observation's hash bucket u_t = h(o_t), and for each candidate action a, applies a logit nudge
`delta_logit(a) = alpha * ( n_a - m_a )`
where n_a is the count of actions a' such that Phi[a, :] coordinate-wise strictly dominates Phi[a', :] (Pareto-non-dominance count) and m_a is the reverse count. f_m is recomputed every K episodes by Lanczos / Arnoldi on the sparse Laplacian; W_m, Phi[a, m], and Phi[a, :] are accumulated online. No critic, no Bellman, no scalar reward weight, no per-cell revisit precondition — every transition contributes one edge weight to every channel's graph.

## Required candidate shape

1. **Experience object:** ordinary trajectories of (o_t, a_t, v_t in R^k, o_{t+1}) — per-step vector-channel feedback already exposed by the substrate via `info["vector"]`. No reset hooks, no expert annotation, no segment grammar.

2. **Core primitive:** the per-(action, channel) Fiedler-ascent vector Phi[a, :] in R^k, derived from the second eigenvector f_m of the channel-m-reweighted observation-transition Laplacian L_m. Phi is a global spectral statistic of the experience graph, not a per-state value function and not a per-trajectory return.

3. **Improvement operator:** logit nudge `delta_logit(a) = alpha * (n_a - m_a)` where n_a, m_a are Pareto-non-dominance counts on Phi[a, :] under the coordinate-wise partial order on R^k. No scalarization, no Bellman backup, no log-prob × reward weighting. The update is an additive bias on the policy's pre-softmax logits at each decision step; the policy is otherwise an ordinary categorical-action network trained only by entropy regularization plus this nudge (no actor-critic, no PPO loss, no DQN target).

4. **Execution rule:** at each decision step, the policy samples from softmax(logits_theta(o_t) + delta_logit(:)). delta_logit is read from a small online table indexed by action only (Phi is action-conditioned, not state-conditioned at the read site — the state-conditioning is folded into Phi during accumulation). No look-ahead, no MCTS, no verifier call.

5. **Vector feedback rule:** Phi[a, :] is in R^k throughout; the Pareto-non-dominance count operator is invariant to monotone per-channel rescaling and never collapses to w^T Phi for any fixed or learned w. The k channels enter the Laplacian construction independently (one Laplacian per channel) and are aggregated only at the final partial-order vote — exactly the structure the panel's vector envs are designed to detect.

6. **Rollout-cost discipline:** one environment interaction per gradient step, identical to vanilla on-policy training. The Fiedler eigendecomposition is the only extra cost; on a substrate with at most O(10^4) observation-hash buckets and k=2..6 channels, sparse Lanczos costs O(|E| * k * iterations) and is amortized over K=4 episodes between recomputations. No counterfactual rollouts, no simulator branches, no verifier calls, no extra trajectories per accepted update.

7. **Nearest-neighbor novelty audit:** the closest disqualifier family is GVFs / successor features (and the closest *attempt* is #14 Primal Behavior Flow Pivot, which pivoted to occupancy flow mu_pi(s, a) and was ruled out as not exposing new side information). The structural distinction: SF / GVF / behavior-flow define a per-state expected discounted future cumulant E[sum gamma^t v_t[m] | s_0 = s, pi], a *local* future-prediction object used as a value-function surrogate that is then scalarized via w^T to recover Q. CSA defines a *global* graph-Laplacian eigenvector f_m — a partition statistic, not a per-state value — and uses Pareto-non-dominance on per-channel ascents, never scalarized. The Fiedler vector is the smoothed indicator of the dominant cut of the channel-reweighted graph; it cannot be obtained by any linear combination of single-policy successor features, and the improvement operator is partial-order-based rather than scalar-Q-extraction-based.

8. **Predicted failure modes:**
   - **(a) Channel-degeneracy collapse:** for any channel m whose v_t[m] is zero on all but a vanishingly small fraction of transitions (terminal-only reward channels before first success), L_m's eigenvalue gap is dominated by the epsilon-backbone term and f_m is near-collinear with the all-ones vector — Phi[a, m] approx 0 for all a, k_eff drops by one. If this happens for k - 1 channels simultaneously, the operator collapses to scalar Fiedler-ascent maximization on the surviving channel (typically step-penalty), reproducing the FED/PRAR/ATP failure mode. **Falsifier instrumented: log per-channel eigenvalue gap and rank of L_m every K episodes; abort the candidate if gap < 1e-6 on >= k - 1 channels for the entire 120 s budget.**
   - **(b) Hash-collision starvation:** if the observation-hash bucketing produces near-singleton buckets (one observation per bucket on long horizons with high-dimensional pixels), |V| grows linearly with experience and the graph is a tree-like structure with vacuous Fiedler partition. **Diagnostic: log mean bucket revisit count; if < 2.0 across the whole training run, the graph is too thin to support spectral structure.** This is a known long-horizon failure shared with FED/SIT/CEC; the falsifier subsumes those.
   - **(c) Stochastic-transition smoothing:** on Resource Gathering, transition stochasticity makes W_m matrices nearly uniform across edge endpoints, washing out the Fiedler partition. Predicted: Phi[a, :] near-uniform across actions on RG, Pareto vote degenerate, beat_random = 0 on RG. This is the same failure mode CWAI / DPC hit on RG.
   - **(d) Spectral-update lag:** Phi is recomputed every K=4 episodes; if K is too large, Phi is stale; if K = 1, the eigendecomposition cost dominates the 120 s budget. The candidate is sensitive to K and to Lanczos iteration count.

9. **Side-information channel:** **transition geometry** (the observation-transition graph used to build L_m) and **vector diagnostics** (`info["vector"]` per-step k-vector reweighting the edge weights). No expert demonstrations, no event lens, no environment instrumentation beyond what every panel candidate uses, no reset structure, no language description. The side-information declaration is non-trivially used: without per-channel reweighting the candidate would compute a single Fiedler vector and trivially scalarize.

10. **Monotonic improvement claim:** define the policy's *channel-m structural alignment* as A_m(pi) = E_{(o, a) ~ d_pi} [ Phi[a, m] ] — the policy's expected Fiedler ascent under its own occupancy. Under the assumption that the Fiedler-vector estimate is stable (eigenvalue gap > epsilon) and the Pareto-non-dominance margin is non-zero somewhere, the logit nudge increases A_m(pi) for every channel m on which the chosen Pareto-non-dominated action has positive Phi[a, m] - mean_a' Phi[a', m], in expectation under a small enough alpha. The monotone proxy A_m(pi) is a per-channel partition-flow alignment — a graph-Cheeger-like quantity — and the operator monotonically improves this k-vector quantity in the coordinate-wise partial order whenever the Pareto vote is non-empty. This is not a return improvement claim and is not a value-function improvement claim; it is the structural-alignment analogue.

## Why it is not GVFs / successor features (or #14 Primal Behavior Flow)

GVFs / successor features and behavior-flow learn a *local* per-state expected future occupancy or cumulant — an object indexed by starting state and used as a Q-surrogate after scalarization. CSA's central object is a *global* eigenvector of a channel-reweighted Laplacian, indexed by the whole graph at once, whose entries are partition-coordinates rather than expected-future-rewards; the improvement operator is Pareto-non-dominance on per-channel ascents and never reduces to w^T M psi for any policy-evaluation matrix M and weight w. Concretely: with a single channel and a fixed scalarization, SF gives Q-learning while CSA gives a graph-Cheeger-cut alignment heuristic that does not satisfy any Bellman recursion and cannot extract argmax_a Q(s, a) — the partial-order over Phi[a, :] does not totally order actions when k >= 2.

## Why it scales beyond the substrate

At long horizons (20 k actions), per-trajectory bootstrap targets vanish but the *transition graph* keeps growing — every step contributes one edge — and the second-eigenvector partition of the graph remains well-defined regardless of how rare reward is. The Laplacian eigenvector is a long-range structural object: it encodes the dominant bottleneck cut of the whole experience graph, exactly the kind of summary that local TD bootstrapping cannot recover at depth 10^4. At LLM-tool-use scale, "observation hash" becomes "tool-trace embedding cluster" (the same online-grown vocabulary technique applies to any embedding bucketed by k-means or random projection); the per-channel reweighting accommodates 6+ vector channels (success / cost / safety / latency / validity / preference) directly without weight tuning, because the Pareto-non-dominance vote treats each channel as its own Laplacian. The operator's per-update cost is one sparse Lanczos pass plus a Pareto count over |A| actions — sublinear in trajectory length, so the substrate-cost story does not break when the horizon grows by 1000x.
