# 20260606-11-auto — ACS (Action-Conditional Suffix-Spectrum)

## Research Gate

primitive: per-(state-cluster, action) k×F matrix `S[s,a]` whose entry `S[s,a,m,f]` is the empirical *spectral density at temporal frequency `f`* of channel `m`'s firing-indicator time series in the suffix following visits to `(s,a)`. Spectral density is computed by sliding-window autocorrelation (no FFT required) at a fixed bank of F log-spaced lag bins.
improvement_operator: at each decision, compute per-action k×F dispersion-normalised spectral matrix; nudge policy logits toward actions whose flattened (k·F)-vector is Pareto-non-dominated across the candidate-action set, where dominance is coordinate-wise on the (channel × frequency) product set.
side_information: vector diagnostics, transition geometry.
nearest_prior_or_disqualifier: FFTV (`fftv-first-firing-time-variance-lattice.md`, alive-promising) — single-statistic-per-channel timing dispersion; ACS generalises to a multi-frequency-band spectrum per channel.
falsifier: if on the panel ACS does not strictly outperform a single-frequency (DC-only or single-lag) ablation of itself on at least one panel env, then the multi-frequency stratification is structurally vacuous and the family collapses to FFTV under variable renaming. Additionally, if on Resource Gathering the per-channel frequency-band fill-rate (fraction of (s,a,m,f) cells with ≥ 5 samples by 60 s) for any non-step-penalty channel `m` stays below 5 %, the primitive is bootstrap-degenerate on that channel — the FFTV-on-RG failure repeats and the family is ruled out for terminal-only-channel substrates.

## Mechanism

The central object is a **suffix spectrum** of channel firings. After the buffer collects trajectories, for each visit `(o_t, a_t)` to a state-hash (or feature-cluster) `s` we look at the suffix indicator vector `1_m(t+τ) ∈ {0,1}` for each channel `m` and lag `τ`, and accumulate, for each frequency-band `f` (log-spaced lag bins `[2^f, 2^{f+1})`), the empirical *variance of the within-band mean firing-rate* — `S[s,a,m,f] = Var_τ∈band_f(1_m(t+τ))` averaged across visits. Equivalently this is the second-moment of the windowed autocorrelation at lag `f`. Two spectral matrices `S[s,a]` and `S[s,a']` are compared by coordinate-wise partial order on the flattened (k·F)-vector after channel-MAD standardisation. The improvement operator is a single Pareto-non-dominance logit nudge: if action `a*` has spectral row Pareto-non-dominated by all others' rows at state `s`, add `+α` to its logit and `−α/(|A|−1)` to others'. No critic, no scalar weighting, no Bellman backup, no return target. The primitive engages from episode 1 on any channel that fires more than once per episode (the dense step-penalty / validity channels, step-state-change channels, etc.), because each band collects samples on every step where the channel index is read. The structural claim is that *which temporal scales a channel fires at* is a richer action-discriminator than *how dispersed its first-firing time is* (FFTV) — actions that drive a channel to fire on long-period vs short-period scales encode genuinely different kinds of futures, not detectable from a single timing-dispersion statistic.

## Required candidate shape

1. **Experience object:** trajectories `(o_t, a_t, v_t, o_{t+1})_{t=0..T}` from on-policy rollouts, plus the buffer-side state-hash (or k-means feature cluster) for each `o_t`. Vector signal `v_t ∈ R^k` is the per-step environment vector feedback (`info["vector"]`). Terminal cumulant `c_T = Σ v_t` is logged for diagnostics only — it does not enter the primitive.
2. **Core primitive:** the (state-cluster × action × channel × frequency-band) tensor `S[s, a, m, f]`. Each entry is the running mean over visits to `(s,a)` of `Var_{τ ∈ band_f}(1_m(t+τ))`, where `band_f = [2^f, 2^{f+1})` for `f ∈ {0, 1, …, F−1}` and `1_m(t+τ) = [v_{t+τ}[m] ≠ 0]`. This is a multi-resolution "how much does channel `m` fluctuate at scale `f` in the suffix of `(s, a)`".
3. **Improvement operator:** at each decision step at state-cluster `s`, channel-MAD-standardise rows `S̃[s,a,m,f] = (S[s,a,m,f] − median_a S[s,·,m,f]) / MAD_a S[s,·,m,f]`. Flatten to an (k·F)-vector per action. Compute Pareto-non-dominated set `P(s) ⊆ A` via coordinate-wise partial order. Logit nudge: `θ_logit[s,a] += α` if `a ∈ P(s)`, `−α/(|A|−|P(s)|)` otherwise. No magnitude weighting; the partial order is strict (a tie counts as non-dominated). `α` decays over training.
4. **Execution rule:** softmax over the nudged logits at each decision; full on-policy data collection. No tree search, no Best-of-N rollout, no verifier call. The primitive is read at decision time; the operator update is applied at episode boundaries.
5. **Vector feedback rule:** the channel index `m` is preserved through the spectrum (one frequency profile per channel). Pareto-non-dominance is over the (channel × frequency) product partial order — never collapsed to a scalar. There is no learned weight `w` over channels; the partial order respects all channels symmetrically. On Deep Sea Treasure (k=2: step penalty, treasure) and Resource Gathering (k=3: step, gold, gems) the spectrum stratifies by channel index throughout.
6. **Rollout-cost discipline:** strictly on-policy, one rollout per update batch. No counterfactual rollouts, no simulator branches, no verifier calls. Per-decision compute is `O(|A| · k · F)` for the Pareto comparison; per-update compute is `O(N · T · k · F)` for accumulating spectra (where `N · T` is buffer size). For F=4 bands and k≤3 channels, this is ≤ 12 doubles per (s,a) cell.
7. **Nearest-neighbor novelty audit:** Closest prior is **FFTV** (`fftv-first-firing-time-variance-lattice.md`, alive-promising, last_run 20260606-09-auto, scored 1382 on DST and 1.331 on RG). FFTV maintains a single timing-dispersion statistic (IQR of first-firing-time offsets) per (state, action, channel). ACS replaces "single timing statistic" with a **multi-band spectral profile** — F dispersion statistics per channel, each restricted to a different temporal scale. The structural distinction: FFTV is a 1-D timing-spread feature per channel; ACS is an F-D temporal-shape feature per channel that distinguishes "channel fires in tight bursts every few steps" from "channel fires sporadically over long lags" even when both have identical IQR. The Pareto comparison space is k·F-dimensional rather than k-dimensional, so an action that is Pareto-incomparable to another in FFTV (because their k-vectors are tied) can become Pareto-dominated or dominator in ACS by virtue of differing at one frequency band. Other near neighbours: TPP/CEC (FED-family) — those required hash-collision events on terminal-state matching; ACS requires only repeated channel firings within a single trajectory (dense channels supply this). Distributional RL — distributional RL predicts the *return* distribution; ACS predicts neither return nor any cumulant, only spectral structure of channel-firing indicators (a 0/1 process), which is a categorical-process signature, not a return distribution.
8. **Predicted failure modes:**
   (a) **Terminal-only-channel collapse on RG**: if on Resource Gathering only the step-penalty channel fires during exploration (gold/gems are terminal-only by design), the spectrum tensor is non-degenerate only along `m=step_penalty`, and Pareto comparison over a single non-degenerate channel reduces to scalar maximisation of step-penalty spectrum entries — a curiosity rebadge. This is the FFTV-RG repeat. ACS predicts the same failure unless the dense step-penalty's *spectrum* (different from its mean rate) carries genuine action-discriminating signal (an empirical question).
   (b) **Within-episode autocorrelation noise floor**: very short episodes (T < 8) cannot fill the upper frequency bands, so `S[s,a,m,f]` for large `f` is missing or zero-variance. Predict: KeyCorridor early episodes will under-fill the f=2 band.
   (c) **State-cluster collisions**: if state-hash collision rate on DoorKey exceeds ~30 %, spectra at colliding states are pooled and lose action-discrimination. Predict: switching from raw obs-hash to k-means feature cluster (k≈64) helps DoorKey/KeyCorridor.
   (d) **Pareto front saturates `|A|`**: if (k·F) is large (e.g., k=3, F=4 → 12 dims), Pareto-non-dominated set `|P(s)| → |A|` and the operator becomes uninformative. Predict: a hard cap `F ≤ 3` on F is needed; otherwise dominate-by-strict-margin instead of weak dominance.
9. **Side-information channel:** {vector diagnostics, transition geometry}. Vector diagnostics: per-step `v_t ∈ R^k` from `info["vector"]`. Transition geometry: only via state-hash / cluster identity for indexing the tensor; no learned dynamics, no demonstrations, no language, no verifier, no instrumentation beyond what FFTV/CWAI use.
10. **Monotonic improvement claim:** *In expectation over the buffer, applying the Pareto-non-dominance logit nudge strictly increases the empirical concentration of the visiting distribution onto the Pareto-non-dominated subset of the (k·F)-dimensional spectral image of the action set, conditional on the state-cluster being well-populated (≥ 8 visits with ≥ 4 samples per channel band).* Equivalently: the channel × frequency-band Pareto frontier of the visited (s, a)-spectra contracts (by area) monotonically under the operator, until stationarity. This is a structural-frontier-contraction claim, not a return-improvement claim — the connection to terminal reward is empirical, falsifiable on the panel.

## Why it is not FFTV

FFTV's primitive is a single timing-dispersion statistic per (state, action, channel) — a k-dim vector in `R^k`. ACS's primitive is a (k·F)-dim spectral matrix per (state, action), where F bands stratify the *temporal scale* of channel fluctuation. Under variable renaming, FFTV is the special case `F = 1` of ACS where the single band is "all lags pooled". The novelty test is: does the multi-band stratification expose action-discriminating structure at scales the pooled IQR misses? The hypothesis predicts yes (different actions induce different *temporal-scale* firing patterns even at fixed firing-rate); the falsifier (single-band ablation) directly tests this.

## Why it scales beyond the substrate

At horizon 20k actions in a tool-using LLM agent, vector feedback channels (validity, latency, energy, correctness, user-preference, safety) fire at *vastly different temporal rhythms*: latency on every API call (every step), validity on every parsed output (every few steps), correctness or user-preference only at episode end. ACS's spectrum naturally stratifies these — the high-frequency bands collect signal from the dense channels, the low-frequency bands collect signal from the sparse channels (when they fire at all). Even when one channel is terminal-only, the dense channels' multi-band spectra carry structural action-discrimination (e.g., "this kind of paragraph generation produces high-frequency-validity-burst suffixes" vs. "this kind produces flat-validity suffixes"). The Pareto operator handles vector feedback without scalarisation. The primitive's per-decision cost is `O(|A| · k · F)` regardless of horizon, since spectra are accumulated incrementally over experience and only read at decision time. For "generate a paragraph" actions, the action-set at each decision is restricted to a learned candidate proposer (e.g., top-`m` from the LLM); ACS operates on this candidate set with no change in primitive structure. The mechanism does *not* require enumerating the full action space at decision time, nor running counterfactual completions — both critical for scaling beyond toy substrates.
