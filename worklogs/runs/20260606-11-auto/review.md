## Reasoning

ACS's central primitive — a (state-cluster × action × channel × frequency-band) spectral-variance tensor `S[s,a,m,f]` measuring within-band firing-indicator variance at log-spaced temporal scales — is structurally distinct from every named disqualifier family: it is not a value estimate, not a return distribution, not a cumulant/GVF, not a scalar-weighted log-prob update, not a curiosity bonus, and not a Pareto-front over outcome cumulants (which is the FED family). Its nearest prior is FFTV, and the hypothesis correctly characterizes FFTV as the `F=1` special case (single timing-dispersion statistic per channel, all lags pooled). The structural distinction is real: the multi-band stratification encodes *temporal-scale* of firing patterns — "channel fires in tight bursts" vs. "channel fires sporadically over long lags" — which is information the pooled IQR discards. The falsifier (single-band ablation must score strictly lower on at least one panel env) directly tests this. Crucially, the primitive fires from episode 1 on dense channels (step-penalty, step-state-change), so it does not inherit the FED/CEC/TPP/PICAV/PCR bootstrap wall, which required either terminal-outcome collisions or a reward-gated operator to fire at all. All required candidate-shape slots are filled with substantive answers: experience object, core primitive, improvement operator, execution rule, vector feedback rule (Pareto over the (k×F) product space, never scalarized), rollout-cost discipline, nearest-neighbor novelty audit, four distinct predicted failure modes, side-information channel declaration, and a monotonic improvement claim (structural-frontier-contraction over the Pareto-non-dominated (k×F) spectral image of the action set).

The one tension is failure mode (a): on Resource Gathering, if only the step-penalty channel fires densely while gold/gems are terminal-only, the Pareto comparison degenerates to scalar maximization of the step-penalty spectrum — a curiosity-rebadge collapse. The hypothesis names this honestly and the bootstrap-degenerate falsifier (< 5 % fill rate on non-step-penalty channels) can detect it. Whether the dense channels' *spectral shape* (not just mean rate) carries genuine action-discrimination is the empirical question the run will answer. This is a legitimate open empirical question, not a structural impossibility — making the candidate a genuine novel-direction worth running.

## Risks the Engineer should be aware of

- The bootstrap-degenerate failure on Resource Gathering is the highest-risk path: if gold/gems never fire densely enough to populate `S[s,a,m=gold,f]` and `S[s,a,m=gems,f]`, the Pareto comparison degenerates to the step-penalty channel alone, which is effectively count-based curiosity in spectral clothing. Monitor per-channel fill rates as the hypothesis instructs.
- The Pareto-front saturation risk (failure mode d): with k=3 channels and F=4 bands, the 12-dimensional product space may make all actions Pareto-non-dominated early in training, rendering the logit nudge uninformative. The hypothesis recommends capping F ≤ 3 and/or using strict-margin dominance; the Engineer should verify this is implemented before the run.
- The side-information channel is declared as {vector diagnostics, transition geometry}. The state-cluster (k-means on features) counts as transition geometry, consistent with prior FFTV and CWAI usage. No hand-engineered event lenses are involved; this is clean.
