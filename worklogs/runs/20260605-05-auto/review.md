## Reasoning

VCC's structural distinction from the negative space is real and survives scrutiny. The central primitive — the continuation-cumulant Pareto front `F(b, a)` indexed by the *running vector cumulant bucket* `c_t = Σ_{s≤t} v_s` — is genuinely different from FED (#15), SIT, and RSD, which all index by observation-hash. As the hypothesis correctly diagnoses, observation-hash buckets are sparse on long-horizon sparse envs (the bootstrap wall that killed FED), whereas the cumulant bucket is dense by construction because every transition contributes an increment to `c_t`. This is not a renaming: observation-hash partitions the state-space, while cumulant-bucket partitions vector-trajectory-space, and these have different geometric properties under sparse-reward long-horizon exploration. The dominance margin `m(a)` is a partial-order operation on `ℝᵏ` with no fixed or learned scalar weight `w`, so scalarized return-to-go (disqualifier: "Q/V/advantage/return-to-go as the central learned object") and scalarized vector maximization (`wᵀr`) are structurally excluded. The improvement operator is not a Bellman backup, not TD-error minimization, not policy-gradient reward weighting, not elite cloning, and not a verifier stack. All required-candidate-shape slots are filled with substantive answers: a clear primitive (cumulant-partitioned Pareto front), a precise update rule (logit nudge by dominance count, distilled into policy), a composition law (single rule: bucket-by-cumulant → partial-order comparison → logit nudge), named side-information channels (vector diagnostics + transition geometry), predicted failure modes with testable conditions, and a monotonic improvement claim with a Pareto-generalization of policy-iteration argument.

The hypothesis also correctly flags its own risk on scalar-reward envs without auxiliary channels: if `k = 1`, VCC reduces to "prefer action with highest observed continuation cumulant from the same prefix," which is conditional return-to-go, a disqualifier. The proposed safeguard — augmenting with cheap structural channels (step-parity, action-repeat-streak, terminal-flag) — is reasonable but the hypothesis acknowledges this is a structural risk the experiment must report on. The falsifier condition is sharp and testable (bucket-saturation + at least two distinct Pareto faces + non-zero policy KL, yet beat_strong=0), which means the theory, not just the bootstrap, can be falsified.

## Risks the Engineer should be aware of

- On scalar-reward envs (DoorKey, KeyCorridor, Craftax-Symbolic where `k=1`), VCC silently reduces to conditional return-to-go unless the auxiliary structural channels carry genuine discrimination signal. The experiment must report per-env channel-count and whether the auxiliary channels (step-parity, action-repeat-streak, etc.) are producing non-trivial Pareto-incomparable points; if all continuations in a bucket are totally ordered, VCC is a return-to-go rebadge on that env.
- The bucket-coverage exploration term ("take the action whose bucket has the fewest recorded continuations") risks being a renamed count bonus over cumulant space rather than an intrinsic property of the primitive. The experiment should verify this term is ablatable without destroying performance on vector envs, and that it is genuinely over the cumulant manifold rather than a state-visitation proxy.
- Off-policy contamination of continuation deltas is real: old Pareto front entries are from a different policy. The hypothesis flags this and recommends aging or down-weighting; the Engineer should implement a recency window or decay to prevent the improvement operator from chasing stale dominance margins.
