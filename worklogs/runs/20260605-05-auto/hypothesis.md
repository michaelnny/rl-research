# 20260605-05-auto — VCC (Vector-Cumulant Confluence)

## Research Gate

primitive: per-(cumulant-prefix-bucket, action) attainment cones over the *remainder-of-episode vector signature*, where the index is the running per-channel cumulant `c_t = Σ_{s≤t} v_s` (quantized) and the cone is the empirical Pareto front of `Δ_τ = c_T − c_t` measured along all observed continuations from contexts hitting that prefix-bucket
improvement_operator: at decision time in context with cumulant `c_t`, the policy logits are nudged toward actions whose continuation-Pareto-front under that cumulant-bucket strictly partial-order-dominates the bucket's current attainment; nudge magnitude proportional to dominance margin counted across channels; *no scalar collapse, no Bellman backup, no critic, no rollout verification*
side_information: vector diagnostics (the panel's `info["vector"]` channel — required by the substrate rule on vector envs) plus transition geometry (running cumulant trajectory)
nearest_prior_or_disqualifier: #15 FED (per-action empirical Pareto-front indexed by observation-hash bucket); #11 TOP (first-hit event/outcome profiles); SIT/RSD candidates (observation-hash indexed dominance)
falsifier: on the panel's vector envs (Deep Sea Treasure, Resource Gathering) the cumulant-prefix bucket count saturates within the time budget AND at least one bucket accumulates ≥2 distinct continuation-Pareto faces AND the operator nudges produce non-zero policy KL change — but final scoring is still beat_strong=0 on all envs. That falsifies the *theory* (the cumulant-prefix index is not a discriminating partition for sparse-reward learning) rather than just the bootstrap (which is bypassed by the index choice).

## Mechanism

The primitive indexes experience by an object that is *guaranteed dense even before reward exists on long-horizon sparse envs*: the running per-channel vector cumulant `c_t = Σ_{s≤t} v_s`, where `v_s ∈ ℝᵏ` is the per-step vector feedback the env exposes (success channel, cost channel, validity channel, etc.; on scalar-reward envs, augmented with cheap structural channels — episode-step parity, last-action repeat-count — that are part of the trajectory and do not require reward). For each quantized bucket `b = q(c_t)` and each action `a`, we maintain the empirical multiset of *continuation cumulant deltas* `Δ = c_T − c_t` from every recorded transition in that bucket that took action `a`, and we compress this to its coordinate-wise Pareto front `F(b, a) ⊂ ℝᵏ`. The improvement operator at decision time computes, for the current context's bucket `b`, a per-action *dominance margin* `m(a) = #{points in ⋃_{a'≠a} F(b, a') strictly dominated by some point in F(b, a)} − #{points in F(b, a) strictly dominated by some point in ⋃_{a'≠a} F(b, a')}`, and adds `α · m(a)` to the policy logits. The composition law is one rule: bucket-by-cumulant, partial-order on continuation cumulants, logit nudge by dominance count. Crucially the index is *not* observation-hash (which has bootstrap-collision-rate issues) and *not* state-action value (which collapses the channels to a scalar); it is the running vector signature itself, which is dense by construction because every step contributes to it.

## Required candidate shape

1. **Experience object:** ordinary on-policy interaction tuples `(s_t, a_t, v_t, s_{t+1})` where `v_t ∈ ℝᵏ` is the per-step vector feedback (env-supplied on vector envs; on scalar-reward envs, augmented with cheap reward-free structural channels: `step-parity`, `action-repeat-streak`, `terminal-flag`, `unique-step` indicator). Also stored: the running cumulant trajectory `(c_0, c_1, …, c_T)` per episode.

2. **Core primitive:** for each (bucket, action) pair `(b, a)`, the *continuation-cumulant Pareto front* `F(b, a) = ParetoFront({c_T − c_t : episode visited bucket b at step t and took action a})`. This is a finite set of points in `ℝᵏ`, *not* a scalar, *not* an expectation, *not* a return-to-go.

3. **Improvement operator:** policy logits `ℓ(s, a)` are augmented by `α · m(a; b(s))` where `m` is the cross-action dominance margin defined above. The policy network is trained by supervised KL distillation toward `softmax(ℓ + α·m)` on observed states. No reward weighting. No critic. No advantage. The Pareto front is computed channel-wise with strict dominance — collapse to scalar is impossible because the comparison rule is `x ≻ y iff ∀i x_i ≥ y_i ∧ ∃j x_j > y_j`.

4. **Execution rule:** sample from `softmax(ℓ_θ(s, ·))` where `ℓ_θ` is the neural policy, with the dominance-nudge already baked in via distillation. A small `ε`-fraction of the time, take the action `a` whose bucket `b(s)` has the *fewest* recorded continuations (front-discovery exploration). This is intrinsic to the primitive — bucket-coverage drives discovery — not a renamed novelty bonus, because the coverage signal is over the *cumulant manifold*, not state space.

5. **Vector feedback rule:** never collapsed. The Pareto front is the only summary; channel weights never appear. The dominance margin counts dominated/dominating front points, which is a partial-order operation on `ℝᵏ`. On a 6-channel feedback environment, all 6 channels participate in the dominance check; no fixed `w` or learned `w` exists in the algorithm.

6. **Rollout-cost discipline:** one environment step per stored transition, period. No counterfactual replays. No simulator branches. No verifier calls. Per accepted improvement: zero additional rollouts (the dominance margin is computed offline from the running trie of continuation deltas). Per deployment step: one neural-policy forward pass plus one bucket-lookup (constant time with hash table). The whole training loop is one-rollout-per-step, indistinguishable in env-cost from REINFORCE.

7. **Nearest-neighbor novelty audit:** Closest priors are #15 FED, candidate SIT, candidate RSD. **Distinction**: FED, SIT, and RSD all index by *observation-hash bucket*; their bootstrap failure was that observation-hash collisions are rare on long-horizon sparse envs, so buckets never fill. VCC indexes by *cumulant bucket*, which is dense by construction because the cumulant accumulates at every step and naturally clusters trajectories by their evolving vector signature. This is not a renamed observation hash; observation-hash partitions state space, while cumulant partitions *vector-trajectory space*, which is the geometry the env's vector feedback already lives in. Also distinct from #11 TOP: TOP stored first-hit *event* profiles (a hand-chosen lens over the env); VCC stores *running-cumulant continuations* (a measure-theoretic object, not a hand-chosen lens).

8. **Predicted failure modes:**
   - *Cumulant bucket explosion*: if the quantization is too fine, buckets are singleton and no continuation comparison is possible; if too coarse, all trajectories collapse to one bucket and the operator is uninformative. This is testable by reporting bucket-occupancy histograms.
   - *Stochastic transitions*: the continuation-delta Pareto front mixes outcome variance with policy-conditional structure. Stochastic envs would inflate the front and reduce dominance margins. The dominance margin should still be valid in expectation but lose statistical power.
   - *Scalar-reward envs without auxiliary channels*: if `k = 1`, Pareto reduces to scalar max, and VCC reduces to "prefer action with highest observed continuation cumulant from the same prefix" — which is conditional return-to-go, a disqualifier. The auxiliary structural channels (step-parity, action-repeat-streak, etc.) are the safeguard, but if those channels carry no useful information, the method silently falls back to return-to-go and should be flagged. This is a structural risk the experiment must report on.
   - *Continuation comparison contamination*: dominance margin is computed over *full-episode* continuations rather than bounded-horizon ones; if the policy is changing fast, old continuations may be off-policy. Trie entries should be aged or down-weighted; the experiment should report whether on-policy-only vs. all-history changes the result.

9. **Side-information channel:** **vector diagnostics** (primary; required by the substrate rule on vector envs) and **transition geometry** (the running cumulant `c_t` is a transition-derived quantity). On scalar-reward envs, the auxiliary structural channels (step-parity, action-repeat-streak, terminal-flag) are ordinary trajectory metadata; they are not a hand-engineered event lens because they do not encode task semantics, only trajectory shape. This distinction should be audited.

10. **Monotonic improvement claim:** *Conditional on a fixed bucketization function `q` and a fixed environment dynamics*, applying the dominance-margin nudge with infinitesimal `α` weakly improves the policy's expected continuation-cumulant Pareto front in every bucket — formally, after the update, the bucket's marginal distribution over `Δ` first-order Pareto-dominates the prior distribution (or is incomparable; never strictly dominated). Proof sketch: the nudge raises probability of an action `a` only if its existing continuation front contains a point dominating some point in another action's front, which weakly raises the post-update mixture's expected dominance count. This is a Pareto generalization of a policy-iteration-style monotonicity argument and is *not* a Bellman backup because the per-action quantity being increased is a partial-order count, not a scalar Q.

## Why it is not #15 FED, SIT, RSD, or scalarized return-to-go

#15 FED indexed Pareto fronts by observation-hash bucket and failed because hash buckets did not accumulate sample mass on long-horizon sparse envs. VCC swaps the indexing object: it buckets by *running vector cumulant* `c_t`, which is dense at every step (every transition contributes to it), so the bootstrap wall that killed FED, SIT, and RSD is structurally absent. The distinction from scalarized return-to-go (a disqualifier under "Q/V/advantage/return-to-go as the central learned object") is that VCC's central object is the *partial-order Pareto front* of continuation cumulants, not a scalar return-to-go; the dominance comparison is irreducible to any fixed or learned weighting `w` because the front contains incomparable points.

## Why it scales beyond the substrate

At horizon 20k actions, observation-hash methods become hopeless because each state is essentially unique; cumulant-bucket methods *gain* power because the cumulant has 20k contributions and its quantized buckets become more meaningful, not less. With a 6-component vector feedback (success, cost, safety, latency, energy, validity), the Pareto front in `ℝ⁶` carries genuine non-scalar structure that scalar methods discard; VCC's dominance count operates on this directly. For LLM-tool-use settings where actions are paragraphs or API calls, the cumulant is over *channels of side feedback* (validity from a parser, cost from API meter, latency, etc.) — these channels accumulate naturally per token or per call, and the Pareto front in continuation-cumulant space distinguishes "long but valid and cheap" from "short but invalid and expensive" without ever choosing a weighting. The action space being huge is irrelevant to the primitive: the index is over cumulants, not over actions, and the nudge is a per-action logit adjustment that scales with the policy's natural action representation (token logits, tool-argument distributions). The primitive does not enumerate actions; it enumerates *channels*, and channels are typically O(10), not O(vocabulary).
