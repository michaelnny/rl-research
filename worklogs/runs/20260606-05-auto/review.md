## Reasoning

TPP's central primitive — the postfix-divergence anchor derived by walking two terminal-observation-matched trajectories backward in lockstep until their observation sequences first disagree — is structurally distinct from every disqualifier family and every numbered prior attempt. The improvement operator is a Pareto-vote count W[s, a] over qualifying pairs, with a logit nudge at each anchor; there is no scalar reward weighting of log-probabilities (not PPO/REINFORCE), no bootstrapped value target (not Bellman/Q/TD), no full-trajectory cloning (not top-k), and no fictitious goal substitution (not HER — the terminal observation is the natural trajectory terminal, not a relabeled goal). The structural distinction from the nearest prior DPC is precisely stated: DPC accumulates votes at the first prefix-divergence state and leaves the downstream suffix unbounded in length, which is the attributed cause of DPC's Resource-Gathering failure; TPP fixes the post-anchor suffix length to exactly Δ (the postfix-shared depth) by construction, so the comparison is over "what got us into the shared final approach" rather than "what happened after we diverged." That is not a variable renaming of DPC. All required-candidate-shape slots carry substantive answers: experience object, primitive, improvement operator, execution rule, vector feedback rule (Pareto comparison, no scalarization, Pareto-incomparable pairs abstain), rollout-cost discipline (zero additional env interactions per update), nearest-neighbor novelty audit, four concrete predicted failure modes with falsifier thresholds, side-information channel declaration (vector diagnostics + transition geometry / terminal observation as postfix anchor), and a conditional monotonic improvement claim (mirror-descent on the categorical at each anchor within each terminal-observation class).

The candidate's own bootstrap concern (the FED/CEC family failure) is partially but honestly addressed: TPP requires only terminal-hash collisions (not mid-trajectory bucket accumulation), and in the gridworld panel envs goal cells are shared across many trajectories, so terminal-hash collision rates should be meaningfully higher than mid-trajectory rates. The hypothesis's falsifier — log fraction of pairs with matching terminal hash; below 5% means dead — is the right gate to run first.

## Risks the Engineer should be aware of

- In partially-observable envs (KeyCorridor, DoorKey with partial obs) the terminal observation encodes the agent's local view at the exact final step; if the agent's facing direction or local context varies at goal-reach, terminal-hash collisions may still be rare even though the goal cell is shared. The 5%-collision falsifier should be checked per-env, not aggregated across the panel, so KeyCorridor can be declared dead independently without suppressing a positive DST signal.
- The hypothesis's monotonic improvement claim is conditional on "trajectories ending at the same terminal observation form a stationary outcome distribution conditional on the postfix-divergence anchor." On non-stationary exploration phases (early training when policy changes rapidly), this stationarity assumption is violated and the claim does not apply; W built from an old policy may point the logit nudge in the wrong direction. A staleness cutoff or recency weighting on the pair buffer would reduce this risk.
- The O(N_traj^2) pair-comparison cost is noted as compute-only (no extra env interactions), but at N_traj = 1000 completed trajectories per update this is 500k comparisons each requiring a backward lockstep walk of up to T steps. The Engineer should cap the buffer size or use reservoir sampling to keep wall-clock per update bounded within the 120 s panel budget.
