# 20260606-05-auto — TPP (Terminal-Postfix Pairing)

## Research Gate

primitive: per-(observation-hash, action) **terminal-aligned postfix-divergence index**, defined only at the **last-shared-from-the-end** observation between trajectory pairs that ended at the same terminal observation
improvement_operator: at the per-pair postfix-divergence point, increment the logit of the action whose trajectory's terminal vector outcome Pareto-dominates the other's terminal vector outcome; if Pareto-incomparable, no nudge
side_information: vector diagnostics, transition geometry (specifically the terminal observation as a postfix anchor)
nearest_prior_or_disqualifier: DPC (`worklogs/candidates/dpc-divergent-prefix-concordance.md`); structurally inverted — DPC anchors at the prefix and looks forward, TPP anchors at the postfix and looks backward
falsifier: if on Deep-Sea-Treasure and Resource-Gathering the **fraction of trajectory pairs sharing a terminal observation-hash** stays below 5% within a 120 s vector-stage run (measured directly as a diagnostic), the primitive does not fire and the candidate is dead. Additionally: if the score on Resource-Gathering does not exceed random within 120 s — DPC matched random there because per-channel sign votes washed out under stochastic transitions — TPP fails for the same family-level reason.

## Mechanism

A trajectory `τ = (s_0, a_0, ..., s_T)` is anchored not by its starting state but by its **terminal observation** `s_T`. For every pair of completed trajectories `(τ, τ')` whose terminal-observation hashes match (`hash(s_T) = hash(s'_{T'})`), walk both trajectories backward from termination in lockstep and find the largest `Δ` such that `hash(s_{T-δ}) = hash(s'_{T'-δ})` for all `0 ≤ δ ≤ Δ`. Call this the **postfix-shared depth**. The **postfix-divergence point** is `(s_{T-Δ-1}, a_{T-Δ-1})` for `τ` and `(s'_{T'-Δ-1}, a'_{T'-Δ-1})` for `τ'`; these are the last actions before the trajectories' backward-walked observation sequences first disagreed. (When the divergence is at the action only — same observation, different action chosen — that observation is itself the postfix-divergence anchor.) The primitive is: at this anchor, **only one of `a_{T-Δ-1}` or `a'_{T'-Δ-1}` is "the action whose suffix ended in a Pareto-non-dominated terminal vector outcome"**; the improvement operator nudges the policy logit at that anchor's observation toward that action by a small fixed step. No scalar reward weight; no Bellman backup; no critic; no count; no average over the bucket — every pair contributes one nudge at one anchor. Pairs with Pareto-incomparable terminal vectors abstain. The primitive is one mathematical object (the postfix-divergence-anchored Pareto-side) with one composition law (sum nudges over qualifying pairs).

## Required candidate shape

1. **Experience object:** Completed trajectories with per-step `(obs_hash, action)` and terminal vector outcome `c_T ∈ ℝ^k` from `info["vector"]`. No edit grammar, no event lens, no demonstrations — just rollouts from the running policy.
2. **Core primitive:** For each unordered pair `(τ, τ')` whose terminal observation-hashes match, the postfix-divergence anchor `(s_anchor, a, a')` and the Pareto relation between `c_T(τ)` and `c_T(τ')`. The induced object is a per-(observation-hash, action) integer **postfix-Pareto-vote count** `W[s, a] = #{pairs where a was the postfix-divergence action of the Pareto-non-dominated side at anchor s}`.
3. **Improvement operator:** Logit nudge at every observation `s` with `W[s, ·]` non-zero: `logit(a | s) += α · sign(W[s, a] − max_{a'} W[s, a'])` weighted by margin. Equivalently, KL-project the policy at `s` toward a softmax of `W[s, ·]`. No scalar reward weighting of trajectories; the count `W` is a structural object built from terminal-vector Pareto comparisons restricted to terminal-aligned pairs.
4. **Execution rule:** Sample actions from the current logits (standard categorical sampling); no goal conditioning, no side-info input at action time. The structural object `W` is updated between rollout batches and influences only the logits.
5. **Vector feedback rule:** Pareto comparison of terminal vectors `c_T(τ) ⪰ c_T(τ')` (componentwise). No scalarization. Pareto-incomparable pairs abstain — they contribute nothing to `W`. Channels are MAD-standardized only for tie-breaking under near-equality (with absolute tolerance per channel proportional to its observed range), never combined into a scalar.
6. **Rollout-cost discipline:** One rollout per episode, no counterfactual replays, no verifier calls, no simulator branches. The pair-comparison cost is `O(N_traj^2)` *computation* on trajectories already in the buffer — but **zero additional environment interactions per accepted improvement**. At deployment, the trained policy runs forward without `W` lookup.
7. **Nearest-neighbor novelty audit:** Closest prior is **DPC** (`worklogs/candidates/dpc-divergent-prefix-concordance.md`). DPC anchors trajectory pairs at their **first prefix-divergence state** and walks forward; the divergence event is "first action choice that differs." TPP anchors at the **last postfix-shared state walking backward from a matched terminal observation** and the divergence event is "last action chosen before the two backward-walked observation sequences first disagreed." The two are not duals: prefix-divergence sees noisy long suffixes downstream of a divergence (DPC's RG failure mode), whereas postfix-divergence by construction has identical (length-`Δ`) terminal walks downstream — the comparison is over *what got us into the shared final approach*, not *what happened after we diverged*. The structural distinction holds under variable renaming: re-pointing DPC at the postfix would still leave the suffix length unbounded, whereas TPP fixes the suffix length to `Δ` (the postfix-shared depth) by construction.
8. **Predicted failure modes:**
   - On envs whose terminal observation is unique per trajectory (e.g. partially-observable settings where the agent's last view depends on heading), terminal-hash collisions are rare and `W` stays empty. Falsifier: log fraction of pairs with matching terminal hash; below 5% means dead.
   - On stochastic-transition envs (Resource-Gathering's random gem placement, slippery cells), even matched terminal-hash pairs may have semantically incomparable trajectories and Pareto-incomparable outcomes; abstention rate may approach 100%. Falsifier: log fraction of qualifying pairs that are Pareto-comparable.
   - On long-horizon envs where the goal is reached only by a few trajectories, `W` is dominated by a few high-pair-count goal cells and underweights upstream decisions (the postfix-shared depth `Δ` is large, so the divergence anchor sits near trajectory start, far from the goal action). The nudge accumulates at the strategic-decision points by construction — but if those decisions are at `t=0` only (every successful trajectory makes the same first move), the divergence anchor is shifted to `t=1`, etc. Falsifier: log the distribution of `Δ` and verify it spans more than just the goal-corridor approach.
   - On collinear vector channels (DST: terminal treasure value + step penalty, both correlated with episode length), Pareto comparison degenerates to scalar comparison and the operator reduces to "imitate the trajectory with higher return at the divergence anchor" — close to but not identical to MC-advantage at one anchor per pair. Falsifier: flag if Pareto-comparable rate is >95% (collinear regime) and audit collapse to MC-advantage in that regime.
9. **Side-information channel:** vector diagnostics (`info["vector"]` per step, used only at termination for `c_T`); transition geometry (observation-hash and the terminal observation as the postfix anchor). No event lens, no demonstrations, no resets, no learned dynamics, no verifier.
10. **Monotonic improvement claim:** Under the assumption that **trajectories ending at the same terminal observation form a stationary outcome distribution conditional on the postfix-divergence anchor**, the operator monotonically increases the policy's marginal probability of reaching Pareto-non-dominated terminal vectors *within each terminal-observation class*, by classical mirror-descent on the categorical distribution at each anchor towards a target softmax of `W[s, ·]`. The improvement is local (per-anchor) and conditional (given terminal-observation class); no global monotone-improvement claim across terminal-observation classes is made because cross-class comparison is undefined under the primitive.

## Why it is not DPC

Closest prior is the alive-weak DPC candidate. Under variable renaming DPC accumulates a sign-vote tensor at the **first state where a pair of trajectories first chose different actions**, with the suffix-tail extending all the way to termination (unbounded length). TPP accumulates votes at the **last state where two terminal-aligned trajectories' backward-walked observation sequences last agreed**, so the *post-anchor walk is identical in length on both sides by construction* (the postfix-shared depth `Δ`). DPC's RG failure was attributed to "stochastic transitions swamping per-channel sign votes" — that swamping is downstream of the prefix-divergence anchor; TPP, by anchoring at a state where the suffix-tail is structurally controlled, removes the unbounded-suffix variance. It is also not return-to-go (no scalar attribution to anchor), not advantage (no baseline), not Bellman backup (no recursion), not reward-machine (no automaton), and not HER (no goal relabeling — terminal observation is the *natural* terminal of the trajectory, not a fictive goal substituted in hindsight).

## Why it scales beyond the substrate

In long-horizon agentic settings (LLM agent with a 20k-step tool-use trajectory, a 6-component vector of {success, cost, latency, safety, validity, user-satisfaction}), terminal observations exist and are commonly shared: many trajectories end with "task complete and tool returned X" or "task aborted by user." The postfix-divergence anchor — the last shared environment state walking backward from a matched terminal — corresponds to the **last common situation before the two trajectories' final approaches diverged**, which is exactly the decision point a human reviewer would identify as "this is where the agent committed." The primitive does not scale by a counts table; the per-anchor Pareto-vote `W[s, a]` is a low-rank object indexable by the (anchor-observation, action) pair and updateable from streaming pair-comparisons. No bucket bootstrap is required: any two completed trajectories with matched terminal observations contribute. Vector feedback is handled natively (no `wᵀr`). The operator does not require enumerable actions at the anchor — it nudges a logit, which generalizes to a transformer policy parameterizing a categorical or continuous action distribution at that observation. The candidate's substrate test is its first falsification gate; the same primitive is well-defined for an LLM-tool-use trajectory whose terminal observation is the final tool-output-or-abort state.
