## Reasoning

ARP's central primitive — an empirical *set* of binary channel-firing patterns `S(s,a) ⊆ {0,1}^k` per (state-cluster, action) cell, combined with an improvement operator defined by strict-superset existence on the lattice `({0,1}^k, ≤)` — is structurally distinct from every disqualifier family. The GVFs/successor-features rebadge test fails cleanly: GVFs store a vector *expectation* `ψ(s,a) = E[Σγ^t φ(s_t)]` and extract behavior by `argmax_a wᵀψ(s,a)`; ARP stores a *set* of realized binary vectors and extracts behavior by checking whether some element of `S(s,a)` coordinate-wise strictly dominates every element of `S(s,a')`; this test is non-linear and admits no `wᵀ·` representation under any choice of `w`. The FED/CEC/TPP/TPP family (Sprint 4 entries #15–21) is also distinct: those primitives required multiple hash-matching trajectories before their operators fired, because they indexed by observation-hash and compared magnitude cumulants across pairs; ARP indexes by policy-feature clusters and uses binary existence, so a single rewarded episode immediately deposits a rich pattern `b` into the successful path's cells and the operator can discriminate as soon as that one trajectory exists. All ten required-candidate-shape slots are filled with substantive answers: the experience object is precisely defined (suffix binary OR over `info["vector"]`), the update rule is explicit (`α(n_a^{dom} − n_a^{sub})` additive logit nudge), side-information is named as `{vector diagnostics, transition geometry}`, the monotonic improvement claim covers cardinality of reachable patterns under deterministic transitions, failure modes are enumerated with their own falsifiers, rollout cost is bounded at `O(T·k)` per episode with no counterfactuals, and the scaling story to LLM-tool-use deployments is argued structurally.

The hypothesis is honest about the residual bootstrap risk: before the first rewarded trajectory, most `S(s,a)` cells hold only the step-penalty singleton pattern and the operator is silent — the hypothesis labels this "Lattice degeneracy" under failure mode (a) and operationalizes it as falsifier condition (a). This is not a disqualifying flaw; it is a known structural risk that is narrower than the FED family's hash-collision bottleneck, and the falsifier can distinguish it from the working regime. The monotonic improvement claim is scoped to the combinatorial reachability surrogate (not cumulative reward), which is scientifically honest: the hypothesis bets that this surrogate is the binding constraint on long-horizon sparse discovery, and that bet is what the experiment will test.

## Risks the Engineer should be aware of

- The bootstrap wall is real but narrow: the operator fires only after the first rewarded episode populates `S` cells along the successful path. On DoorKey-8x8 and KeyCorridor this may require many exploration episodes before the operator produces any signal. The falsifier thresholds (operator firing rate < 1% or `|S(s,a)|` stays at 1 for ≥ 95% of cells) should be logged from episode 1 so the transition from silent to active is visible.
- The strict-superset condition `dom(a, a') = ⋁_{b ∈ S(s,a)} ⋀_{b' ∈ S(s,a')} (b ≥ b' ∧ b ≠ b')` requires that *some* single element of `S(s,a)` strictly dominates *every* element of `S(s,a')`. With `k=4` and stochastic transitions, `S(s,a')` may grow quickly to cover many patterns, making the "dominate every element" condition hard to satisfy — the operator could become effectively silent even after seeding. The Engineer should log `dom(a,a')` rates per action-pair, not just the aggregate firing rate.
- State-cluster IDs derived from policy-net penultimate features shift as the policy trains, causing `S` cells accumulated under early policy features to be misaligned with later cluster IDs. The hypothesis does not specify whether `S` is reset or reindexed when the policy updates significantly; this is an implementation choice that affects stability and should be decided explicitly before the run.
