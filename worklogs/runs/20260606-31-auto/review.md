## Reasoning

KSV's central primitive is a kernel-weighted, per-(action, channel) signed pair-vote tensor `SV[a, m]` whose weights are continuous Gaussian kernels on a learned observation embedding rather than discrete hash-bucket or cluster collisions. The improvement operator is strict coordinate-wise Pareto-non-dominance margin applied to the k-vector `SV[a, :]` — no scalar collapse, no critic, no Bellman backup. The structural distinction from all prior sprint-4 attempts is genuine: FED (#15), CEC (#18), CWTP (#19), TPP (#21), TRAC (#26), CSD (#32), ARP (#28), and PCR (#24) all died from hash-collision or cluster-revisitation starvation — they require discrete coincidences in observation space before the primitive fires at all. KSV's kernel formulation gives every trajectory pair in the buffer a non-zero (though possibly small) weight contribution at every decision step, bypassing the bootstrap-wall entirely. The hypothesis also distinguishes itself from DPC (alive-weak, first-divergence hash, single influence per pair per state) and KTAC (alive-weak, k-means cluster identity, population threshold before firing) on exactly this dimension. The candidate's required-shape slots are all filled with substantive content: the experience object is precisely specified (sliding replay buffer with per-step vector signal from `info["vector"]`), the core primitive has an explicit formula, the improvement operator has an explicit per-step update rule, the rollout-cost discipline accounts for the pairwise approximation via M-pair sub-sampling, the side-information channel is declared as {vector diagnostics, transition geometry}, the monotonic improvement claim is stated with its limiting conditions, and five predicted failure modes are named with concrete falsifiers.

The one genuine risk is the collinearity-collapse failure mode (predicted failure mode (a) in the hypothesis itself): before any rewarded trajectory enters the buffer, all reward channels have `δ_m^{ij} = 0` and the Pareto vote degenerates to scalar comparison on the step-penalty channel alone. This is the same bootstrap-wall that killed PRAR (#36), ATP (#31), ACFC (#33), and PCR (#24) once the operator's gating condition was terminal-only. KSV has no exploration primitive to inject rewarded trajectories early, and the hypothesis's falsifier correctly identifies this. The method's viability therefore hinges on whether the step-penalty channel's non-zero pre-reward signal plus the kernel's continuous weighting provide enough directional information for the policy to reach rewarded states within the 120 s budget — a question the panel run will answer directly.

## Risks the Engineer should be aware of

- The collinearity-collapse mode (failure mode (a)) is the binding risk: on DST and RG, reward channels remain zero until the first rewarded terminal, so `SV[a, reward_channel]` is identically zero throughout the bootstrap window and the Pareto vote over `SV[a, :]` degenerates to a 1-dimensional comparison on the step-penalty channel. The per-channel sign histogram falsifier (> 99% zero on reward channels) should be logged from step 1 to confirm or refute this early.
- The REINFORCE-style entropy-only base-policy update means the policy receives no scalar reward gradient at all; if the kernel bandwidth is poorly initialized or the forward-model embedding underfits quickly, the logit nudge may be too noisy to steer exploration toward rewarded trajectories within budget. Engineering should log forward-model MSE and mean kernel weight per decision step alongside the falsifiers declared in the hypothesis.
- The pairwise sub-sampling variance (failure mode (e)) interacts with the small action-set size on MiniGrid envs: with |A| = 7 and M = 256 pairs, most pairs will share the same action-a / action-not-a split, potentially producing low-variance but biased SV estimates if the buffer is dominated by one action. The ablation on M recommended in the hypothesis is important before interpreting RG results.
