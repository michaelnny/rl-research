## Reasoning

SIT's central improvement operator is suffix grafting conditioned on observation-hash equivalence, accepted only when a single verification rollout confirms Pareto dominance. This is structurally distinct from every disqualifier family: it is not top-k trajectory cloning (selection is structural, not rank-based), not Go-Explore with renamed cells (no return-and-explore loop; the operation is algebraic surgery transferring a suffix between trie prefixes that happen to share an observation hash), not verifier-guided search (the rollout is a binary accept/reject corrective for partial-observability slippage, not a scoring function over a beam or tree of candidates), and not CEM/ES (no parameter population). The distinction from BRIC (#2) is well-articulated and holds: BRIC's swap points came from a hand-specified edit grammar making them off-distribution and requiring many counterfactual rollouts per accepted bracket; SIT's graft points are on-distribution by construction because they arise from observation equivalence discovered within the trie itself. The distinction from ETB/HPC (#13) is also clean: SIT has no goals and no event lens; the suffix outcome is the natural episode outcome and grafting is unconditional on which states a suffix visits.

The primitive (observation-keyed action-suffix trie) is a single object with a well-defined composition law (graft = concatenate path-to-node with donor suffix, verify, commit or mark non-fungible). The side-information channel is declared as transition geometry (observation-equivalence as the graft-point rule) plus vector diagnostics (Pareto dominance read from info["vector"]). The monotonic improvement claim is coherent: under exact Markov-in-observation every committed graft strictly Pareto-improves the best outcome at the receiving node; under approximate Markov the verification rollout is the corrective and the non-fungible flag prevents repeated losing grafts, so the committed graft set still produces monotone non-decreasing best-from-root outcome. The falsifier is sharp and env-specific. All structural slots are filled.

## Risks the Engineer should be aware of

- The observation hash must be collision-resistant enough to make observation-equivalence meaningful across episodes. On pixel-based envs (e.g. with `ImgObsWrapper`) hashing full frames is expensive and may produce excessive non-fungible pairs if partial observability is severe; the Engineer should plan a lightweight hash (e.g. SHA-1 of flattened int8 obs) and instrument the collision/non-fungible rate early.
- On DoorKey-8x8 the trie may grow combinatorially before the first reward-bearing suffix is discovered, leaving the observation-equivalence rule with no Pareto-dominant suffix to graft. The falsifier threshold (< 5% graft replication within 60 s) is the right early kill signal, but the Engineer should also log trie depth and node count to catch memory blowup before that threshold fires.
- The softmax-over-child-suffix-outcomes sampling policy inherits all outcome estimates from O(n), which is built from past episodes under a changing policy. This is an off-policy mixture, not a stationary distribution; on envs with non-stationary dynamics (e.g. procedurally generated MiniGrid) the outcome distribution in O(n) may shift under the trie's own grafts. This is not a disqualifier but it may require a decay or recency weighting on O(n) to keep the sampling policy from exploiting stale grafts.
