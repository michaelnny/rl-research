# 20260605-02-auto — Suffix-Inheritance Trie (SIT)

## Research Gate

```text
primitive: observation-keyed action-suffix trie — a prefix tree over observed
  action-sequences whose nodes are tagged with the observation hash reached at
  that prefix and the empirical outcome vector accumulated by every suffix that
  has continued from that node.
improvement_operator: Pareto-dominant suffix inheritance — when two prefixes
  in the trie share an observation hash, graft the suffix that Pareto-dominates
  the other under the env's natural outcome (vector for vector envs, scalar +
  shorter-suffix structural tiebreak for scalar envs); confirm the graft with
  a single verification rollout in a fresh env, accept only if the rollout
  replicates dominance, otherwise discard the graft and mark the (prefix,
  observation) pair as non-fungible.
side_information: transition geometry (observation-equivalence as the graft-
  point rule) and vector diagnostics (Pareto dominance read from
  info["vector"] on vector envs).
nearest_prior_or_disqualifier: BRIC (#2). Also adjacent: ETB/HPC (#13).
falsifier: on DoorKey-8x8 with a 60 s budget, fewer than 5% of attempted
  grafts replicate their predicted dominance under verification rollout. If
  the observation-equivalence rule is too lossy to make grafts on-distribution,
  the primitive has no signal to operate on and the candidate dies.
```

## Mechanism

The agent maintains a single trie T over executed action-sequences a_{1:t}.
At each trie node n we store: (i) a hash h(n) of the observation seen at
that prefix, (ii) the multiset O(n) of outcome vectors `o ∈ ℝ^k` accumulated
by every recorded suffix continuing from n through to episode end (k = 1 for
scalar envs; k = vector dim otherwise), and (iii) a non-fungible flag.
Episodes are generated by sampling action-sequences from the trie itself: at
node n we sample the next action with probability proportional to a softmax
over child suffixes' best outcome under the env's dominance order, with a
small uniform mixture for unexplored actions. After every episode we run the
**suffix-inheritance** operator: for each pair of nodes (n, n') with
h(n) = h(n') and neither marked non-fungible, let s* be the suffix from n'
whose outcome Pareto-dominates n's best suffix outcome by the largest margin
(if any such s* exists; on scalar envs, dominance is `>` with shorter-suffix
tiebreak). We **graft** s* under n by constructing the candidate full
sequence (path-to-n) ⧺ s*, then **verify** it by running it in a fresh
env instance (one rollout). If the verified outcome Pareto-dominates the
current best outcome at n, the graft is committed (the trie absorbs the new
sequence and updates O(n)); otherwise the (h(n), s*) pair is recorded as
non-fungible so the same graft is not retried. The policy returned at
deployment is the trie's best-suffix walk from the root.

## Monotonic improvement claim

Under deterministic env dynamics that are Markov in the observation hash,
the operator is exact: every committed graft strictly Pareto-improves the
best outcome at the receiving node, so the trie's best-from-root outcome is
monotone non-decreasing in the dominance order. Under approximate Markov-
in-observation (the realistic case — `ImgObsWrapper` is partial), the
verification rollout is the corrective: a graft that fails to replicate
dominance is rejected and never re-attempted, so the *committed* graft set
still produces monotone non-decreasing best outcome, at the cost of some
wasted verification rollouts. The non-fungible flag prevents repeated
verification of the same losing graft.

## Why it is not BRIC (#2)

BRIC chose segment-swap points from a hand-specified *edit grammar* and
required many counterfactual rollouts per accepted bracket because its
swap points were not on-distribution. SIT chooses graft points from
**observation-equivalence discovered in the trie**, so the suffix is
on-distribution by construction up to partial-observability slippage,
and a single verification rollout per graft is enough. BRIC also used
scalar terminal reward; SIT uses Pareto dominance on vector outcomes for
vector envs (DST-concave, Resource-Gathering) and a structural shorter-
suffix tiebreak on scalar envs, so it never collapses a vector reward to
`wᵀr`. Distinction from ETB/HPC (#13): ETB is goal-conditioned hindsight
("first action on shortest suffix to event g"); SIT has no goals and no
event lens — the suffix's outcome is its natural episode outcome, and
grafting is unconditional on which states a suffix happens to visit.
