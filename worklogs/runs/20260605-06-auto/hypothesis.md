# 20260605-06-auto — PICAV: Path-Integrated Channel-Asymmetry Voting

## Research Gate

primitive: per-(obs, action) empirical distribution of pairwise channel-asymmetry contributions `δ_{jk,t} = v_t[j]·Δn_t[k] − v_t[k]·Δn_t[j]` accumulated across all observed trajectories
improvement_operator: nudge policy logits toward actions whose pair-contribution vector lies on the upper-orthant frontier of the local set of action contribution-vectors at the same observation-hash; no scalar reward weight, no Bellman, no critic
side_information: vector diagnostics (per-step `info["vector"]`) and transition geometry (observation-hash for local comparison)
nearest_prior_or_disqualifier: 15 (FED — empirical Pareto front of vector-outcome cumulants indexed by obs-hash); also adjacent to GVFs / successor-features
falsifier: on Deep Sea Treasure and Resource Gathering, where channels are temporally asymmetric by construction (treasure value vs. step cost; gold/gem vs. enemy hits over time), the per-step contribution-frontier indicator must fire on >= 20% of decisions after a short warm-up, and panel score must exceed `panel_n_beat_strong` on at least one vector env. Predicted failure if it does not — and explicit predicted failure on single-channel envs (DoorKey, KeyCorridor) where the pair `(j,k)` is undefined.

## Mechanism

For each trajectory of length T with per-step vector feedback `v_1, ..., v_T ∈ ℝ^k`, define the running future-cumulant `n_t[k] = Σ_{s>t} v_s[k]` (channel k's remaining-trajectory mass after step t). The per-step **pair-contribution** to channel-pair `(j, k)` is `δ_{jk,t} = v_t[j]·(n_{t-1}[k] − n_t[k]) − v_t[k]·(n_{t-1}[j] − n_t[j])`. The trajectory integral `A_{jk} = Σ_t δ_{jk,t}` is antisymmetric in (j,k) and measures whether channel j tended to fire **before** channel k along the trajectory — a coordinate-free temporal-ordering moment computed without reward, without a learned model, and without segment boundaries. The primitive is the per-(obs-hash, action) **empirical distribution of the contribution vector** `δ_·,t ∈ ℝ^{k(k-1)/2}` across all visits. The improvement operator, evaluated at decision time, computes the upper-orthant Pareto frontier of mean contribution vectors over the available actions at the current observation-hash and nudges policy logits toward frontier actions in proportion to the count of off-frontier actions they coordinate-wise dominate. Monotonicity claim: under fixed transition statistics and policy soft-update, the expected absolute pairwise channel-asymmetry `E[Σ_{j<k} |A_{jk}|]` of the policy's trajectory distribution is non-decreasing — a measurable proxy for the long-horizon causal coupling the policy induces between feedback channels.

## Required candidate shape

1. **Experience object:** trajectories `(s_t, a_t, v_t, h(s_t))_{t=1..T}` from ordinary policy rollouts; `v_t = info["vector"]` is the per-step k-channel feedback vector and `h(s_t)` is an observation-hash bucket. No counterfactual rollouts, no verifier calls, no demonstrations.

2. **Core primitive:** For each (obs-hash bucket `b`, action `a`), the running mean vector `μ(b, a) ∈ ℝ^{k(k-1)/2}` of pair-contribution entries `δ_{jk,t}` accumulated over every trajectory step where `h(s_t) = b` and `a_t = a`. The vector dimension scales as `k(k-1)/2`; each entry encodes whether action `a` from bucket `b` tended to fire channel `j` before channel `k` (positive) or after (negative). This is **not** a scalar value, **not** a return, **not** a count, **not** a Pareto front of outcomes — it is a signed antisymmetric moment over channel pairs.

3. **Improvement operator:** At update time, for each obs-hash bucket `b` with sufficient samples across multiple actions, compute the upper-orthant Pareto frontier `F(b) = {a : μ(b,a) is not coordinate-wise ≤ μ(b,a') for any a' ≠ a}`. For each non-frontier action `a` at bucket `b`, find the frontier action `a* ∈ F(b)` that coordinate-wise dominates it on the most pair-coordinates; add to the policy loss a logit-shift `α · D(a*, a) · (log π(a*|s) − log π(a|s))` summed across decisions in bucket `b`, where `D(a*, a)` is the count of dominated coordinates. No reward weight enters this loss. No bootstrap target. No critic.

4. **Execution rule:** Sample `a ~ π(·|s)` with a small entropy bonus during training. The policy is the deployed object; no plan, no tree search, no verifier at decision time.

5. **Vector feedback rule:** Channels are never collapsed. The pair-contribution `δ_{jk,t}` is intrinsically pairwise and antisymmetric; collapsing the vector to a scalar `r = wᵀv` zeroes the antisymmetric part by construction (every pair becomes proportional to a single number). The frontier in step 3 is an **upper-orthant partial order** on `ℝ^{k(k-1)/2}` — no scalarization. On single-channel envs, the pair set is empty and the operator deliberately no-ops (declared failure mode).

6. **Rollout-cost discipline:** One environment step per training step. Zero counterfactual rollouts. Zero verifier calls. No replay branches. The contribution vector `δ_·,t` is computed in O(k^2) per step from `v_t` and the running future-cumulant `n_t`, both of which are accumulated in a single backward pass over each completed trajectory.

7. **Nearest-neighbor novelty audit:** Closest prior is **#15 FED (Frontier-Expanding Dispersion)** — also indexes by obs-hash and uses a partial-order indicator on vector signals. Distinction: FED's primitive is the empirical Pareto front of **outcome cumulant multisets** per (bucket, action), and FED accepts an update iff a new outcome **extends** the front. PICAV's primitive is the **mean signed antisymmetric pair-contribution vector** per (bucket, action), and the operator compares **actions to each other within a bucket** by upper-orthant dominance on a fundamentally different object: the antisymmetric temporal-ordering moments are zero whenever channels co-fire simultaneously and grow only when channels acquire temporal sequence — they encode *causal pre-order between channels*, not attainment of channel-states. FED's bootstrap failure mode (empty bucket cells until rare reward arrives) does not apply because every step contributes nonzero pair-contributions whenever any two channels have nonzero variance on the trajectory — buckets fill at every step on vector envs. Adjacent to **GVFs / successor features**: GVFs predict per-state cumulants conditional on policy; PICAV's object is per-(state, action) signed pair moments, not a prediction; the improvement operator does not use a Bellman backup on the cumulants and does not extract behavior by argmax over a learned scalar. Adjacent to **#11 TOP (Temporal Outcome Profiles)**: TOP's profile is per-channel first-hit time conditional on state; PICAV's object is the antisymmetric pairwise integral, not first-hit, and the operator compares actions within a bucket by orthant dominance over the pair vector rather than by per-channel first-hit deltas.

8. **Predicted failure modes:**
   - **Single-channel envs (DoorKey, KeyCorridor):** k=1 makes the pair set empty; primitive is undefined and the operator no-ops. PICAV will score `beat_strong = 0` on those envs. This is declared, not hidden.
   - **Channel-symmetric envs:** if all channels co-fire in lockstep at every step (perfect correlation), every `δ_{jk,t}` is zero and the operator never fires.
   - **Obs-hash collision:** if `h(s_t)` collapses too many distinct underlying states into one bucket, mean contribution vectors mix incompatible distributions and the orthant frontier becomes noise. Diagnostic: measure mean per-bucket sample variance of `δ_·,t` and compare to cross-bucket variance.
   - **Stochastic transitions:** under high stochasticity, `μ(b,a)` becomes a noisy estimate; the orthant-dominance frontier shrinks (fewer strict dominations) and the loss weight `D(a*, a)` decays toward zero. Mitigation is a min-sample threshold per (b, a).
   - **k=2 with single-sign channels:** if both channels are non-negative everywhere (e.g., reward and step-count), the pair integral has sign determined by overall ordering and the orthant degenerates to a scalar comparison — risk of being a return-to-go rebadge on k=2 envs. Audit by checking whether `δ_{12,t}` has both signs across trajectories.

9. **Side-information channel:** **vector diagnostics** (per-step `info["vector"]` is the only signal driving the primitive — no reward, no count, no novelty, no learned dynamics) and **transition geometry** (observation-hash for local within-bucket action comparisons; this is a passive index, not a count-based bonus).

10. **Monotonic improvement claim:** Under a fixed transition kernel and a sufficiently small soft-update step, the policy's expected total absolute pairwise channel-asymmetry `J(π) = E_π[Σ_{j<k} |A_{jk}|]` is non-decreasing across updates. This holds because each update is a convex combination toward action distributions whose mean pair-contribution vector lies on the upper-orthant frontier of the action set at each visited bucket; coordinate-wise frontier movement of the per-action mean is sufficient (under standard regularity and bucket coverage) to make the expected absolute pair-integral non-decreasing in `π`. The claim is a measurable proxy: PICAV does **not** monotonically improve a return; it monotonically improves the policy's tendency to **temporally separate** vector-feedback channels — the long-horizon causal-structure quantity that distinguishes Pareto-good behavior from random-walk behavior on vector envs.

## Why it is not FED (#15) or a GVF/successor-feature rebadge

FED's primitive is the empirical Pareto front of cumulant **outcomes** per (bucket, action), with the improvement indicator being "this action's outcome multiset extends the bucket's attainment set." That object is bounded above by reachable channel-attainments and stays empty until reward-bearing trajectories enter the bucket, which is exactly why FED scored 0.0. PICAV's primitive is the **signed antisymmetric pair-contribution mean** — defined and informative on every step where any two channels have nonzero increment, regardless of whether reward-bearing trajectories exist. The operator compares actions inside a bucket by upper-orthant dominance over `k(k-1)/2`-dimensional pair vectors, not by extension of an attainment set; under variable renaming this is not Bellman, not return-to-go, not a Pareto front of outcomes, and not a GVF (no per-state cumulant prediction with bootstrap).

## Why it scales beyond the substrate

At horizon 20k actions with a 6-channel feedback (success, cost, safety, latency, energy, validity), there are 15 channel pairs; the antisymmetric pair-integral `A_{jk}` is the natural sufficient statistic for "channel j tends to fire before channel k along this episode." This is exactly the long-horizon causal-coupling structure that scalar-reward optimization destroys: a policy that learns to ensure validity-before-cost-incurred and safety-before-latency-degradation has temporally separated channels that an LLM-tool-use trajectory would express as "verify before commit, gather context before act." Per-(observation-bucket, action) contribution vectors fill at every step (not only at rare reward), so the primitive does not stall on long horizons. For action spaces of paragraph-generation, the obs-hash bucket can be replaced by a learned embedding-quantizer over the policy's hidden state, and the action set per bucket can be the recently-sampled action set (not the full action vocabulary) — orthant dominance is computed only among observed alternatives, never enumerated over the full action space.
