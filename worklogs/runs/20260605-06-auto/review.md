## Reasoning

PICAV's central primitive — the per-(obs-hash, action) empirical mean of signed antisymmetric pair-contribution vectors `δ_{jk,t} = v_t[j]·Δn_t[k] − v_t[k]·Δn_t[j]` — is structurally distinct from every disqualifier family and every prior attempt. Under variable renaming it does not reduce to a Bellman backup (no bootstrap, no scalar target), a policy-gradient weight (no reward-weighted log-prob), a Pareto front of outcome cumulants (FED #15 indexes by attainment sets of channel-state outcomes and stays empty until reward-bearing trajectories enter a bucket; PICAV's object is a signed temporal-ordering moment that is nonzero on any step where two vector channels have nonzero increments with different temporal profiles), GVFs/successor features (no per-state cumulant prediction with Bellman recursion), or scalarization (the improvement operator acts on an upper-orthant partial order over `k(k-1)/2`-dimensional space, not `wᵀr`). The structural distinction from FED is the most important to verify: FED's primitive is "does this action's outcome multiset extend the bucket's attainment set," a set-extension indicator over cumulant end-states; PICAV's primitive is "what is the signed cross-channel temporal-ordering moment for this (bucket, action) pair," a per-step antisymmetric integral that encodes whether channel j tended to fire before channel k, not whether any particular channel attainment was reached. The hypothesis correctly identifies the bootstrap failure of FED and explains why PICAV avoids it: pair-contribution entries are defined and informative on every step with nonzero vector feedback, regardless of whether reward-bearing trajectories exist.

All required-candidate-shape slots are filled with substantive answers: the primitive has a precise algebraic definition, the improvement operator has a concrete update rule (logit shift `α·D(a*,a)·(log π(a*|s) − log π(a|s))` where `D` counts coordinate-wise dominated pairs), side-information channels are explicitly declared (per-step `info["vector"]` and observation-hash transition geometry), a monotonic improvement claim is stated for `J(π) = E_π[Σ_{j<k} |A_{jk}|]`, and multiple concrete predicted failure modes are enumerated — including the explicit self-declaration that single-channel envs (DoorKey, KeyCorridor) will score 0 because the pair set is empty.

## Risks the Engineer should be aware of

- The claim that buckets fill on every step (bypassing FED's bootstrap wall) holds only if the vector channels carry nonzero increments on non-terminal steps. On Deep Sea Treasure, the step-cost channel fires every step but the treasure channel is terminal-only; `Δn_t[treasure]` will be zero on every non-terminal step, making `δ_{j,treasure,t} = 0` for all j throughout the episode except the final step. The Engineer should verify that the primitive still fires meaningfully on DST and is not reduced to a single-step indicator — measure mean per-step contribution-vector magnitude early in training to confirm.
- The k=2 degenerate case flagged in failure mode 8 (both channels non-negative everywhere, pair integral sign determined by overall ordering) is real on Resource Gathering (gold and gem are non-negative; enemy hits are non-positive). The Engineer should instrument the `δ_{12,t}` sign distribution to confirm both signs appear regularly, ruling out silent collapse to a return-to-go comparison.
- The obs-hash bucket collision risk is real at longer horizons with high-dimensional observations (Craftax-Symbolic). The Engineer should log per-bucket sample variance vs. cross-bucket variance as a diagnostic to catch when the frontier degenerates to noise.
