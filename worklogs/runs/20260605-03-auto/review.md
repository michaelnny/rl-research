## Reasoning

RSD's central primitive — a sparse multigraph whose edge multiset `E(u,v)` stores accumulated vector signals for all observed action segments sharing both a start observation-hash `u` and an end observation-hash `v` — does not reduce to any disqualifier family under variable renaming. The improvement operator clones behavior toward a Pareto-dominant edge, weighted by its dominance count (an ordinal, not a reward magnitude), with no scalar projection at any point. This is not top-k trajectory cloning (Pareto-dominance on a k-dim vector is structurally different from scalar ranking), not REINFORCE/PPO (the gradient weight is a count of dominated peers, not a return or advantage), not HER (no virtual goal relabeling), and not scalarization (coordinate-wise dominance never produces `wᵀr`). The structural distinction from SIT (alive-weak, nearest prior) is substantive and correctly identified: SIT compares divergent suffixes from a shared prefix and requires verification rollouts because the downstream future differs between suffix pairs; RSD compares convergent segments sharing *both* endpoints, so the downstream future distribution is identically shared, making dominance statistically valid without verification. All required candidate-shape slots carry substantive answers: the primitive is one object (the edge multiset), the improvement operator is one rule (Pareto-dominant behavior cloning weighted by dominance count), the side-information channel is explicitly named (transition geometry + vector diagnostics), the monotonic improvement claim is stated and conditioned on well-defined assumptions, predicted failure modes are concrete and testable, and the falsifier is measurable within the panel time budget.

The scalability argument for long-horizon settings is well-motivated: the supply of natural counterfactual pairs grows roughly as `O(T²/|O|)`, meaning longer horizons produce more parallel edges rather than fewer — the opposite of value-bootstrap degradation. Vector feedback is handled natively by coordinate-wise Pareto dominance with no collapse to a fixed or learned scalar weight at any step. These properties are exactly what the mission requires, and the candidate articulates a genuine structural reason why the primitive would be stronger at horizon 20k than at horizon 200.

## Risks the Engineer should be aware of

- The "no-reconvergence" failure mode on Craftax-Symbolic is real: if the symbolic state space is high-entropy enough that `|E(u,v)| = 1` everywhere within the time budget, the operator is a no-op and the candidate produces no learning signal. The Engineer should instrument and log the maximum observed edge multiplicity per run so this is diagnosable immediately.
- The observation-hash function `h(o)` is doing serious structural work. On MiniGrid pixel/symbolic observations a deterministic hash over the full observation tensor should be sufficient; on any env with partial observability or stochastic rendering it may produce collision noise that corrupts dominance comparisons. The hash collision diagnostic (post-segment trajectory divergence between paired edges) named in the hypothesis should be logged as a first-class metric.
- The behavior-cloning update is a supervised log-prob step on the dominant segment's action sequence. Under a recurrent or transformer policy with context dependence, the "context up to `o_i`" input must be consistently truncated or the gradient will backpropagate into context representations from a different trajectory — verify the implementation treats segment boundaries correctly.
