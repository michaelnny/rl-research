## Reasoning

JFP's structural distinction is that its central primitive is the per-(state, action, channel) **argmax-timestep** of the gradient norm of a learned forward model's cumulant head along an H-step counterfactual rollout — a timing quantity, not a magnitude, value, or prediction-error quantity. The improvement operator is Pareto-non-dominance over this timing vector `J(s,a) ∈ R^k`, which is genuinely vector-native: no scalarization, no critic, no Bellman backup, no scalar-weighted log-prob. The forward model's role is to provide gradient structure (when does a one-shot action intervention cause each channel's Jacobian to peak?), not to plan or supply expected return. This is structurally orthogonal to every disqualifier family: it is not model-based planning (no trajectory selection by expected return), not RND/curiosity (prediction error is irrelevant — the Jacobian structure of a well-fit model is the primitive), not GVFs/successor features (the cumulant head defines the gradient target only; the policy never sees cumulant predictions), not actor-critic (no critic supplies a scalar weight). The nearest live candidate CWAI uses 1-step Jacobian column-norm **magnitude**; JFP uses H-step argmax-timestep of gradient norm — amplitude vs. phase, 1-step vs. unrolled. These are structurally distinct and respond differently to noise, as the hypothesis explains. All required candidate-shape slots are substantively filled: experience object, core primitive with precise math, improvement operator with explicit update rule `Δ_a = α(n_a − m_a)`, execution rule, vector feedback rule, rollout-cost discipline, nearest-neighbor novelty audit, four concrete predicted failure modes, side-information channel declaration (learned dynamics + vector diagnostics), and a monotonic improvement claim grounded in Pareto-dominance count rather than scalar return.

The critical bootstrap-wall concern that killed the FED/CEC/CWTP/PCR family does not apply here: the forward model is supervised on every observed transition (no reward correlation required), and the cumulant head is supervised on per-step `v_t` signals (dense channels like step-penalty fire every step). The falsifier for the terminal-only channel failure (failure mode a: cumulant head underfit on treasure/gold channels causing argmax over noise) is honestly stated and is qualitatively different from the FED-family bootstrap wall — it is a learning-speed problem, not a sample-mass/hash-collision problem. The hypothesis also correctly identifies that the Pareto-non-dominance step over the full timing vector `R^k` does not collapse to scalar optimization as long as k ≥ 2 channels remain discriminative, which is a meaningful distinction from TCP (#23) whose channel residual collapsed to a singleton.

## Risks the Engineer should be aware of

- The cumulant head for terminal-only channels (treasure in DST, gold/gem in RG) will be near-zero gradient for most of training; the stated failure mode (a) — `t_m^*` determined by argmax over noise for those channels — is the most likely substrate-specific collapse. The Engineer should monitor per-channel Jacobian norms during training and consider whether H needs to be tuned per environment or whether the terminal channels can be given higher supervised-loss weight.
- The H-step unrolled backward pass through the forward model computes `|A| * H` gradient norms per decision step. On the substrate with `|A| ≤ 7` and `H = 8` this is 56 backward passes; if the forward model has a non-trivial latent size, GPU memory pressure under batched gradient computation may require gradient checkpointing or reducing H to stay within the 120 s budget.
- The side-information channel "learned dynamics" requires the forward model to be non-trivially expressive relative to the environment's transition structure. On Resource Gathering with slip noise, the stochastic mean predictor pathology (failure mode c) may cause `t_m^*` to be an inductive-bias artifact rather than a genuine phase signal — the Engineer should add a diagnostic that checks variance of `J(s, ·)` across actions and flags near-constant rows early.
