## Reasoning

ATP's central primitive — an action-conditional k-vector of **level-set crossing horizons** `h*[o,a,m]` from a learned forward model — is structurally distinct from every disqualifier family. GVFs / successor features (the declared nearest prior) predict expected discounted sums `Σ_h γ^h · v_m(s_{t+h})`, a continuous magnitude over which linear scalarization recovers TD-style value. ATP predicts the *first step* at which a predicted firing probability crosses a threshold, which is a level-set crossing time: non-linear in the probability sequence, magnitude-destroying (only the event boundary matters), and not recoverable from any linear combination of successor features. The improvement operator — a Pareto-non-dominance logit nudge over the k-vector of integer horizons — cannot be obtained by renaming any of the disqualifier families because (1) there is no scalar collapse at any stage, (2) the update weight is a Pareto-margin count not an advantage or return weight, and (3) the learned objects (`f_θ` and `{p_m}`) are supervised on directly observed transitions, not on reward signals. The hypothesis distinguishes itself from prior sprint-4 attempts (FED/CEC/TPP/CWTP/TRAC families) by explicitly defeating the hash-collision bootstrap wall: the forward model fires reward-independently from episode step 1, so the primitive produces non-zero action discrimination before any reward is observed. All required-candidate-shape slots are filled with substantive answers: primitive (level-set crossing horizon), improvement operator (Pareto-margin logit nudge), experience object (standard on-policy transitions + `info["vector"]`), execution rule, vector-feedback rule, rollout-cost discipline, side-information channel ({learned dynamics, vector diagnostics}), monotonic improvement claim (frontier-set probability mass increases in expectation when forward model is locally calibrated), and predicted failure modes.

The hypothesis also addresses the two most dangerous prior failure modes: the bootstrap wall (the forward model is reward-free and fires from step 1) and within-trajectory signal-geometry collapse on terminal-only channels (the threshold τ_m is self-tuned to the empirical 75th-percentile of `p_m` over the replay buffer, so it adapts to the noise floor rather than depending on reward-correlated signal). The structural-distinction from CWAI (#06 alive-promising) and JFP (#12 alive-weak) is clearly articulated: CWAI uses Jacobian column-norms (magnitudes that shrink under stochastic transitions); JFP uses argmax of Jacobian norm over a horizon (still magnitude-based); ATP uses threshold-crossing times (magnitudes are projected away by the level-set operator). The composition law (forward-model rollout → threshold crossing → Pareto-non-dominance margin → logit nudge) is a single well-defined pipeline with no unnamed components and no reward in the loop.

## Risks the Engineer should be aware of

- The improvement operator is well-defined only when `|A|` is small enough for per-action forward-model rollouts to be practical at decision time. On environments with large discrete action sets (or continuous actions), the `|A|·H_max` rollout cost becomes a bottleneck — verify that `|A|` on the 5-env panel is within the claimed ~4 range.
- The bootstrap-wall argument depends on the forward model `f_θ` being useful early in training before reward is observed. If `f_θ` underfits on the step-penalty-only trajectories that dominate early experience (as noted in failure mode (d)), the persistence-horizon vectors may be action-uniform from the model's prediction error rather than from stochastic transitions — a different failure path than predicted that the AUC diagnostic should be verified to catch.
- The monotonic improvement claim holds only when "forward model is locally calibrated" — this is a non-trivial assumption on partial-observable environments like KeyCorridor. The hypothesis acknowledges this implicitly in failure mode (d) but does not state whether the AUC diagnostic can distinguish underfitting from genuine action-uniformity.
