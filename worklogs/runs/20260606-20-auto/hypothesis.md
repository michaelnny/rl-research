# 20260606-20-auto — ATP (Action-Tangent Persistence)

## Research Gate

primitive: per-(observation, action, channel) **persistence horizon** `h*[o,a,m] ∈ {0,1,...,H}` — the smallest integer step h at which a learned forward model predicts the per-channel firing probability under the policy of "execute a now, then continue under current π" *crosses* a self-tuned threshold τ_m. A horizon, not a magnitude, not a discounted sum.
improvement_operator: at each decision step, compute the action-conditional k-vector of persistence horizons `h*[o, a, :]`; nudge policy logits by `α·(n_a − m_a)` where `n_a` counts actions whose horizon vector strictly Pareto-dominates `h*[o,a,:]` (coordinate-wise SHORTER horizons across channels) and `m_a` counts those it dominates. No scalar collapse, no critic, no Bellman backup.
side_information: [learned dynamics, vector diagnostics]
nearest_prior_or_disqualifier: GVFs / successor features (disqualifier family); CWAI (alive-promising); JFP (alive-weak)
falsifier: if the per-channel forward-model rollouts produce horizon vectors that are constant across actions on Resource Gathering for >50% of decision steps within the 120 s budget (Pareto vote silently zero — same failure-mode as CWAI/JFP under stochastic transitions), the primitive is dead. Falsified by a diagnostic check: fraction of decision steps with non-zero `n_a − m_a` for any action.

## Mechanism

ATP trains a small forward-dynamics model `f_θ: (o, a) → ô` jointly with k auxiliary per-channel firing-probability heads `p_m: o → [0, 1]` (binary supervised: did channel m fire on this step). At each decision step it does the following without any reward signal: for each candidate action a, it unrolls the forward model H_max steps deep using the *intervention-then-policy* schedule (force a at step 0, then sample π for steps 1..H_max), records the predicted firing-probability sequence `(p_m(ô_1), p_m(ô_2), ..., p_m(ô_{H_max}))` for each channel m, and reads off the **persistence horizon** `h*[o,a,m] = min{h : p_m(ô_h) ≥ τ_m}` (or H_max if never crossed). The threshold τ_m is the empirical 75th-percentile of `p_m` over the replay buffer (self-tuned, not hand-set), so τ_m absorbs scale and underfitting. The per-action k-vector `h*[o,a,:]` is the primitive. The improvement operator computes the Pareto-non-dominance margin (`n_a − m_a`, coordinate-wise comparison of horizons where shorter dominates longer per channel) and applies a logit nudge of magnitude `α·(n_a − m_a)`. There is no scalar return, no advantage, no critic-supplied weight, no return-to-go target, no value bootstrap — the only learned objects are `f_θ` and `{p_m}`, both supervised on directly observed quantities.

## Required candidate shape

1. **Experience object:** ordinary on-policy trajectories with per-step `info["vector"]` channels. Each transition `(o_t, a_t, o_{t+1}, v_{t+1})` is stored in a small replay buffer used to fit `f_θ` (one-step next-observation regression) and `p_m` (one-step binary firing-probability classification per channel).
2. **Core primitive:** the action-conditional persistence-horizon k-vector `h*[o, a, :] ∈ {0,1,...,H_max}^k`, where `h*[o,a,m]` is the *first* predicted forward-rollout step at which channel m's predicted firing probability crosses the channel's empirical 75th-percentile threshold. Magnitude-invariant (only the level-set crossing matters), per-channel (no scalar collapse), forward-model-grounded (fires from step 1 of any episode, before any reward is observed).
3. **Improvement operator:** `θ ← θ + lr · α · ∇_θ log π(a^* | o_t)` where `a^*` is the action with the largest Pareto-non-dominance margin `n_a − m_a` among the k-vector horizon comparisons. Crucially, the *comparison* is Pareto-non-dominance with the convention that smaller `h*` (faster channel onset) dominates: action a Pareto-dominates a' iff `h*[o,a,m] ≤ h*[o,a',m]` for all m and strict for some m. This is not Bellman, not policy-gradient with scalar reward, not advantage-weighted, not elite-cloning, not return-to-go.
4. **Execution rule:** sample `a ~ softmax(logits + α·δ(o))` where `δ(o)[a] = n_a − m_a` is the Pareto-non-dominance margin computed at decision time via H_max forward-model rollouts (one per action). No greedy argmax (preserves exploration); the nudge is a soft preference, not a constraint.
5. **Vector feedback rule:** the k channels are NEVER weighted, summed, or projected to a scalar at any point. The Pareto-non-dominance comparison operates directly on the k-dimensional integer-valued horizon vector. A channel can be ignored by the operator only if τ_m is set to a value that `p_m` never crosses (handled by a lower-bound clamp on τ_m). On envs with k=1, ATP degenerates gracefully to a single-channel persistence-horizon ranker — flagged in diagnostics as "scalar regime, distinction lost."
6. **Rollout-cost discipline:** one env step per decision (standard). At each decision step: |A| forward-model rollouts of length H_max ≤ 8 each — counterfactual rollouts on the *learned* model only, no env queries. So per-update cost is exactly one env step plus `|A|·H_max` cheap network forward passes. No paired replay, no simulator branches, no verifier calls. Forward-model and `{p_m}` heads are updated by SGD on the replay buffer at the standard rate. Total wall-clock cost per env step: dominated by `|A|·H_max ≈ 30` model forward passes — negligible vs. one env step on substrate.
7. **Nearest-neighbor novelty audit:** Closest priors are CWAI (#06 alive-promising), JFP (#12 alive-weak), and the GVF/successor-features disqualifier family. CWAI uses Jacobian *column-norms* (gradient magnitudes that shrink under stochastic transitions); ATP uses *level-set crossing horizons* on the predicted probability sequence — magnitudes are projected away by the threshold operator, so stochastic noise re-weights `p_m` rather than shrinking the primitive toward zero. JFP uses argmax of Jacobian norm over a horizon (still magnitude-based; underfits when channels are sparse). GVFs/SF predict expected discounted *sums* of channel firings — a continuous magnitude in linear-scalarization-ready form; ATP predicts an *integer horizon at which a level set is first crossed*, which is not a discounted sum and admits no scalarization that recovers SF.
8. **Predicted failure modes:** (a) on Resource Gathering's stochastic transitions, the forward model `f_θ` may produce action-uniform predictions early in training (same failure as JFP), causing all `h*[o, a, m] = H_max` and silencing the operator — we expect this to be partially absorbed by the threshold (since τ_m is data-driven the threshold rises with the noise floor) but it may not be fully rescued. (b) on environments where one channel dominates the others' onset times by a large margin (e.g. step-penalty fires every step → `h* = 0` on that channel for every action), the Pareto comparison may collapse to a single discriminating channel — same as CRP/CHX/LRA collapse — diagnostic check: per-channel variance of `h*` across actions. (c) if H_max is too small (channel never crosses τ_m within the rollout), the primitive saturates at H_max for all actions on that channel — needs adaptive H_max. (d) if `f_θ` overfits to step-penalty dynamics and never learns to predict treasure/gold/gem channels, the operator is silent on the rare channels that matter — same bootstrap wall as JFP. We will detect this via per-channel `p_m` AUC on held-out replay slice.
9. **Side-information channel:** {learned dynamics, vector diagnostics}. The learned dynamics model and per-channel firing classifiers are trained on directly observed signals (next-observation regression and binary-channel labels from `info["vector"]`). No hand-engineered event lens, no demonstrations, no language hints. The forward model is a generic component; what carries the explanatory load is the level-set-crossing horizon primitive operating on its predicted trajectories.
10. **Monotonic improvement claim:** under the assumptions that (i) the forward model is locally calibrated (predicted firing probabilities match empirical post-action firing rates within ε), and (ii) the threshold τ_m is set to the env-frequency quantile such that `p_m ≥ τ_m` is action-discriminating (i.e. some actions lead faster than others to channel m firing), the Pareto-non-dominance nudge monotonically increases the policy's probability of selecting actions whose true (env-conditional) per-channel onset times are shortest on the Pareto frontier. The monotonic claim is over the *frontier-set probability mass* `Σ_{a ∈ Pareto-front(o)} π(a | o)`, which is a single scalar measurable proxy that the operator increases at each update step in expectation when (i)-(ii) hold.

## Why it is not GVFs / successor features (disqualifier family)

GVFs / SF predict expected discounted *sums* `Σ_h γ^h · v_m(s_{t+h})` — a continuous magnitude that can be linearly scalarized (`Σ_m w_m · ψ_m`) to recover any TD-style value function. ATP predicts an *integer horizon* at which the predicted firing probability *first crosses a threshold* — this is a level-set crossing time, not a sum or expectation, and there is no linear scalarization of horizons that recovers a value function (the level-set operator is non-linear and information-destroying about magnitudes). The improvement operator then uses Pareto-non-dominance over the k-vector of horizons, which is structurally a vector-comparison — not a scalarized weighted sum, not a critic supplying an actor weight. Under variable renaming, ATP cannot be reduced to GVF prediction + linear scalarization because the threshold-crossing-then-Pareto composition is non-invertible.

## Why it scales beyond the substrate

At horizon 20k, the bootstrap wall is exactly the issue ATP is designed to address: the persistence-horizon primitive fires from step 1 of any episode (forward-model-driven, no reward dependence), so a 20k-step episode that never sees reward still produces useful action discrimination on every step. For LLM-with-tools settings, "channels" become semantic indicators (validity, citation-correctness, latency, safety-flag, user-satisfaction-proxy), and the persistence horizon `h*` is the predicted number of LLM tokens / tool calls until each channel-indicator is expected to first flip — a quantity that a small probe head over an LLM's hidden state can predict directly. The Pareto-non-dominance comparison then operates over per-channel time-to-event vectors, which is the natural decision-theoretic object for "which action gets us to which terminal/intermediate event soonest, on the Pareto frontier of channels we care about." The composition law (level-set then Pareto vote) is the same at any horizon; only the rollout depth H_max scales (and is hard-capped by compute).
