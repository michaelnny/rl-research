## Reasoning

CSD's primitive — `TV(p(c'|c,a, channel_m fires in [t+1..t+K]), p(c'|c,a, channel_m does not fire in [t+1..t+K]))` — is structurally distinct from every disqualifier family: it is not a value backup, not a policy-gradient weight, not a scalar collapse, not count-based exploration (the signal is sensitive to *what next cluster* is entered, not how often), and not a GVF/successor-feature (it does not predict expected cumulants; it measures distributional separation in next-state identity conditional on future channel firing). The nearest prior is TRAC (#26), which also computes a divergence between successor-cluster histograms partitioned by channel firing and applies Pareto-non-dominance logit nudges. The structural distinction is genuine and precisely articulated: TRAC used plug-in JSD (undefined with one sample per side) over an immediate post-action window; CSD uses Laplace-smoothed TV (well-defined with one sample per side) over a K-step window (relaxing the firing-rate requirement). These are not parameter changes — they directly target TRAC's stated failure mechanism (cell-collision undersaturation causing the JSD primitive to stay silent). All required-candidate-shape slots are filled substantively: experience object, precise primitive formula, improvement operator with explicit `α(n_a − m_a)` update rule, vector feedback rule (per-channel independent, Pareto-only aggregation, never collapsed), rollout-cost discipline (zero counterfactuals), five named predicted failure modes with measurable falsifiers, named side-information channels (vector diagnostics + transition geometry, neither hand-engineered), and a monotonic improvement claim grounded in a Pinsker-type mutual-information lower bound.

The hypothesis is honest about the bootstrap sequence: the step-penalty channel provides non-degenerate TV values from episode 1 and seeds the Pareto vote; terminal-only reward channels remain degenerate until first success, with the mitigation being that step-penalty-driven channel-discrimination diversity acts as a structural prior toward "interesting" states. The step-penalty-domination falsifier is the most likely failure mode on the substrate and is correctly pre-registered. The remaining structural risk is whether TV-vs-JSD stability and a K-step window are sufficient to overcome the cell-revisitation frequency problem that killed TRAC within a 120 s budget on DoorKey-8x8 and KeyCorridor — TRAC's ruling explicitly identified `(cluster, action)` revisitation frequency (not channel-firing frequency) as the bottleneck, and CSD reduces the sample-size requirement per cell but does not change the revisitation regime. This is a legitimate empirical question, not a structural disqualification.

## Risks the Engineer should be aware of

- The cell-revisitation frequency issue that killed TRAC (#26) applies to the same `(c, a)` cells in CSD. The K-step window and TV-vs-JSD improvements reduce the sample-size requirement per cell but do not increase revisitation frequency under uniform exploration. On DoorKey-8x8 and KeyCorridor the (cluster, action) cells may still be undersaturated within 120 s; the pre-commit diagnostic (log mean bin-occupancy and Pareto-vote firing rate on those envs, not just RG) is essential for distinguishing "operator silent because cell-mass insufficient" from "operator firing but wrong."
- The step-penalty-domination falsifier threshold (5x mean-S ratio) is the key pre-commit gate on the vector envs; the Engineer should log this every N-transition refresh and halt early if the ratio is confirmed above threshold, to avoid burning the full budget on a collapsed operator.
- The cluster vocabulary size is a critical hyperparameter: too few clusters collapse TV to near-zero (failure mode 3), too many cause singleton support and TV uniformly 1 (failure mode 2). The hypothesis recommends ≤ 256 clusters; the Engineer should confirm cluster assignment entropy is in a useful range (e.g., effective vocabulary ≥ 16) before the Pareto vote is trusted.
