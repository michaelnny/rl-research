## Reasoning

DPC's structural distinction is: **improvement is driven by pairwise per-channel sign-votes at trajectory-pair first-divergence steps, with a Pareto-non-dominance gate — no scalar collapse, no Bellman backup, no return-to-go, no bucket-coverage precondition.** This is not a rename of any disqualifier family. The nearest disqualifier threat is scalar-weighted log-prob (MC-advantage): DPC fires only at first-divergence steps of pairs (not every step of every trajectory), uses per-channel sign-votes rather than a scalar return, and the Pareto-non-dominance gate's satisfaction provably depends on per-channel signs — substituting any `wᵀv` changes the gate's output, which is the decisive test. The nearest prior attempts are RSD (requires shared end-hash — exponentially rare; DPC drops this condition entirely), PICAV (uses within-trajectory cross-channel moments that collapse when any channel is terminal-only; DPC uses terminal suffix cumulants whose sign is nonzero whenever two trajectories differ on any terminal channel), and CHX (within-trajectory cumulant geometry that degrades to return-to-go on near-1D traces; DPC's cross-trajectory comparison does not have this pathology). All required-candidate-shape slots are filled substantively: primitive, improvement operator, execution rule, vector-feedback rule, rollout-cost discipline, nearest-neighbor novelty audit, predicted failure modes, side-information channel, and monotonic improvement claim.

The candidate is honest about its limits: it self-predicts that on collinear-channel envs (Deep Sea Treasure, where the only non-step-penalty channel is terminal treasure) the per-channel sign-vote degrades toward a pairwise-signed-MC-advantage rebadge. This is the correct structural honesty and does not disqualify the direction — it sharpens the falsifier. The falsifier is concrete (invocation rate below 5% on vector envs, or fires but scores below-random on DST/RG) and matches the failure mode declared in the hypothesis.

## Risks the Engineer should be aware of

- The "no bootstrap wall" claim rests on terminal suffix cumulants being nonzero whenever any terminal vector channel differs between two trajectories. On Deep Sea Treasure specifically, the treasure channel fires only at episode termination and the step-penalty is shared — two trajectories reaching the same treasure cell will have identical cumulants. The operator will fire, but its signal will be noise-dominated until trajectories to *different* treasure cells coexist in the buffer. Verify that the invocation rate diagnostic is logged early in training.
- The observation-hash collision risk at `s_div` (acknowledged in §8, failure mode 4) may be significant in pixel-based or high-dimensional obs envs. For the current substrate (MiniGrid, symbolic Craftax) collision risk is low, but the per-run diagnostic `mean #pairs / unique-(s_div, a, a')` should be reported.
- The improvement operator fires a cross-entropy SGD step on every unique `s_div` in the buffer. For long-horizon envs where every step produces a new `s_div`, the number of gradient steps per environment step grows quadratically with buffer size — the rollout-cost discipline claim (§6) holds for env interactions but not for compute. The Engineer should cap buffer size or subsample pairs to avoid a per-iteration compute explosion.
