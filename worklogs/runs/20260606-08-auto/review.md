## Reasoning

TCP's central primitive — the cross-trajectory, cross-channel lag-asymmetry tensor `Λ[j,k]` and its derived precedence DAG P — is structurally distinct from every disqualifier family and all prior attempts #1–22. The improvement operator (prefix-induced residual channel set R, Pareto-vote logit nudge restricted to R-coordinates) is not Q/V/advantage/return-to-go, not a scalar-weighted log-prob update, and not scalarization in disguise — channels are never summed, no `w^T r` materializes. The closest prior-attempt comparison (FED #15, CEC #18) fails to match because TCP's indexing object is a prefix-induced residual channel set derived from temporal-asymmetry statistics, not an observation-hash bucket requiring state-revisit accumulation — this difference is load-bearing and the hypothesis articulates it clearly and correctly. The reward-machines disqualifier is the sharpest structural risk; the hypothesis addresses it directly: the precedence DAG is empirically discovered from channel firing-time asymmetry, does not extend the state space into a product MDP, does not modify the reward signal, and the improvement operator executes a Pareto-vote count rather than value backup over any automaton-augmented state. That distinction holds under scrutiny. All required candidate-shape slots are substantively filled: a single primitive, a precise update rule, named side-information channels, articulated nearest-neighbor distinction, four concrete predicted failure modes with falsification criteria, and a monotonic improvement claim stated under explicit assumptions.

The spring-4 bootstrap-wall pattern (sprint 4 attempts #16–22 all failed because their primitive was silent until observation-hash or trajectory-pair collisions accumulated) does not apply in the same way here: `Λ` entries become non-trivial as soon as any two channels fire at distinct times in any trajectory, which is a much weaker threshold. The residual risk is the `μ[c,a,m]` cluster-indexed firing-mean statistic, which retains a soft form of the bootstrap problem (sparse cluster–action coverage), but this is explicitly acknowledged as a predicted failure mode — it is a performance risk, not a structural novelty disqualification.

## Risks the Engineer should be aware of

- The `μ[c,a,m]` statistic is indexed by observation cluster, which reintroduces a soft form of the bootstrap-wall problem on long-horizon sparse envs where (cluster, action) pairs accumulate slowly. Monitor whether the Pareto-vote count fires at all during the 120 s budget before concluding the operator is silent.
- On Resource Gathering where channel precedence is not a consistent DAG (gold-then-gem vs gem-then-gold both occur), `Λ` for those channels averages to near-zero and the DAG drops those edges — as the hypothesis notes, the family-killer is if this is the typical case across all informative channel pairs rather than just some. The Engineer should log DAG edge counts and residual set sizes during training.
- The hypothesis's monotonic improvement claim assumes the precedence DAG P is stationary across episodes. On non-stationary envs or in the early learning phase where the policy shifts the induced channel-firing distribution, P will itself be non-stationary and the monotonicity guarantee weakens to an empirical heuristic. This is acceptable for an initial panel run but should be noted as a source of instability.
