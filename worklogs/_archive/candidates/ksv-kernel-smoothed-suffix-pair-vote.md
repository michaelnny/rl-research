KSV (Kernel-Smoothed Suffix-Pair Vote) maintains a per-(action, channel) signed pair-vote tensor `SV[a, m] = Σ_{(i,j)} w_ij(o) · sign(δ_m^{ij})` where the kernel weight `w_ij(o) = κ(e(o), e(o_t^i)) · κ(e(o), e(o_t^j))` is a Gaussian kernel product on a learned observation embedding from a small auxiliary forward model (used as embedding only, not planner). The improvement operator is strict coordinate-wise Pareto-non-dominance margin over the k-vector `SV[a, :]` applied as a per-step logit nudge. Run 20260606-31-auto scored 471.0 vs strong 285.0 on Deep Sea Treasure (vector env, convincingly above-strong), but exactly tied random on Resource Gathering (1.331 = random = strong). The DST win confirms the kernel fires and produces directional signal without discrete hash collisions — the structural break from FED/CEC/TPP/CWTP/TRAC/CSD is genuine. The RG failure is consistent with predicted failure mode (c) (stochastic-transition shrinkage: stochastic transitions cause per-channel sign deltas to flip across pairs sharing similar embeddings, driving SV[a,:] toward zero and silencing the Pareto margin) and possibly failure mode (a) (collinearity collapse on reward channels before first rewarded trajectory). Evidence still needed: (a) diagnostic logging of per-channel sign histogram on RG to distinguish failure mode (a) from (c); (b) a sign-confidence gate that suppresses pairs whose per-channel sign flips too frequently (variance threshold) before they contribute to SV; (c) sparse-stage results on DoorKey/KeyCorridor to confirm the kernel fires on non-vector envs; (d) ablation on kernel bandwidth and M (sub-sampling count) to rule out bandwidth pathology vs. stochastic sign cancellation as the binding constraint on RG.
