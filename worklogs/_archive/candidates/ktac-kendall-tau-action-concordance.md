KTAC (Kendall-Tau Action-Concordance) maintains a per-(context-cluster, channel) pairwise dominance probability matrix `P_m[s, a, a']` estimated from trajectory pairs that share a context-cluster (online k-means on penultimate-layer features) and differ on terminal cumulant sign for channel m. The improvement operator is Kemeny consensus over per-channel action rankings — the permutation minimizing total weighted Kendall-tau distance to the k per-channel pairwise order matrices — with a centered rank-score logit nudge `alpha * score_sigma*(a)`. This is structurally distinct from DPC (which works in value-margin space per channel, using Pareto-non-dominance of scalar channel margins) and from scalarized multi-objective RL (which forms a scalar function of channel cumulants): KTAC's Kemeny operator works entirely in rank/order space and is provably not recoverable by any fixed `w^T r` scalarization.

Run 20260606-22-auto scored 0.0 on DST and 0.011 on RG, both below random (194.0 / 1.331). This is consistent with the context-cluster bootstrap wall: `P_m` cells require trajectory pairs sharing a cluster before any nudge fires; the default hyperparameters and 120s budget appear insufficient to seed the clusters with enough pairs. DPC (structurally similar family, but operating on exact divergence-state hashes) scored 289.0 vs strong 285.0 on DST — confirming the pairwise-comparison family can fire on DST, and suggesting the k-means cluster-population bottleneck is the binding constraint for KTAC.
