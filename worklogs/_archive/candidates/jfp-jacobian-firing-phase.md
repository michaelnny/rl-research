JFP (Jacobian Firing-Phase) trains a self-supervised forward model f_θ: (o_t, a_t) → o_{t+1} with a per-channel cumulant head ĉ_t ∈ R^k supervised on observed info["vector"] signals. At each decision step, for each action a, it unrolls the forward model H steps with a one-shot action intervention at t=0, and computes J(s,a) = (t_1^*, …, t_k^*) where t_m^* = argmax_{1≤τ≤H} ‖∂ĉ_τ[m]/∂e(a)‖_2 — the per-channel timestep at which the predicted cumulant's gradient w.r.t. the head action embedding peaks. The improvement operator nudges policy logits by α(n_a − m_a) where n_a counts actions that J(s,a) Pareto-dominates and m_a counts actions that dominate it coordinate-wise. The run scored 99.0/0.011 vs random 194.0/1.331 on DST/RG (beat_random=0, beat_strong=0), consistent with the hypothesis's own predicted failure mode (a): the cumulant head underfit the terminal-only treasure/gold/gem channels within the 120 s budget, causing t_m^* to be argmax over near-zero gradient noise rather than a genuine phase signal. This is a learning-speed failure, not a structural collapse — the step-penalty channel (dense, fires every step) should still provide a gradient scaffold. Evidence still needed: (1) retry with higher loss weight on terminal-only channels (e.g. 10x for treasure/gold/gem) or a higher cumulant-head learning rate; (2) diagnostic logging of per-channel Jacobian norms early in training to verify the cumulant head has non-trivial gradient before the phase signal is trusted; (3) at least one env beating random to confirm the primitive is learning anything. Structurally distinct from CWAI (timing vs. magnitude) and from all disqualifier families (no critic, no Bellman, no scalar reward, no prediction-error).
